## Knowledge Coverage

### 1. Water-Focused Foundations (water-foundations.md)

**Organizations Documented:**
- Water Foundation - Only US intermediary focused solely on water
  - $17M+ invested in California (284 grants)
  - Does not accept unsolicited proposals

- Pisces Foundation - One Water urban strategy
  - $6M+ invested in 21 cities since 2014
  - Open RFPs for urban water transformation

- Spring Point Partners - Delta program for utility leadership
  - Focus on workforce equity and community representation
  - Invitation-only partnerships

- S.D. Bechtel, Jr. Foundation (Legacy) - Concluded 2020
  - $130M+ in water programs 2009-2020
  - Community Foundation Water Initiative model

- Resources Legacy Fund - Campaign and fiscal sponsorship
  - $35M+ annual program expenses
  - Strategic partnerships over open applications

### 2. Environmental/Conservation Foundations (environmental-foundations.md)

**Organizations Documented:**
- David and Lucile Packard Foundation
  - California Communities Initiative (6 counties)
  - Ocean Initiative (marine conservation)
  - Distinctive indirect cost policy (25% for small orgs)

- William and Flora Hewlett Foundation
  - $13.9B assets, largest giving in environment
  - Western Conservation strategy (320M acre goal by 2035)
  - Klamath Basin collaborative funding

- Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation
  - No unsolicited proposals accepted
  - Open Lands, Clean Water, Healthy Oceans programs
  - $5-12M annual grantmaking

- National Fish and Wildlife Foundation
  - Five Star Wetland and Urban Waters Program
  - $20K-$50K grants, 1:1 match required
  - 70+ programs, Easygrants online system

- California Community Foundation
  - LA County focus, environmental justice emphasis
  - Safe, Clean Water grants partnership ($8.5M available)

### 3. Alternative Financing Mechanisms (alternative-financing.md)

**Mechanisms Documented:**
- Environmental Impact Bonds (EIBs)
  - DC Water model case study ($25M, Goldman Sachs/Calvert)
  - Performance-based returns, outcomes-linked
  - Atlanta, Baltimore, Louisiana expanding model

- Green Bonds
  - San Francisco PUC: $3.7B+ issued (FY15-23)
  - Climate Bonds Standard certification
  - State bonds: Prop 1 ($7.5B), Prop 68 ($4.1B), Prop 4 ($10B)

- Public-Private Partnerships (P3s)
  - California Infrastructure Finance Act framework
  - Rialto Water Services case study
  - Carlsbad Desalination Plant model

- Water Markets and Trading
  - 1.5M acre-feet traded annually in California
  - SGMA creating groundwater trading frameworks
  - Mojave Basin: $400M+ economic value from adjudication

- Pay-for-Success Models
  - Clean Water Commerce Act: $20M/year through 2030
  - Anne Arundel County PFS program model

### 4. Technical Assistance Providers (technical-assistance-providers.md)

**Organizations Documented:**
- Rural Community Assistance Corporation (RCAC)
  - Western US coverage, tribal circuit riders
  - Financial packaging, compliance, training
  - Lending programs for Indigenous communities

- Self-Help Enterprises
  - San Joaquin Valley focus, 85 communities served
  - Contamination: arsenic, nitrates, uranium, pesticides
  - Emergency response capability demonstrated

- Community Water Center
  - Environmental justice advocacy + direct services
  - San Joaquin Valley and Central Coast programs
  - Instrumental in SAFER extension through 2045

- California Rural Water Association (CRWA)
  - Operator training and certification
  - No-cost technical assistance
  - Rural Water Financing Agency lending

- State TA Funding Program / SAFER
  - Up to $130M annually for TA, planning, feasibility
  - Over $1.6B distributed, 320 systems returned to compliance

## Key Findings

### Private Foundation Landscape
- Major foundations largely operate by invitation only
- Open RFPs exist but are competitive and periodic
- Equity and environmental justice increasingly prioritized
- Collaborative funding models emerging (Klamath, CFWI)

### Alternative Financing Potential
- EIBs proven viable for water infrastructure
- Green bonds mainstream for California utilities
- P3s legal but require significant capacity
- Water markets evolving with SGMA implementation

### Technical Assistance Ecosystem
- Comprehensive TA network exists for disadvantaged communities
- SAFER program central to funding TA and capital projects
- Tribal-specific programs available through RCAC
- Demand exceeds capacity - prioritization necessary
