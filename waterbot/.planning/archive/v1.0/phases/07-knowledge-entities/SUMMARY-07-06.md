## Key Content Covered

### state-agencies.md

**CalEPA:**
- Umbrella structure for 6 boards/departments
- Coordination functions
- CalEnviroScreen development

**Department of Water Resources:**
- State Water Project (400 miles, 27M people served)
- December 2024: 5% initial allocation
- November 2024: New Incidental Take Permit
- "Elevate to '28" strategic approach
- Forecast Informed Reservoir Operations

**Department of Fish and Wildlife:**
- Lake and Streambed Alteration (Section 1602)
- 30-day completeness, 60-day draft agreement timelines
- CESA Incidental Take Permits
- November 2024 SWP ITP (5 species, 10-year term)

**Coastal Commission:**
- Coastal Development Permits
- 85th percentile storm design standards
- Low Impact Development emphasis

**Department of Pesticide Regulation:**
- Surface water monitoring
- SURF database (1.58M+ water samples)
- 2019 MAA with Water Board
- Urban monitoring since 2007

**OEHHA:**
- Public Health Goals development
- October 2025: PFHxS and arsenic PHGs initiated
- More stringent CA standards (Cr6, perchlorate, 1,2,3-TCP)

**Delta Stewardship Council:**
- Delta Plan (coequal goals)
- 2024 Five-Year Review completed
- Covered actions oversight

**State Lands Commission:**
- Public Trust Doctrine
- Sovereign lands administration
- 80+ local trustees

### federal-agencies.md

**Army Corps of Engineers:**
- Section 404 permits (dredge/fill)
- 3 California districts (LA, Sacramento, SF)
- 33 CFR Parts 320-332

**Bureau of Reclamation:**
- Central Valley Project (20 dams, 12M AF capacity)
- 250+ contractors, 29 counties
- 5M AF/year agricultural, 600K AF M&I
- Coordinated Operations Agreement with SWP

**U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service:**
- ESA Section 7 consultation
- 90-day formal consultation, 45-day BiOp
- IPaC planning tool

**NOAA Fisheries:**
- Anadromous fish critical habitat
- West Coast Region maps and GIS data
- Joint BiOps for water operations

**Coast Guard:**
- Vessel General Permit coordination
- 26 discharge types covered
- MOU with EPA

**NRCS:**
- EQIP program
- National Water Quality Initiative
- Free technical assistance

**USGS:**
- California Water Science Center
- Water Quality Portal (380M+ records)
- Real-time monitoring network

**Bureau of Land Management:**
- $73.4M FY2024 Aquatic Resources
- 5-year strategic plan through 2029
