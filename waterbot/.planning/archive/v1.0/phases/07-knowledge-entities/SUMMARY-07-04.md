## Key Content Covered

### regions-1-3.md (North Coast, SF Bay, Central Coast)

**Region 1 - North Coast:**
- Forest Activities Program and timber harvest oversight
- Cannabis Cultivation Program (December 2024 enforcement strategy)
- North Coast Salmon Project (since 2018)
- Key issues: salmon habitat, sediment, cannabis

**Region 2 - San Francisco Bay:**
- Regional Monitoring Program (since 1992, SF Estuary Institute)
- Nutrients Watershed Permit (July 2024: 40% reduction requirement)
- Municipal Regional Permit (MRP) for stormwater
- PFAS detection at 22 sites
- July-August 2022 HAB and fish kills

**Region 3 - Central Coast:**
- Agricultural Order 4.0 (R3-2021-0040) - performance-based shift
- $600M+ in public grants for water quality
- Nitrate contamination focus
- CCAMP-GAP groundwater program

### regions-4-6.md (Los Angeles, Central Valley, Lahontan)

**Region 4 - Los Angeles:**
- MS4 permits (LA County, Long Beach, Ventura)
- Numerous TMDLs (bacteria, nutrients, metals, trash)
- Industrial remediation (Boeing Santa Susana)
- Post-fire monitoring

**Region 5 - Central Valley:**
- CV-SALTS program (initiated 2006, approved 2019-2020)
- Nitrate Control Program: 1,800+ wells with replacement water
- February 2025: Priority 2 basin expansion
- ILRP and dairy facility oversight
- Three office locations (Rancho Cordova, Fresno, Redding)

**Region 6 - Lahontan:**
- Lake Tahoe TMDL (2011, joint with Nevada)
- Nearshore Water Quality Protection Plan
- ONRW designations under 2025 Triennial Review
- Two office locations (S. Lake Tahoe, Victorville)

### regions-7-9.md (Colorado River, Santa Ana, San Diego)

**Region 7 - Colorado River Basin:**
- Salton Sea ecological crisis (salinity 57 ppt vs. 35 ppt ocean)
- Pupfish Connectivity Project (March 2025: ~5,000 acres habitat)
- Agricultural conditional waivers
- New River transboundary issues

**Region 8 - Santa Ana:**
- Multiple lake TMDLs (Big Bear, Elsinore, Canyon Lake)
- Newport Bay TMDLs (bacteria amendment adopted 2022)
- Perchlorate contamination program
- Groundwater protection

**Region 9 - San Diego:**
- Tijuana River Valley Recovery Strategy (2009-present)
- August 2025: Cease and Desist Order to USIBWC
- October 2025: $1.4M Superfund assessment request
- 2025 Binational Resilience Initiative (~$650K)
- Regulatory escalation after collaborative approach insufficient
