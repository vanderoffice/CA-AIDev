# PLAN-06-03: Basin Plans (Water Quality Control Plans)

## Objective
Create comprehensive knowledge files covering California's Basin Plans - the master planning documents that establish water quality standards, beneficial uses, and implementation programs for each region.

## Deliverables
```
knowledge/06-water-quality/basin-plans/
├── basin-plans-overview.md
├── basin-plan-contents.md
└── amendment-process.md
```

## Research Sources (Validated via Perplexity)
- Water Code Section 13240 (Basin Plan requirement)
- Porter-Cologne Water Quality Control Act
- 9 Regional Water Board Basin Plans
- State Water Board policies incorporated into Basin Plans
- EPA approval requirements for federal CWA compliance

## Content Outline

### basin-plans-overview.md
- Definition: master water quality control planning document
- Legal authority: Porter-Cologne + Clean Water Act
- Three core components:
  1. Beneficial use designations
  2. Water quality objectives
  3. Implementation programs
- Nine Regional Water Boards and their regions:
  - Region 1: North Coast
  - Region 2: San Francisco Bay
  - Region 3: Central Coast
  - Region 4: Los Angeles
  - Region 5: Central Valley
  - Region 6: Lahontan
  - Region 7: Colorado River Basin
  - Region 8: Santa Ana
  - Region 9: San Diego
- Basin Plan Portal mapping tool

### basin-plan-contents.md
- **Chapter 1**: Introduction/Regional Description
- **Chapter 2**: Beneficial Uses (tables by water body)
- **Chapter 3**: Water Quality Objectives (numeric + narrative)
- **Chapter 4**: Implementation Programs
  - NPDES permits
  - Waste discharge requirements
  - Nonpoint source programs
  - TMDLs
- **Chapter 5-7**: Surveillance, plans, enforcement
- State policies incorporated by reference:
  - Antidegradation Policy (Resolution 68-16)
  - Sources of Drinking Water Policy
  - Toxics Standards Implementation Policy
  - Tribal Beneficial Uses

### amendment-process.md
- Triennial review requirement (CWA 303(c), Water Code 13240)
- Amendment triggers (new science, emerging issues, law changes)
- 7-step Basin Plan amendment process:
  1. Project proposal/request
  2. Project scoping + CEQA scoping
  3. Information gathering + draft development
  4. Peer review (external scientific review)
  5. Public workshops + 45-day comment period
  6. Regional Board hearing + adoption
  7. State Board → OAL → EPA approval
- Timeline: 12-18 months from draft to approval
- Tribal consultation requirements
