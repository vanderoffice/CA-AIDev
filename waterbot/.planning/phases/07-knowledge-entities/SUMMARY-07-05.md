## Key Content Covered

### epa-delegation.md

**NPDES Delegation:**
- California delegated since 1973 (among first states)
- Full authority for permits, enforcement, modifications
- Data flows: CIWQS â†’ ICIS-NPDES

**Section 401 Certification:**
- State certifies federal licenses/permits
- Regional Boards issue most certifications
- 2023 rule restored broader certification authority

**Section 404 Relationship:**
- Three-agency system (Corps, EPA, State)
- State issues 401 cert for Corps permits
- Section 404(g) state assumption explored but not pursued

**Oversight Authority:**
- EPA conducts periodic program reviews
- Permit quality reviews and enforcement audits
- Delegation withdrawal process (never fully invoked for CA)

### epa-programs.md

**Water Quality Standards:**
- EPA reviews within 90 days of submission
- California human health criteria (2017) more stringent
- Triennial review requirement

**TMDL Oversight:**
- 500+ TMDLs established in California
- EPA can add impaired waters to 303(d) list
- Must establish TMDL if state's disapproved

**Enforcement Coordination:**
- MOU defines lead agency roles
- State handles routine, EPA handles multi-state/federal
- SNC (Significant Noncompliance) tracking

**Joint Programs:**
- Superfund site coordination
- Environmental justice alignment (CalEnviroScreen/EJScreen)
- Climate adaptation coordination

### federal-requirements.md

**Clean Water Act Compliance:**
- Technology-based and water quality-based limits
- Section 319 nonpoint source management
- 305(b) and 303(d) reporting (Integrated Report)

**Safe Drinking Water Act:**
- California has SDWA primacy
- More stringent state MCLs (hexavalent chromium, perchlorate, 1,2,3-TCP)

**PFAS Requirements (2024):**
- PFOA: 4 ppt MCL
- PFOS: 4 ppt MCL
- Hazard Index for mixtures
- NPDES effluent guidelines in development

**Grant Requirements:**
- CWSRF and DWSRF: 20% state match
- Section 106: Pollution control programs
- Section 319: 40% non-federal match for NPS

**NEPA/CEQA Interface:**
- Joint documents for federal funding/permits
- CEQA often more stringent
- Coordination mechanisms documented
