# Formal Enforcement Actions

## Administrative Civil Liability (ACL) Complaints

### Authority
California Water Code Section 13323 and related provisions

### Process
1. **Complaint Issuance**: Regional Board Executive Officer issues complaint
2. **Contents**: Violations alleged, statutory citations, supporting evidence
3. **Response Required**: Discharger must respond within timeframe specified
4. **Hearing Request**: 90 days to request hearing (or waive)

### Hearing Process
- Prosecution Team: Water Board staff + Office of Enforcement attorneys
- Advisory Team: Provides technical/legal advice to Board members
- Board members function as judges (must be impartial)
- Burden of proof on Prosecution Team

### Resolution Options
| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Withdrawal | Fundamental error in allegations demonstrated |
| Reissuance | Prosecution Team identifies correctable flaws |
| Payment & Waiver | Full payment without contest (subject to public comment) |
| Settlement | Negotiated resolution (payment schedules, SEPs, compliance projects) |
| Full Hearing | Board decision after presentations, testimony, cross-examination |

### Board Actions After Hearing
- Impose full recommended amount
- Impose reduced amount
- Impose higher amount
- Decline to impose assessment
- Refer to Attorney General's Office

### Penalty Calculation Factors (CWC)
- Nature, circumstance, extent, gravity of violation
- Susceptibility to cleanup/abatement
- Degree of toxicity
- Ability to pay / effect on business continuity
- Voluntary cleanup efforts
- Prior violation history
- Degree of culpability
- Economic benefit from violations

## Cease and Desist Orders (CDOs)

### Authority
- CWC Section 1831 (water rights)
- CWC Section 13301 (water quality)

### Purpose
Halt ongoing violations or prevent threatened violations

### CDO Requirements
Orders direct violators to:
- Comply forthwith
- Comply per time schedule
- Take remedial/preventive action (threatened violations)

### Effective Date
CDOs become effective and final upon issuance

### Service Requirements
- Personal service, or
- Registered mail to person charged
- Copy to affected persons who appeared at hearing

### Non-Compliance Penalties
- Up to $1,000/day civil liability for violations
- Injunctive relief (TROs, preliminary/permanent injunctions)
- Attorney General referral

### Water Rights CDOs
Common uses:
- Unauthorized diversions
- Diversions harming fish and wildlife
- Curtailment order violations

## Cleanup and Abatement Orders (CAOs)

### Authority
California Water Code Section 13304

### Trigger
Discharge of waste into waters of the state in violation of:
- Waste discharge requirements
- Board orders

### Order Contents
- Identification of discharge and responsible party
- Required corrective action
- Remediation requirements
- Timeline for compliance

### Special Provisions
CAOs may require:
- Provision of replacement water supplies, OR
- Payment for uninterrupted replacement water
- For persons affected by contaminated wells/supplies

### Cleanup and Abatement Account (CAA)
- Created by CWC Sections 13440-13443
- Funded by enforcement penalties and judgments
- Provides grants for cleanup when no viable responsible party exists

### Strategic Purpose
- Establishes legal remediation obligation
- Creates baseline for measuring future compliance
- Failure to comply = separate violation

## Time Schedule Orders (TSOs)

### Authority
- CWC Section 13300: Compliance schedules
- CWC Section 13308: Conditional penalty TSOs

### Purpose
Establish enforceable compliance deadlines when immediate compliance is not feasible

### CWC 13300 TSOs
- Require submittal of detailed time schedule
- Specific actions to correct/prevent violations
- Board approval required

### CWC 13308 TSOs (Conditional Penalty)
- Establishes time schedule
- Prescribes civil penalty that becomes due if milestones missed
- Penalty amount based on amount needed to achieve compliance
- Cannot include punishment for previous violations

### Typical TSO Milestones
1. Complete engineering design
2. Obtain permits
3. Begin construction
4. Complete construction
5. Achieve final compliance

### Common Uses
- Capital improvement projects
- Treatment system upgrades
- Infrastructure construction
- BMP implementation (stormwater)

## Expedited Payment Letters (CWC 13385.1)

### Purpose
Resolve enforcement matters without formal hearing

### Process
1. Board issues expedited payment letter
2. Violator accepts allegations
3. Payment within 30 calendar days of executed waiver
4. Matter resolved without adjudication

### Benefits
- Faster resolution
- Reduced costs for all parties
- Avoids hearing process

## Notices to Comply

### Nature
Formal orders requiring corrective action with established deadlines

### Distinction from Informal NOVs
- Issued with Water Code authority
- Creates enforceable legal obligations
- Does not typically involve civil penalties initially
- Non-compliance triggers penalty assessment

## Criminal Referrals

### When Appropriate
- Fraud or false statements
- Intentional misconduct
- Knowing violations causing substantial harm
- Pattern of deliberate deception

### Referral Recipients
- District Attorney
- City Attorney
- California Attorney General
- US EPA Criminal Investigation Division
- US Attorney

### Criminal Penalties (CWA)
| Violation Type | Fine Range | Imprisonment |
|----------------|------------|--------------|
| Knowing (1st) | $5,000-$50,000/day | Up to 3 years |
| Knowing (subsequent) | Up to $100,000/day | Up to 6 years |
| Negligent | $2,500-$25,000/day | Up to 1 year |

### CWC Criminal Provisions
Willful false statements to Water Board:
- Civil penalty up to $10,000
- Prosecution by any public prosecutor

## Supplemental Environmental Projects (SEPs)

### Definition
Projects providing environmental or public health benefits beyond legal requirements

### Use in Settlements
California retains robust authority to approve SEPs in settlements (unlike federal CWA limitations)

### Characteristics
- Benefits affected communities/environments
- Often addresses impacts where violations occurred
- Provides alternative to direct monetary penalties
- Must provide tangible environmental benefit

## Escalation Pathway Summary

```
Informal → NOV → Notice to Comply → TSO/CAO/CDO → ACL Complaint → Criminal Referral
```

**Progressive enforcement moves left to right based on:**
- Violation severity
- Violator response
- Environmental harm
- Compliance history
- Intent/culpability
