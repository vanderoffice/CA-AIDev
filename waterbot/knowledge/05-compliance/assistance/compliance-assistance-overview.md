# Compliance Assistance Overview

## Philosophy

California Water Boards' compliance assistance emphasizes:
- **Prevention over punishment** - Build capacity before violations occur
- **Root cause solutions** - Address underlying barriers, not just symptoms
- **Tiered approach** - Distinguish systems that can't comply from those that won't
- **Human Right to Water** - Safe, affordable water is a fundamental right

## Legal Foundation

### California Human Right to Water
> "Every human being has the right to safe, clean, affordable, and accessible water adequate for human consumption, cooking, and sanitary purposes."

This principle reframes compliance from regulatory matter to human rights issue.

### Key Legislation
- **SB 1307 (1997)**: Established drinking water financial assistance and technical assistance for small systems
- **SB 200 (2019)**: Created Safe and Affordable Drinking Water Fund ($130M/year for 10 years)

## Compliance Assistance Hierarchy

### Progressive Approach
1. **Informal Assistance** - Technical guidance, training, resources
2. **Formal Assistance** - Compliance schedules, corrective action plans
3. **Intensive Support** - Administrator appointments, consolidation
4. **Enforcement** - Reserved for systems that won't comply despite assistance

### When Enforcement Applies
- Systems with capacity to comply that choose not to
- Repeated failure despite available assistance
- Recalcitrant parties deliberately avoiding compliance

### Technical Assistance
- Project planning and development
- Engineering and environmental analysis
- Financial analysis and rate studies
- Compliance audits
- Board and operator training

### Direct Support
- Interim water supplies (bottled, hauled)
- Direct O&M funding
- Water system administrators
- Consolidation facilitation

## Priority Populations

### Disadvantaged Communities (DAC)
- MHI < 80% of statewide median (~$56,982)
- Priority for most assistance programs
- Additional funding flexibility

### Severely Disadvantaged Communities (SDAC)
- MHI < 60% of statewide median (~$43,737)
- Highest priority
- Most favorable financing terms

## Accessing Assistance

### Technical Assistance
1. Complete Technical Assistance Request Form
2. Submit to: DFA-TArequest@waterboards.ca.gov
3. Division reviews and prioritizes
4. Matched with approved TA provider

### Financial Assistance
1. Contact Division of Financial Assistance
2. Determine eligible programs
3. Complete application requirements
4. Work with assigned project manager

### Emergency Assistance
1. Contact SAFER Engagement Unit
2. Document immediate health threat
3. Access interim solutions while planning long-term fixes

## SAFER Engagement Units

### Function
Water resource engineers and specialists supporting:
- Sustainable drinking water solutions
- Water system partnerships
- Consolidations

### Contact
Through Division of Drinking Water district offices or State Water Board
