# Small Water System Support Programs

## SAFER Program (Safe and Affordable Funding for Equity and Resilience)

### Overview
Comprehensive framework ensuring one million Californians lacking safe drinking water receive it as quickly as possible.

### Funding
- $130 million/year for 10 years (starting FY 2020-21)
- From Safe and Affordable Drinking Water Fund (SADW)

### Priority Uses (Ranked)
1. **Emergency/Urgent Needs** - Critical water shortages where other funds unavailable
2. **Out-of-Compliance Systems** - Especially small DAC systems with primary health standard violations
3. **Consolidation Acceleration** - Out-of-compliance, at-risk, and state small systems
4. **Interim Solutions + Planning** - For domestic wells above MCLs

### SAFER Components
| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Interim Water | Bottled/hauled water during crises |
| CAA Urgent Projects | Up to $500K for emergency improvements |
| Direct O&M Funding | Ongoing operational cost support |
| Technical Assistance | Expert project development help |
| Administrators | Professional management for failing systems |

## Technical Assistance Funding Program

### Administered By
Office of Sustainable Water Solutions

### Eligible Recipients
- Small disadvantaged communities (<10,000 population)
- Systems out of compliance
- Systems with insufficient water delivery
- Consolidation projects
- Systems with <200 connections

### Services Provided
- Capital improvement project coordination
- Operations and maintenance facilitation
- Engineering and environmental analysis
- Legal assistance
- Leak detection and water audits
- Compliance audits
- Financial analysis
- TMF assessments
- Board/operator training

### TA Providers
501(c)(3) non-profits and public universities selected through competitive process

### How to Apply
1. Complete Technical Assistance Request Form
2. Submit to: DFA-TArequest@waterboards.ca.gov

## Direct Operation and Maintenance Funding

### Purpose
Help small DAC/SDAC systems afford operational costs without unsustainable rate increases.

### Eligibility
- Small disadvantaged community water systems
- High affordability burden (rates >2.5% of community MHI)

### What's Funded
- Ongoing operational costs
- Maintenance expenses
- Allows affordable rates during system improvements

## Small Suppliers Water Conservation Assistance

### Partners
- CA Department of Water Resources
- CalMutuals
- GHD

### Services
- Customer metering assistance
- Indoor water-efficient fixtures (toilets, showerheads)
- Leak detection services
- Flow meters and groundwater monitors
- Special studies (water shortage plans, rate studies)

### Eligibility (By Invitation)
- Mutual water companies and very small systems
- Out of compliance with metering requirements
- High water shortage vulnerability
- Disadvantaged communities

### Impact
- Efficient toilets: Save >10,000 gallons/year
- Efficient showerheads: Save ~2,700 gallons/year

## TMF Capacity Assessment and Development

### What is TMF?
Technical, Managerial, and Financial capacity - the expertise and resources needed to provide safe, reliable water service.

### When Required
- Systems applying for state funding
- New systems seeking permits
- Systems changing ownership

### Technical Capacity
- Operator certifications
- Staff expertise
- Maintenance documentation
- Treatment system knowledge

### Managerial Capacity
- Effective governing boards
- Clear authority and responsibility
- Written policies
- Budget management procedures

### Financial Capacity
- Adequate rate structures
- Sufficient revenue generation
- Appropriate reserves
- Periodic financial audits

### TMF Forms
- Full TMF Assessment Form (most systems)
- TMF Assessment E-Z Form (simple transient non-community systems)

## Operator Certification Support

### Program
Drinking Water Operator Certification Program (~35,000 operators statewide)

### Certification Levels
| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Level 1 | Basic operations |
| Level 2 | Standard operations |
| Level 3 | Advanced operations |
| Level 4 | Overall system responsibility |

### Certification Types
- Treatment Operator
- Distribution Operator

### Support Available
- Exam preparation resources
- Training provider listings
- Free workshops (RCAC) for small systems
- Online and in-person exam options

### Certification Valid
5 years, with continuing education for recertification

## Pre-Planning and Legal Entity Formation

### Purpose
Help communities without existing water system structure establish governance.

### Services
- Formation of water districts
- Mutual water company creation
- Nonprofit corporation establishment
- Governance structure development

### Why Important
Communities need legal entities before they can:
- Own and operate systems
- Access state funding
- Enter contracts
- Hold permits

## Consolidation Support

### Step-by-Step Assistance

| Step | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 1 | Identify nearby systems (Consolidation Outreach Map Tool) |
| 2 | Check service boundaries (LAFCO, water rights) |
| 3 | Contact local systems for discussions |
| 4 | Consult SAFER Engagement Unit |
| 5 | Arrange funding (up to $5M zero-interest for receiving systems) |
| 6 | Help customers sign up with new system |
| 7 | Dissolve subsumed system |

### Consolidation Incentives
- Up to $5 million zero-interest loans for receiving systems
- Principal forgiveness for DAC consolidations
- Direct O&M support post-consolidation
- Technical assistance throughout process

## Water System Administrators

### When Appointed
System has consistently failed to provide adequate, affordable, safe drinking water to disadvantaged community despite available assistance.

### Administrator Functions
- Technical, managerial, and financial expertise
- Full or limited scope management control
- Water system improvement plan development
- Community accountability plans
- Post-administrator transition planning

### Appointment Process
1. System notification
2. Public notification and meeting
3. Public input on proposed administrator
4. Scoping and funding development
5. Formal Administrator Order
6. Implementation with community accountability
7. Transition to sustainable governance
