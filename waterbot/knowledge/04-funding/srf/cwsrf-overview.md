# Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF)

## Program Overview

The Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF) is a federal-state partnership established through Title VI of the Federal Clean Water Act of 1987. California's program is administered by the State Water Resources Control Board's Division of Financial Assistance.

### Key Statistics (FY 2024-25)
- **New loan commitments:** $679.3 million (152% increase from prior year)
- **Restricted net position:** $3.81 billion
- **Cumulative EPA funding received:** $4.24 billion
- **Cumulative state match provided:** $763.7 million

## Eligible Projects

### Core Project Categories
- **Wastewater Treatment:** Treatment plants, local sewers, sewer interceptors
- **Water Reclamation:** Recycled water treatment and distribution systems
- **Stormwater Management:** Both gray and green infrastructure solutions
- **Nonpoint Source Pollution:** Agricultural runoff, septic systems, brownfields
- **Estuary Protection:** San Francisco Bay, Morro Bay, Santa Monica Bay programs

### Green Infrastructure Solutions
- Green roofs, green streets, green walls
- Rainwater harvesting and infiltration basins
- Constructed wetlands and bioretention systems
- Permeable pavement surfaces
- Urban tree canopy establishment

### Emerging Contaminants
- PFAS treatment at publicly-owned treatment works
- Advanced treatment (reverse osmosis, granular activated carbon, ion exchange)
- Stormwater management for contaminant removal
- Microplastics capture and filtration systems

## Eligible Applicants

- Cities, towns, districts, and other public bodies
- State agencies
- Native American tribal governments and organizations
- 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
- Designated management agencies under Section 208
- National Estuary Programs
- Watershed groups, community groups, homeowner's associations (for specific project types)

## Financing Terms

### Interest Rate
- **Rate calculation:** One-half of most recent General Obligation Bond rate
- **Current rate (Nov 2025):** 2.2%
- **Historical range:** 0.80% (Nov 2021) to 3.00% (Oct 2023)

### Loan Terms
- **Maximum term:** 30 years or project useful life (whichever is shorter)
- **Repayment start:** One year after construction completion
- **No maximum funding limit** per project
- **No maximum disbursement limit** per project

### Principal Forgiveness (Bipartisan Infrastructure Law)
- **General Supplemental Funding:** 49% must be principal forgiveness/grants
- **Emerging Contaminants Funding:** 100% as principal forgiveness (no loan required)

## Application Process

### Timeline
- Applications accepted continuously throughout calendar year
- Applications received by December 31 considered for next year's priority list
- **Goal:** 95% of complete applications receive financing agreement within 9 months
- **Disbursement goal:** 100% of requests paid within 30 days

### Required Packages
1. **General Package:** Project description and applicant information
2. **Environmental Package:** CEQA compliance, federal cross-cutting requirements
3. **Technical Package:** Engineering reports and design specifications
4. **Financial Package:** Budget and financial capacity demonstration

### Priority Scoring (Maximum 20 points)
| Category | Max Points | Criteria |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Project Score | 10 | Water quality resource/impact, corrective purpose |
| Secondary Score | 3 | Multiple environmental benefits, equivalency |
| Affordability Score | 5 | Community size, MHI, rate burden |
| Readiness Score | 2 | Plans/specs status, implementation readiness |

**FY 2025-26 Fundable List Cutoff:** 13 points

## Disadvantaged Community Support

### Definitions
- **Disadvantaged Community (DAC):** Population <10,000, MHI between 60-79% of statewide MHI
- **Severely Disadvantaged Community (SDAC):** Population <10,000, MHI <60% of statewide MHI

### Special Provisions
- Automatically fundable (no competitive scoring required)
- Enhanced principal forgiveness eligibility
- Higher priority ranking in scoring system
- Technical assistance available through Office of Sustainable Water Solutions

## Water Recycling Funding Program

The Water Recycling Funding Program (WRFP) operates as a complementary mechanism:
- Construction loans and grants
- Planning grants for feasibility evaluation
- **Grant amount:** 35% of reasonable construction cost
- **FY 2024-25 appropriation:** $69.8 million
