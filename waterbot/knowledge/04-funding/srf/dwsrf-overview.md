# Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF)

## Program Overview

The Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF) provides affordable financing for drinking water infrastructure projects throughout California. Established under the Safe Drinking Water Act of 1996, the program is administered by the State Water Resources Control Board's Division of Financial Assistance.

### Program Transfer History
- Originally managed by California Department of Health Services
- Transferred to State Department of Public Health
- Transferred to State Water Resources Control Board (July 1, 2014)

### Key Statistics
- **Total financial assistance executed:** Over $5.3 billion
- **Annual repayments to DWSRF:** Approximately $147.9 million
- **FY 2025-26 loan capacity:** $300 million (base program)
- **FY 2024-25 applications:** 47 new projects requesting over $2 billion

## Eligible Project Types

### Six Primary Categories

1. **Treatment Projects**
   - Water treatment plant construction/rehabilitation
   - Filtration system upgrades
   - Advanced treatment for contaminant removal
   - Compliance with new MCL requirements

2. **Transmission and Distribution**
   - Pipe rehabilitation and replacement
   - Service lateral extensions
   - Water meter installation
   - Booster pump stations

3. **Source Development**
   - New groundwater wells
   - Surface water source development
   - Wellhead protection programs
   - Source replacement for contaminated supplies

4. **Storage Facilities**
   - Finished water storage tank construction
   - Storage infrastructure rehabilitation
   - Water quality monitoring equipment

5. **Consolidation Projects**
   - System interconnections
   - Physical and managerial consolidations
   - Regional system integration

6. **New System Creation**
   - Serving homes with contaminated wells
   - Consolidating existing systems into regional systems

## Financing Terms

### Interest Rates (2025)
- **Standard rate:** 1.9% (50% of state GO Bond rate)
- **Historical comparison:** 2.3% (2024), 2.1% (2023), 1.1% (2022)

### Loan Terms by Community Type
| Community Type | Interest Rate | Max Term |
|----------------|---------------|----------|
| Small SDAC | 0% | 40 years |
| Small DAC (rates ≥1.5% MHI) | Reduced | 30-40 years |
| Standard | 50% of GO Bond rate | 30 years |

### Repayment Structure
- Construction loans: Up to 30 years or useful life
- Planning/design loans: Up to 10 years
- Negative interest rate loans available for extreme hardship

## Grant and Principal Forgiveness

### Maximum Amounts by Category

**Small DAC/SDAC/Expanded Small DAC (Category A-C and Consolidation):**
- Up to 100% of total eligible project cost
- $80,000 per connection standard maximum
- $100,000 per connection (Deputy Director approval for good cause)
- $120,000 per connection (mandatory consolidation/appointed administrator)

**Medium DAC (20,001-100,000 population, MHI <80% statewide):**
- Up to 50% of total eligible project cost
- $40,000 per connection maximum
- $5 million per project maximum
- Requires water rates ≥1.5% of MHI

**Emerging Contaminants:**
- $25 million per water system maximum
- No limit for DAC systems serving <25,000 people

**Planning Grants:**
- No cap on individual grant awards
- Count toward 5-year total assistance limit

## Priority Categories

### Project Ranking System
| Priority | Description | Example Issues |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| A | Immediate health risk | Waterborne disease, contamination, treatment failure |
| B | Untreated at-risk sources | Vulnerable sources, emerging contamination |
| C | Compliance/supply needs | Standard violations, insufficient capacity |
| D | System reliability | Current compliance but inadequate reliability |
| E | Secondary risks | Water quality beyond primary standards |
| F | Other eligible | Efficiency improvements, asset management |

## SAFER Program Integration

The Safe and Affordable Funding for Equity and Resilience (SAFER) program, established by Senate Bill 200 (2019), integrates with DWSRF:

### SAFER Funding
- **Annual allocation:** Up to $130 million through 2030
- **Total 10-year investment:** $1.4 billion
- **Source:** 5% of Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund

### SAFER Accomplishments (2019-2024)
- 321 water systems serving 3.3 million people came off Failing list
- 170 consolidations completed, benefiting 324,101 people
- Over $900 million in drinking water grants to DACs

### Current Status (Dec 2024)
- **Failing systems:** 390 serving 811,964 Californians
- **At-Risk systems:** 589 serving 1.49 million people
- **DAC concentration:** 79% of Failing systems serve DACs
- **Equity impact:** 52% serve majority communities of color

## Consolidation Incentives

### Receiving Water System Benefits

**Zero-Interest Financing:**
- Up to $10 million per consolidated community/system

**Grant Funding per Connection:**
| Consolidated System Type | Grant Amount |
|-------------------------|--------------|
| Non-DAC system | Up to $3,000/connection |
| Small DAC | Up to $5,000/connection |
| Small SDAC | Up to $10,000/connection |

**Maximum grant:** $1 million per consolidated community

**Managerial Consolidation:**
- Up to $2 million in 0% financing per project

## Application Process

### Submission Portal
- Financial Assistance Application Submittal Tool (FAAST)

### Required Components
1. **General Application:** System info, project description
2. **Environmental Application:** CEQA compliance
3. **Technical Application:** Engineering design
4. **Financial Application:** Funding sources, financial impacts

### Key Requirements
- Authorized Representative Resolution from governing body
- Opinion of bond/general counsel
- Technical, Managerial, Financial (TMF) Assessment
- Net revenue pledge and security interest

### Disbursement Process
- Planning/design: Available after Agreement execution
- Construction: Available after finalized Agreement with approved budget
- 30-day payment requirement after receipt of funds
- Retention limits: 70% until draft deliverables, 90% until final

## Lead Service Line Replacement

### Bipartisan Infrastructure Law Provisions
- $15 billion nationwide over five years
- California FFY 2022 allocation: $250 million
- FFY 2024-25 allocation: $28.7 million (reduced due to incomplete inventory reporting)

### Requirements
- 49% must be grants/forgivable loans to DACs
- Inventory development and submission required
- Focus on identification, replacement within ROW and private property

## Set-Asides (26% of Capitalization Grants)

| Set-Aside Category | Maximum | Purpose |
|--------------------|---------|---------|
| Administration & TA | 4% or $400K | Program admin, assistance to all systems |
| Small System TA | 2% | Exclusive assistance to systems ≤10,000 |
| State Program Management | 10% | Operator certification, capacity development |
| Local Assistance | 15% (10% max per activity) | Source protection, wellhead programs |
