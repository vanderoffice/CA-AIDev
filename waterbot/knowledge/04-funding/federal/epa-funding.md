# EPA Water Infrastructure Funding Programs

## Overview

The Environmental Protection Agency administers multiple water infrastructure funding programs critical to California communities. These include the Water Infrastructure Finance and Innovation Act (WIFIA), Bipartisan Infrastructure Law allocations through State Revolving Funds, environmental justice grants, Clean Water Act Section 319 nonpoint source grants, and brownfields programs with water components.

## Water Infrastructure Finance and Innovation Act (WIFIA)

### Program Purpose

WIFIA provides federal credit assistance for large water infrastructure projects that are too large or complex for traditional state revolving fund programs. Since inception, the program has closed 141 loans totaling $22 billion in credit assistance, helping finance $48 billion in water infrastructure projects nationwide.

### Eligibility Requirements

**Eligible Borrowers:**
- State, local, and tribal government entities
- Partnerships and joint ventures for water infrastructure
- Corporations and trusts with appropriate structures
- Clean Water and Drinking Water State Revolving Fund programs

**Eligible Projects:**
- Drinking water systems (source, treatment, storage, distribution)
- Wastewater collection, treatment, and disposal systems
- Stormwater management
- Desalination facilities
- Aquifer recharge systems
- Water recycling and alternative water supply projects
- Drought prevention and mitigation

**Minimum Project Size:**
- Large communities: $20 million minimum
- Small communities (25,000 or fewer): $5 million minimum
- 15% of WIFIA funds set aside for small community projects

### Loan Terms and Rates

**Interest Rate:**
- Equal to or exceeds U.S. Treasury rate for securities of similar maturity
- Rate locked on closing date
- One-time interest rate reset available if Treasury rates decline

**Repayment Terms:**
- Up to 35 years from substantial project completion
- Principal and interest deferral up to 5 years after completion
- Customized "sculpted" payment schedules available
- Maximum 49% of eligible project costs
- Total federal assistance cannot exceed 80% when combined with other programs

### Application Process

**Phase 1 - Project Selection:**
- Submit Letter of Interest after Notice of Funding Availability
- Rolling selection throughout fiscal year
- EPA evaluates eligibility, creditworthiness, engineering feasibility

**Phase 2 - Project Approval:**
- Complete WIFIA application with extensive documentation
- EPA conducts credit due diligence and engineering review
- Negotiate loan terms and conditions

**Phase 3 - Project Monitoring:**
- Disbursement processing and loan servicing
- Ongoing compliance monitoring
- Site walkthroughs during construction

### Current Funding (2025-2026)

- $6.5 billion available for WIFIA program
- $550 million for State Water Infrastructure Finance and Innovation Act (SWIFIA)
- Total capacity: approximately $7 billion in credit assistance
- Expected to support $14 billion in total water infrastructure projects

### California Contact

- EPA Pacific Southwest Region (Region 9): https://www.epa.gov/aboutepa/epa-region-9-pacific-southwest

## Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (BIL) Water Allocations

### Overview

The Bipartisan Infrastructure Law (Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act, November 2021) represents the largest federal investment in water infrastructure in American history, appropriating over $50 billion for water projects.

### Funding Distribution

**Drinking Water State Revolving Fund:**
- $11.7 billion in base capitalization grants (5 years)
- $15 billion for lead service line replacement
- $4 billion for emerging contaminants (PFAS focus)

**Clean Water State Revolving Fund:**
- $11.7 billion in base capitalization grants (5 years)
- $1 billion for emerging contaminants

**Other Allocations:**
- $5 billion for WIIN Act grants (small/disadvantaged communities)
- $1.7 billion for geographic programs (Chesapeake Bay, Great Lakes, etc.)
- $267 million for National Estuary Program and related initiatives

### California's FY 2025 Allocations

| Program | Amount |
|---------|--------|
| DWSRF Base Capitalization | $117.662 million |
| CWSRF Base Capitalization | $268.618 million |
| DWSRF Emerging Contaminants | $82.428 million |
| CWSRF Emerging Contaminants | $177.966 million |
| CWSRF NPS Emerging Contaminants | $15.362 million |
| **FY 2025 Total** | **~$468.7 million** |

### Lead Service Line Replacement

- California received $250 million in FY 2022
- Funds identification and full replacement of lead service lines
- Disadvantaged communities: up to 100% principal forgiveness
- Non-disadvantaged: 0% interest rate financing

### Emerging Contaminants Funding

**Eligible Projects:**
- New treatment facilities for emerging contaminants
- Upgrades to existing treatment systems
- Development of new uncontaminated water sources
- Consolidation with uncontaminated systems
- Replacement of contaminated private wells
- Planning and design activities

**Funding Terms:**
- Disadvantaged communities: up to 100% of costs, no repayment
- Non-disadvantaged (>25,000 population): up to 50% as forgivable loans, max $25 million

### Disadvantaged Community Requirements

BIL mandates most funding be provided to disadvantaged communities through grants or principal forgiveness, supporting the Justice40 Initiative (40% of benefits to disadvantaged communities).

**California DAC Definition:**
- Median household income less than 80% of statewide MHI

### Application Process

- Submit through Financial Assistance Application Submittal Tool (FAAST)
- California State Water Resources Control Board administers programs
- Annual Intended Use Plans establish priorities and fundable lists
- Contact: (916) 327-9978

## Environmental Justice Grants

### Environmental Justice Small Grants Program

**Overview:**
- Operating since 1994
- Over $37 million awarded to 1,500+ organizations
- Supports community-based environmental justice solutions

**Funding:**
- Up to $100,000 per project
- FY 2025: Limited to federally recognized tribal governments
- Previous cycles included CBOs, nonprofits, and tribal governments

**Eligible Activities:**
- Understanding exposure to environmental harms
- Building community consensus on priorities
- Public outreach and education
- Projects associated with Clean Water Act, Safe Drinking Water Act

### Inflation Reduction Act Environmental and Climate Justice Program

**Appropriations:**
- $2.8 billion for financial assistance
- $200 million for technical assistance
- All grants must be awarded by September 30, 2026

**Components:**
- Community Change Grants
- Environmental Justice Collaborative Problem-Solving
- Environmental Justice Government-to-Government
- Thriving Communities Technical Assistance Centers and Grantmakers

**Current Status (2025):**
- Program terminated in January 2025
- Class action lawsuit filed June 25, 2025
- Federal court hearing August 5, 2025
- Status uncertain pending litigation

### Water Technical Assistance Program

- Non-financial support for disadvantaged communities
- Helps identify water challenges and develop plans
- Assists with grant application preparation
- Request form available through EPA regional offices

## Clean Water Act Section 319 Grants

### Program Purpose

Section 319 grants fund projects reducing nonpoint source pollution from agricultural operations, forestry, urban stormwater, and other diffuse sources not traceable to specific discharge points.

### California Program

**Annual Funding:** Approximately $3-4 million

**Eligible Applicants:**
- Nonprofit organizations (501(c)(3))
- Federally recognized tribes
- Federal, state, local, and public agencies
- Public colleges

### Eligible Project Types

- Livestock management (fencing, off-stream watering)
- Forest road upgrades and decommissioning
- Stream restoration (woody debris, riparian revegetation)
- Agricultural BMPs (cover crops, filter strips, nutrient management)
- Dairy and equestrian facility improvements
- Dredging contaminated sediments

### Funding Amounts

| Project Type | Minimum | Maximum |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| Implementation Projects | $250,000 | $1,000,000 |
| Planning Projects | $50,000 | $250,000 |

### Requirements

- 25% non-federal match (waiver available for DACs)
- Must address priority watersheds identified by Regional Water Boards
- Nine-element watershed-based plan required

### Application Timeline

- Applications through FAAST system
- FY 2026 deadline: December 19, 2025
- Contact: (916) 327-9978

## Brownfields Grants (Water Components)

### Program Overview

EPA Brownfields grants fund assessment, cleanup, and remediation of contaminated properties, including those with water contamination requiring remediation as a precondition for beneficial reuse.

### California FY 2025 Awards

- $10.45 million to 9 recipients
- Includes assessment, cleanup, and revolving loan fund grants
- Notable: Los Angeles County Malibu Mesa Water Reclamation Refurbishment

### Grant Categories

**Assessment Grants:**
- Fund inventories, planning, environmental assessments
- FY25: ~148 awards, $121.8 million nationally
- Community outreach activities

**Cleanup Grants:**
- Direct cleanup activities at brownfield sites
- FY26: $107 million for ~36 grants
- Award ranges: $500,000 to $4,000,000

**Revolving Loan Fund:**
- Loans and subgrants for cleanup
- Creates permanent local funding mechanisms
- Supplemental funding for high performers

### Water-Related Eligibility

- Groundwater contamination remediation
- Pump-and-treat systems
- Soil vapor extraction
- Permeable reactive barriers
- Containment systems and slurry walls

### Application Information

- FY26 deadline: January 28, 2026
- Submit through Grants.gov
- Contact: brownfields@epa.gov
- EPA Pacific Southwest Regional Office
