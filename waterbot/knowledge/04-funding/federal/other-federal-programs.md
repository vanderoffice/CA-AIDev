# Other Federal Water Funding Programs

## Overview

Beyond EPA, USDA, and FEMA programs, several other federal agencies provide significant water infrastructure funding. These include the Bureau of Reclamation (Interior), Army Corps of Engineers (Defense), HUD Community Development Block Grants, and Economic Development Administration (Commerce).

## Bureau of Reclamation WaterSMART Program

### Program Overview

WaterSMART is a comprehensive federal water funding mechanism for western states. Since 2010, Reclamation has selected 2,379 projects nationwide funded with $3.86 billion, leveraging $9.2 billion in non-federal investment.

### FY 2026 Budget Context

- Total Reclamation budget request: $1.27 billion
- Water and Related Resources: $1.11 billion
- FY 2025 WaterSMART allocation: $90 million (down $57 million from FY 2024)

## Title XVI Water Reclamation and Reuse Program

### Legislative Authority

Title XVI of the Reclamation Projects Authorization and Adjustment Act of 1992 (as amended by WIIN Act 2016) provides the sole federal authority for water recycling/reuse in western states and Hawaii.

### Program Impact

- ~$700 million federal funding leveraged with $3.3 billion non-federal
- Hundreds of thousands of acre-feet added to national water supply

### Cost-Share Requirements

| Component | Federal Share | Non-Federal Share |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Standard | 25% | 75% |
| Maximum Federal | $20 million or 25% of total (whichever less) | - |

### California Authorized Projects

California has 41 authorized Title XVI projects, including:
- Antioch Recycled Water Project (2008)
- City of Corona Water Recycling and Reuse (2009)
- Inland Empire Regional Water Recycling (2008)
- North Bay Water Reuse Program (2009)
- Many others statewide

### Eligibility Requirements

- Completed Feasibility Study reviewed by Reclamation
- Findings transmitted to Congress for project eligibility
- Meets Reclamation Manual Release WTR 11-01 requirements

### Eligible Uses

- Municipal wastewater reuse
- Industrial wastewater reuse
- Agricultural wastewater reuse
- Impaired ground/surface water treatment
- Power plant cooling
- Saltwater intrusion prevention
- Marsh/wetland restoration
- Potable water creation

### Funding Requests

- Planning, design, or construction (or combination)
- NEPA compliance costs eligible
- No ground-disturbing activities until environmental compliance complete

### Cost Calculation

Reclamation calculates cost per acre-foot considering:
- Expected project service life
- Federal planning discount rate
- Planning, design, construction costs
- O&M and energy costs

### Program Demand

- Annual funding: ~$20 million
- Current demand exceeds available funding by >$700 million
- Highly competitive program

## Army Corps of Engineers Programs

### Section 219 Environmental Infrastructure Program

**Authorization:** Water Resources Development Act of 1992, Section 219

**Purpose:** Assists public entities in providing water-related environmental infrastructure

**Cost Share:**
| Standard | 75% federal / 25% local |
| WRDA 2024 Enhancement | Up to 90% for certain projects |

**Eligible Applicants:**
- Cities, municipalities, counties
- Communities and public utilities
- Local governments

**Eligible Projects:**
- Wastewater treatment and related facilities
- Water supply, storage, treatment, distribution
- Wastewater transmission lines
- Recycled water systems
- Well design and construction
- Reservoir replacement

**Requirements:**
- Located in authorized geographic area
- Owned by public entity
- Water-related environmental infrastructure
- Requires technical, planning, or design assistance
- Not connected to other federal/USACE project

**Sponsor Responsibilities:**
- Provide all lands, easements, rights-of-way (LERRDs)
- Responsible for all O&M, repair, replacement, rehabilitation

**Note:** Funding only provided through annual appropriation bills

**Los Angeles District Contact:** SPL219@usace.army.mil

## HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG)

### Program Overview

Annual formula grants to states, cities, and counties for community development, including water and sewer infrastructure.

### Eligible Grantees

**Entitlement Communities (direct from HUD):**
- Principal cities of Metropolitan Statistical Areas
- Cities with populations ≥50,000
- Qualified Urban Counties with populations ≥200,000

**State Program (non-entitlement):**
- Cities with populations <50,000
- Counties with populations <200,000 in unincorporated areas

### National Objectives

All activities must meet one of:
1. Benefit low- and moderate-income persons
2. Prevent/eliminate slums or blight
3. Address urgent community development needs

**70% Rule:** At least 70% of funds must benefit low/moderate-income persons

### California State CDBG

**Administrator:** California Housing and Community Development (HCD)

**DAC Definition:**
- Low/moderate income: ≤80% of county median income (adjusted for family size)

**FY 2025-26 Application:**
- Portal opens: February 2, 2026
- Annual NOFA process
- 2025 NOFA amended December 15, 2025

**Contact:** https://www.hcd.ca.gov/funding/cdbg

### Eligible Water Infrastructure Activities

- Water facilities construction and improvements
- Sewer facilities construction and improvements
- Must serve qualifying populations

## Application Considerations

### Common Requirements

| Program | Cost Share | Application Portal |
|---------|------------|-------------------|
| WaterSMART | 50% federal | Grants.gov |
| Title XVI | 25% federal | Grants.gov |
| USACE Sec 219 | 75-90% federal | Direct to district |
| CDBG | Formula-based | HUD/HCD |
| EDA Public Works | Varies | Grants.gov |

### Environmental Compliance

All federal programs require NEPA compliance:
- Environmental review before ground-disturbing activities
- Some programs fund NEPA compliance costs
- Factor review timelines into project schedules

### Feasibility Studies

Title XVI and large infrastructure projects typically require:
- Completed feasibility study meeting federal standards
- Technical and economic feasibility demonstration
- Non-federal cost-sharing commitments
- 12-24 months typical completion time

### Multi-Program Strategies

Successful projects often combine:
- Federal planning/design funding (Reclamation, USACE)
- State matching funds (DWR, Water Boards)
- Construction funding (Title XVI, WaterSMART)
- Local financing (bonds, user fees)
