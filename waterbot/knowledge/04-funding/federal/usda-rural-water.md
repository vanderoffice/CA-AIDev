# USDA Rural Development Water Programs

## Overview

USDA Rural Development administers specialized water and waste disposal programs providing financial and technical assistance to rural communities. These represent the only federal initiative exclusively focused on rural water infrastructure for communities with populations of 10,000 or fewer.

## Rural Definition and Population Thresholds

### Standard Definition

- **USDA Water Programs:** 10,000 or fewer residents
- **SEARCH Program:** 2,500 or fewer residents
- **Census Bureau Definition:** Open country and settlements with fewer than 2,000 housing units and 5,000 residents

### Key Considerations

- Population thresholds vary by program
- Rural residents may live in metropolitan counties
- Eligibility verified through USDA Rural Development website address database
- Colonias (established before October 1, 1989) have special eligibility

## Water and Waste Disposal Loan & Grant Program

### Program Purpose

The primary financing mechanism for rural water and sanitation projects, providing long-term, low-interest loans combined with grants to communities that cannot obtain adequate credit through conventional channels.

### Eligible Applicants

- State and local governmental entities
- Private nonprofit organizations
- Federally recognized tribes and tribal entities

### Service Area Requirements

- Rural areas and towns with 10,000 or fewer residents
- Tribal lands in rural areas
- Colonias (unincorporated communities established before October 1, 1989)

### Current Interest Rates (Q1 2026)

| Community Classification | Interest Rate |
|-------------------------|---------------|
| Poverty-level (lowest income) | 2.875% |
| Intermediate | 3.750% |
| Market rate (highest income) | 4.750% |

### Loan Terms

- Repayment period: up to 40 years based on useful facility life
- Maximum funding: No stated cap (based on need and fund availability)
- May combine loans with grants when funds available

### Eligible Uses

**Core Infrastructure:**
- Drinking water systems (source, treatment, storage, distribution)
- Sanitary sewage systems (collection, transmission, treatment, disposal)
- Stormwater management systems
- Solid waste disposal infrastructure

**Related Activities:**
- Legal and engineering fees
- Land acquisition and water rights
- Equipment purchase and installation
- Permits and regulatory compliance
- Start-up costs and initial O&M
- Construction period interest
- Acquisition of existing facilities

### Application Process

- Applications accepted year-round
- Submit through RD Apply (online) or local USDA Rural Development office
- Level 2 eAuthentication ID required for online submission
- Contact local office before applying for technical guidance

## Emergency Community Water Assistance Grants (ECWAG)

### Program Purpose

Provides rapid response funding for communities experiencing acute water emergencies threatening safe, reliable drinking water availability.

### Eligible Emergencies

**Natural Disasters:**
- Drought conditions
- Flood events
- Earthquakes and earth movements
- Tornadoes and hurricanes

**Other Events:**
- Disease outbreaks (pathogenic contamination)
- Chemical spills, leaks, or seepage
- Equipment failures

### Key Requirements

- Federal disaster declaration NOT required
- Major decline in water quality/quantity within 2 years of application
- Or imminent decline anticipated
- Population 10,000 or fewer
- Median household income below state non-metropolitan MHI

### Funding Limits

| Grant Type | Maximum Amount |
|------------|----------------|
| Emergency Repairs (existing systems) | $150,000 |
| Water Transmission Lines | $150,000 |
| Water Source Infrastructure | $1,000,000 |

### Eligible Uses

- Waterline extensions and repairs
- Construction of new wells
- Reservoirs and transmission lines
- Water treatment plants
- Infrastructure to water supply point

### Application

- No matching funds required
- Applications accepted year-round
- Submit through RD Apply or local office
- Expedited processing for genuine emergencies

## Rural Water Circuit Rider Program

### Program Purpose

Provides on-site, hands-on technical assistance to small rural water systems through experienced water utility professionals. Operated by National Rural Water Association under USDA contract.

### Program Structure

- 132 circuit riders serving all 50 states and Puerto Rico
- 24/7 availability at no cost to systems
- Activity-based training emphasizing ownership of solutions

### Assistance Topics

**Governance:**
- Board training for water system governance
- Fiduciary responsibilities

**Management and Finance:**
- Financial planning and rate-setting
- Accounting practices
- Financial sustainability analysis

**Operations:**
- Treatment process optimization
- Equipment maintenance
- System reliability improvement
- Infrastructure inspection

**Compliance:**
- Safe Drinking Water Act requirements
- Regulatory compliance assistance
- Quality control practices

**Security:**
- Physical facility security
- SCADA and cybersecurity protection

**Special Support:**
- Loan applications and reporting
- Disaster and emergency response

### Eligibility

- Water systems serving 10,000 or fewer people
- Public bodies, nonprofit corporations, or Indian tribes
- Must have legal authority to own/operate water facility

## Special Evaluation Assistance for Rural Communities (SEARCH)

### Program Purpose

Provides grants to cover predevelopment feasibility studies, design work, and technical assistance that rural communities cannot afford but must complete before applying for construction financing.

### Eligibility Requirements

**Population:** 2,500 or fewer (more restrictive than standard programs)

**Income:** Must meet one of:
- Median household income below poverty line
- MHI less than 80% of statewide non-metropolitan MHI

**Applicants:**
- State and local governmental entities
- Nonprofit organizations
- Federally recognized tribes

### Funding Limits

- Maximum: $60,000 or 75% of predevelopment costs (whichever is lower)
- Based on demonstrated need
- Subject to fund availability

### Eligible Uses

**Predevelopment Planning:**
- Feasibility studies
- Preliminary design and engineering
- Cost estimates
- Technical application assistance

**Related Infrastructure Planning:**
- Public buildings and roads
- Bridges and utilities
- Other public improvements supporting water/waste facilities

### Important Conditions

- Cannot pay for already-completed work
- If project receives USDA construction loan/grant, SEARCH amount subtracted from grant eligibility
- If construction application denied, SEARCH grant does not require repayment

### Application

- Accepted year-round through local USDA Rural Development offices

## Disadvantaged Community Definitions

### USDA Classification

Interest rates tied to median household income:
- **Poverty-level:** Lowest MHI threshold = lowest rates (2.875%)
- **Intermediate:** Middle MHI threshold = moderate rates (3.750%)
- **Market:** Higher MHI threshold = highest rates (4.750%)

### California State Definition

- **Disadvantaged Community (DAC):** MHI < 80% of statewide MHI
- **Severely Disadvantaged Community (SDAC):** MHI < 60% of statewide MHI

### Benefits

Disadvantaged communities receive:
- Lower interest rates
- Higher grant percentages
- Enhanced eligibility for principal forgiveness
- Technical assistance priority
