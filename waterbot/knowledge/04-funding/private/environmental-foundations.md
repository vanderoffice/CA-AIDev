# Environmental and Conservation Foundations

This document covers major environmental and conservation foundations that fund California water infrastructure and conservation projects, including their water-related programs, grant sizes, and application processes.

## David and Lucile Packard Foundation

The Packard Foundation is one of the most significant philanthropic forces in California environmental funding, with strong commitments to communities, conservation, and climate.

### California Communities Initiative

The foundation prioritizes ensuring every person has access to clean air, water, and safe green spaces regardless of income or zip code.

**Geographic Focus (Six Counties):**
- Fresno County
- Monterey County
- San Benito County
- San Mateo County
- Santa Clara County
- Santa Cruz County

**Plus:** Statewide opportunities to scale community-led solutions

### Ocean Initiative

The Ocean Initiative addresses threats to marine and coastal ecosystems:

**Three Major Issue Areas:**
1. Conservation of ocean areas rich in marine life (US, Indonesia, Chile)
2. Ending illegal fishing and ensuring sustainable fisheries management
3. Advancing responsible floating offshore wind energy in California

### Grant Parameters

**Typical Grant Sizes:**
- Open calls: $50,000 to $200,000
- Typically 12-month grant terms
- Larger institutional grants available

**Indirect Cost Policy (Distinctive Feature):**
| Organization Budget | Minimum Indirect Rate |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| Under $5 million | 25% |
| $5-50 million | 20% |
| Over $50 million | 15% |
| Universities | 15% (capped) |

### Eligibility

The foundation can provide grants to:
- Public and private nonprofits
- Fiscal-sponsored projects
- Various organization types

**Cannot provide grants to:** Individuals

**Actively Encouraged to Apply:**
- Organizations without existing foundation relationships
- Smaller grassroots organizations
- Organizations led by Black, Indigenous, Latino, and People of Color

## William and Flora Hewlett Foundation

The Hewlett Foundation operates with approximately $13.9 billion in assets, with environment representing its largest area of giving.

### Environment Program Structure

**Two Core Strategies:**

1. **Climate Initiative**
   - Addresses global climate crisis
   - Supports greenhouse gas reduction efforts
   - Accelerates clean energy transition
   - Priority regions: US, China, India, Europe

2. **Western Conservation Strategy** (since 1969)
   - Protects natural resources of Western US and Canada
   - Goal: Conserve 320 million acres by 2035
   - Community-driven, inclusive approaches
   - Upholds Indigenous sovereignty

### Grant Parameters

**Typical Range:** $10,000 to nearly $20 million
**Most Common:** $50,000 to $750,000

The foundation awards thousands of grants annually, with major recipients including:
- ClimateWorks Foundation
- Energy Foundation
- Regional conservation organizations

### Application Process

**Letters of Inquiry accepted for:**
- Climate and Energy program

**Process:**
1. Submit Letter of Inquiry (any time)
2. If interested, foundation invites full proposal
3. Foundation studies organization's programs and priorities

### Klamath Basin Collaborative

The Hewlett Foundation has made long-term commitments to Klamath River restoration:

- Supported removal of four dams in 2024
- Established Klamath Philanthropic Funder Collaborative
- Funds diverse Tribes and nonprofits for:
  - Environmental monitoring and research
  - Public communications
  - Coalition-building
  - Sustainable agriculture
  - Wildfire resilience
  - Advocacy

## Gordon and Betty Moore Foundation

The Moore Foundation, headquartered in Palo Alto, emphasizes risk-taking and long-term commitments to challenges not accessible to many other organizations.

### Environmental Conservation Program

The program balances long-term conservation with sustainable use:
- Protecting critical ecosystems
- Establishing replicable collaboration models
- Since 2001: Helped conserve over 400 million hectares in the Amazon

### Program Areas

**Open Lands:**
- Land conservation
- Forest management and wildfire protection
- Preventing deforestation

**Clean Water:**
- Advocacy, research, and education
- Water quality protection

**Healthy Oceans:**
- Protected marine zoning and policy
- Fishing protections
- Effective enforcement

**Future Generations:**
- Youth and community programs
- Thought leadership and scholars
- Healthy communities

### Grant Parameters

**Annual Grantmaking:** $5-12 million
**Typical Range:** $2,000 to $500,000
**Average Grant:** Approximately $10,000
**Grants Exceeding $1 Million:** Rare

### Geographic Priorities

While not limited to these areas:
- Colorado
- New Mexico
- Long Island
- North Carolina
- Panama
- Bahamas

### Moore Inventor Fellows

Supports scientist-inventors with:
- **Total Award:** $825,000 over three years
- **Additional:** $50,000/year from home institution
- Focus: Advancing innovations in various fields including environmental technology

### Application Process

**Critical:** The Moore Charitable Foundation does not accept unsolicited proposals and does not provide a direct avenue for getting in touch.

## National Fish and Wildlife Foundation (NFWF)

NFWF provides competitive funding to sustain, restore, and enhance the nation's fish, wildlife, plants, and habitats.

### Grant Programs

NFWF supports over 70 grant programs. Key programs for California water include:

### Five Star Wetland and Urban Waters Restoration Program

**Collaborative Partners:**
- National Fish and Wildlife Foundation
- EPA
- USDA

**Focus Areas:**
- Wetland restoration
- Forest and riparian habitat restoration
- Coastal habitat restoration
- Stormwater management
- Outreach and stewardship

### Five Star Program Requirements

**Five Priority Areas (all required):**
1. Habitat restoration (including green infrastructure for stormwater)
2. Meaningful education and training activities
3. Partnerships
4. Measurable ecological, educational, and community results
5. Sustainability

### Grant Parameters

**Annual Funding:** Approximately $2.6 million
**Award Range:** $20,000 to $50,000
**Average Award:** $35,000
**Duration:** 12-18 months
**Match Requirement:** 1:1 (non-federal, can include in-kind)

### Eligibility

- State and local governments
- U.S. territories
- Tribal governments
- Academic institutions
- Nonprofit organizations

### California Five Star Example

| Grantee | Award | Match | Total | Project |
|---------|-------|-------|-------|---------|
| Friends of the Los Angeles River | $47,800 | $500,900 | $548,700 | LA River watershed restoration, 3,000 volunteers, invasive removal, student education |

### Program Impact

- Over 1,000 projects funded in 50 states
- $30.6 million in grants
- Leveraged $96.1 million in matching funds

### Application Process

**System:** Easygrants (online)

**Features:**
- Submit pre-proposals and full proposals electronically
- Access unlimited times, save before submission
- Update and reuse prior proposals
- Download PDF versions for records

**Contact:**
- **Website:** nfwf.org/apply-grant
- **Email:** info@nfwf.org
- **Review:** Check specific program Request for Proposals

## California Community Foundation

While primarily focused on Los Angeles County, California Community Foundation operates significant environmental and water initiatives.

### Focus Areas

The foundation addresses:
- Climate change adaptation
- Food equity
- Wildfire recovery
- Environmental justice

### Environmental Commitment

Recognizes that marginalized communities face disproportionate impacts from:
- Rising temperatures
- Intensifying wildfires
- Disrupted food systems

### Key Initiatives

**LA County Food Equity Roundtable:**
- Improves access to nutritious food
- Drives systemic changes for long-term resilience

**Natural Disaster Team:**
- Supports marginalized communities
- Equips local organizations
- Strengthens infrastructure
- Advocates for equitable response policies

### Safe, Clean Water Grants Program

Partnership with Water Foundation for LA County stormwater education and engagement:
- **Funding Available:** $8.5 million
- **Application Period:** September 24, 2024 - April 4, 2025
- **Expenditure Deadline:** July 31, 2026

### Eligibility

**Competitive Grants consider:**
- 501(c)(3) nonprofits (not private foundations)
- Located within and primarily serving LA County residents
- Regional/statewide policy efforts benefiting local population may qualify

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Alignment with strategic plans
- Demonstrated program impact
- Organizational capacity
- Engagement in systemic change
- Organizational sustainability
- Service to LA County residents

### Application Process

1. Take online interactive eligibility test
2. Create account on NonprofitConnect portal
3. Submit letter of intent through portal
4. Sample materials available for download

### Contact Information

- **Phone:** 213-239-2330
- **Email:** grantsmanager@calfund.org
- **Website:** calfund.org

## Strategic Recommendations

### Foundation Selection Matrix

| Water Need | Best Foundation Match |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Coastal/Marine | Packard Ocean Initiative, Moore Healthy Oceans |
| Urban Stormwater | California Community Foundation, NFWF Five Star |
| Watershed Restoration | Packard California Communities, Hewlett Western Conservation |
| Climate Resilience | Hewlett Climate Initiative, CCF |
| Habitat Restoration | NFWF, Moore Open Lands |

### Application Strength Factors

1. **Community Engagement:** All foundations value meaningful community participation
2. **Equity Focus:** Prioritize projects serving disadvantaged communities
3. **Measurable Outcomes:** Clear metrics for environmental and community benefits
4. **Partnerships:** Strong coalitions across sectors
5. **Sustainability:** Long-term viability beyond grant period

### Collaborative Funding Opportunities

Foundations increasingly coordinate funding:
- Klamath Philanthropic Funder Collaborative (Hewlett-led)
- Community Foundation Water Initiative model
- NFWF + EPA + USDA partnerships

Consider approaching multiple aligned foundations for larger projects.
