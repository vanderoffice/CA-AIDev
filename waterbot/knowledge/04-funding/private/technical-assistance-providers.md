# Technical Assistance Providers

This document covers organizations that provide technical assistance to California water systems, especially small and disadvantaged communities, including their services, eligibility requirements, and how to access support.

## Overview: California's Drinking Water Crisis

Over one million Californians currently lack safe drinking water, with another million at risk. Technical assistance providers fill a critical gap between regulatory agencies enforcing standards and communities struggling to meet them.

**Common Challenges Addressed:**
- Groundwater contamination (arsenic, nitrates, uranium, pesticides, 1,2,3-TCP)
- Aging infrastructure
- Limited budgets and understaffed operations
- Regulatory compliance
- Access to capital financing

## Rural Community Assistance Corporation (RCAC)

RCAC serves as the primary technical assistance and lending entity for rural and Indigenous communities throughout the Western United States.

### Mission and Coverage

- **Established:** 1978
- **Mission:** Build more resilient and thriving rural communities
- **Service Area:** California, Nevada, Arizona, Hawaii, Pacific Islands, Alaska, Idaho, Oregon, Washington
- **Role:** Western Regional partner within Rural Community Assistance Partnership (RCAP) network

### Services Offered

**Project Development:**
- Financial packaging for grant/loan applications
- Multi-source funding identification
- Asset management strategies
- Capital improvement planning

**Regulatory Compliance:**
- Safe Drinking Water Act compliance
- California Health and Safety Code requirements
- Compliance plan development

**Operations Support:**
- Energy audits
- Leak detection
- Consultant/contractor procurement
- Consolidation and regionalization evaluation

**Financial Management:**
- Rate setting and Proposition 218 compliance
- Fiscal systems and accounting
- Budget development
- Ethics and conflict-of-interest training

**Workforce Development:**
- Operator certification training
- Operation and maintenance manuals
- Training workshops (in-person and online)

### Eligibility

- Small communities (fewer than 10,000 residents)
- Disadvantaged communities (MHI below 80% of state average)
- Communities with documented water quality/quantity problems
- Small water systems, municipal utilities, water districts
- Tribal water authorities
- School districts serving rural communities

### Tribal Circuit Rider Program

Dedicated technical assistance for Indigenous communities:
- Routine, in-depth training visits to tribal water systems
- Works directly with tribal water managers
- Culturally appropriate services
- Operates in California, Arizona, and Nevada
- Native American Water Master's Association for professional networking

### Lending Programs

**Rooted Relative Fund:**
- Patient capital for Indigenous entrepreneurs and community enterprises
- Designed for tribal enterprises with community-oriented missions

**Environmental Finance Center:**
- Financial management training
- Technical assistance for improving financial sustainability

### State Funding Access

RCAC helps communities access:
- SAFER program
- Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF)
- Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF)
- Proposition 68 funding
- Other California water infrastructure programs

## Self-Help Enterprises

Self-Help Enterprises focuses on assisting disadvantaged communities in California's Central Valley with critical infrastructure, housing, and community development.

### Safe Drinking Water Program

**Communities Served:** 85 small disadvantaged communities in San Joaquin Valley

**Contaminants Addressed:**
- Naturally occurring arsenic
- Nitrates (agricultural sources)
- Uranium
- Pesticides (DBCP, 1,2,3-TCP)
- Bacterial contamination

### Services Offered

**Assessment:**
- Water quality investigation
- Testing and documentation
- Help understanding California drinking water standards

**Project Development:**
- Water system needs assessment
- Options evaluation
- Project design development
- Government loan/grant application assistance

**Training:**
- System operations
- Maintenance
- Financial management
- Regulatory compliance

**Rate Setting:**
- Sustainable user rate development
- Balancing affordability with revenue needs

### Geographic Focus

- San Joaquin Valley
- Tulare Lake Basin (Fresno, Tulare, Kings, Kern counties)
- Central Valley region

### Emergency Response Example

**Del Rancho Y Mobile Villa (August 2022):**
- Community well ran dry during extreme heat (105°F+)
- 200+ residents without water
- Self-Help Enterprises delivered bottled water within hours
- Established temporary pipeline connections within days
- Submitted emergency SAFER application for consolidation
- Demonstrated immediate response + long-term solution capacity

### State Funding Access

- SAFER program
- Drinking Water State Revolving Fund
- California Department of Water Resources Small Community Drought Relief Program

## California Rural Water Association (CRWA)

CRWA serves as the state membership organization and primary training provider for small and rural water system operators, managers, and board members.

### Technical Assistance Program

**Services (No Cost):**
- On-site troubleshooting and assessment
- Regulatory guidance
- Leak detection (advanced acoustic tools)
- MHI (Median Household Income) studies
- Treatment, distribution, and collection system assessments
- Rate setting and Proposition 218 compliance
- GIS mapping support
- Customized staff training
- Standard operating procedure development

### Training Programs

**Delivery Methods:**
- Online courses via WaterLMS platform
- In-person workshops
- Certification preparation courses

**Topics:**
- Water distribution systems
- Water treatment
- Operator certification preparation
- Board member governance and financial management

**Cost:** Courses start at $20

### Operator Certification Support

California certification levels:
- Grade 1: Basic knowledge, high school diploma
- Grade 2-4: Progressive experience requirements
- Grade 5: Advanced expertise, 10+ years, specialized training

CRWA provides:
- Preparation courses
- Exam support
- Guidance on education and experience requirements

**Interim Loan Program:**

## State Technical Assistance Funding Program

The State Water Resources Control Board's Office of Sustainable Water Solutions administers the Technical Assistance Funding Program.

### Services Funded

- Coordination and development of capital improvement projects
- Operation and maintenance facilitation
- Engineering and environmental analysis
- Legal assistance
- Leak detection and water audits
- Compliance audits
- Financial analysis
- Technical, managerial, and financial assessments
- Board and operator training

### Priority Categories

**Highest Priority:**
- Systems out of compliance or with insufficient delivery
- Communities experiencing drought or contamination
- Consolidation projects
- Systems serving fewer than 200 connections
- Small/low-cost needs enabling funding applications

### Eligibility Definitions

- **Small Communities:** Population fewer than 10,000
- **Disadvantaged Communities:** MHI below 80% of statewide median

### How to Access

Submit online Technical Assistance Request Form to State Water Board's Division of Financial Assistance

**Note:** Demand significantly exceeds capacity. Not all requests can be accommodated.

### Contact Information

- **Website:** waterboards.ca.gov/water_issues/programs/grants_loans/tech_asst_funding.html

## SAFER Program (Safe and Affordable Funding for Equity and Resilience)

### Program Overview

- **Established:** Senate Bill 200 (2019)
- **Purpose:** Ensure one million Californians without safe water receive access
- **Funding:** Up to $130 million per year for up to ten years

### Eligible Applicants

- Public agencies
- Nonprofit organizations
- Public utilities
- Mutual water companies
- California Native American tribes
- Administrators
- Groundwater sustainability agencies

### Program Priorities

1. Emergency/urgent funding needs
2. Community water systems and schools out of compliance
3. Accelerating consolidations
4. Interim solutions for state small water systems and domestic wells

### Technical Assistance Integration

SAFER provides up to $130 million annually specifically for:
- Technical assistance
- Planning
- Feasibility studies

This TA funding enables communities to complete preliminary work required before accessing capital funding.

### Program Impact

- Distributed over $1.6 billion in grants
- 320 water systems serving 3.3 million people returned to compliance
- $20 million invested in hauled water for drought-impacted households

### Domestic Well Support

Recognizes domestic well users lack governance structures:
- Domestic well testing
- Replacement water provisions
- Consolidation with small water systems

### Recent Achievements

**SAFER Extended Through 2045:**
- Governor Newsom and Legislature three-party deal
- Continuous appropriation of up to $130 million annually
- Additional $180 million from Proposition 4

### Contact Information

- **Website:** waterboards.ca.gov/safer/

## State Funding Programs Accessed Through TA Providers

### Safe and Affordable Drinking Water Fund

- Up to $130 million/year
- Supports planning, construction, TA, emergency drinking water
- Focus: Small, rural, disadvantaged communities

### Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF)

- Low-interest loans
- Principal forgiveness for disadvantaged communities
- Interest rate: Half of GO Bond rate
- Term: Up to 30 years

### Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF)

- Wastewater treatment financing
- Similar terms to DWSRF

### Proposition Funding

**Proposition 68:**
- $4.058 billion for water quality, supply, infrastructure

**Proposition 4:**
- $180 million toward safe drinking water and wastewater projects

**Proposition 1:**
- $7.545 billion for water projects
- Emphasis on TA for small disadvantaged communities

### Small Community Drought Relief Program

- **Amount:** $200 million
- **Purpose:** Resiliency measures and infrastructure improvements
- **Eligibility:** Small water suppliers, rural communities with drought impacts

**Example: West Goshen Consolidation:**
- Community Water Center helped secure $3.45 million
- Cal Water extended service to 44 customers
- Private wells had failed or become contaminated

## Accessing Technical Assistance: Step-by-Step

### Direct Provider Contact

1. Visit provider website
2. Submit online assistance request form
3. Provide water system details, challenges, assistance needs
4. Provider reviews and prioritizes based on capacity and need
5. Initial assessment and scoping meeting
6. Tailored on-site technical assistance delivery
7. Ongoing relationship for continued support

### State-Administered Programs

1. Submit online Technical Assistance Request Form to State Water Board
2. Division of Financial Assistance reviews and prioritizes
3. Contracted TA providers assigned based on system needs

### Training Programs

RCAC and CRWA publish training calendars:
- Community members and operators can attend without individual requests
- Provides continuing education credits for operator certification
- Flexible scheduling (online and in-person options)

### Important Notes

- Demand exceeds capacity - prioritization is necessary
- Inquire about timelines when submitting requests
- Multiple providers may serve your region
- TA often positions communities for capital funding access
- Planning and feasibility work qualifies for SAFER funding
