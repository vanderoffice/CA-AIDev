# Water-Focused Foundations

This document covers philanthropic foundations dedicated specifically to water issues, with details on their California programs, funding mechanisms, and application processes.

## Water Foundation

The Water Foundation is the only intermediary foundation in the United States solely focused on water and its interconnected impacts, serving as a strategic bridge between donors and on-the-ground organizations.

### Mission and Focus

The Water Foundation addresses water challenges across multiple interconnected areas:
- Drinking water access and affordability
- Clean water protection
- Groundwater sustainability
- Ecosystem health and river restoration
- Flooding and drought resilience
- Urban green infrastructure
- Water equity advancement

### California Investment

California represents the Water Foundation's most substantially funded region:
- **284 grants** totaling **$17,041,640** (2020-2024)
- Approximately 71% of western regional grantmaking by dollar amount
- Total national investment: nearly $43 million to over 210 organizations

### Key California Policy Achievements Supported

- California Sustainable Groundwater Management Act (SGMA)
- California Open and Transparent Water Data Act
- Central Valley Flood Protection Plan
- Measure W (Safe Clean Water LA) - secured sustainable funding for green stormwater infrastructure
- California Safe and Affordable Drinking Water Fund (SAFER)
- California Consolidation of At-Risk Water Districts initiative

### Grant Process

**Critical:** The Water Foundation does not accept unsolicited proposals. Organizations are identified and invited by foundation staff or recommended through partner networks.

For invited grantees, the foundation provides:
- Multiyear commitments
- Support beyond the grant (networking, amplification)
- Capacity building and network development

## Pisces Foundation

The Pisces Foundation advances solutions to natural resource challenges through its distinctive "One Water" urban water strategy.

### Mission and Approach

The foundation pursues integrated and equitable water management, recognizing that:
- Water is linked to climate, ecosystems, human health, and social equity
- Solutions must use a systems lens considering interconnectivity
- One Water seeks to solve multiple challenges simultaneously
- Solutions are as diverse as the communities they serve

### Investment Record

- Over **$6 million** invested directly in organizations since 2014
- Supported One Water policies and programs in **21 cities** nationwide
- 2022 Urban Water RFP: **$780,000** to eight organizations across six cities

### 2022 Urban Water RFP Grantees

Recent grants demonstrate the foundation's focus on urban transformation:

| Organization | Location | Focus Area |
|-------------|----------|------------|
| Centro por la Justicia—Southwest Workers Union | San Antonio | Community-based One Water solutions |
| Groundwork San Diego—Chollas Creek | San Diego | Equitable green stormwater infrastructure |
| Miami Waterkeeper | Miami | One Water Action Plan for 34 municipalities |
| National Wildlife Federation/Bayou City Waterkeeper | Houston | First-ever One Water Plan |
| Verde and Oregon Water Futures | Portland | Tribal consultation in water/land planning |
| Water Collaborative of Greater New Orleans | New Orleans | Equitable stormwater funding |

### Eligibility Requirements

- U.S.-based 501(c)(3) public charities
- Consortia eligible (at least one 501(c)(3) anchor)
- Primary grantee must have smart water expertise
- Must include grassroots community-based organization involvement
- Meaningful community engagement required
- Project city/county must have population of at least 200,000

### Grant Parameters

- Typical awards: **$95,000-$125,000** per year
- Multi-year funding available (1-3 years)
- Additional cohort support funding ($150,000 for convenings/stipends)

### Application Process

1. Submit concept note (max 1,000 words) via email
2. Include: organization name, grant purpose, background, partners, context, goals, strategy, budget, funding partners
3. Submit as single PDF to: **water@piscesfoundation.org**
4. Selected applicants invited to submit full proposals

## Spring Point Partners

Spring Point Partners champions community-driven change through an equity-centered approach to water sustainability.

### Mission and Approach

- Invests in transformational leaders, networks, and solutions
- Uses equity and social justice as guideposts for all decisions
- Integrates grant-making, impact investing, and professional development
- Matches support tools to each partner's specific needs

### Delta Program: Water Leadership Focus

The Delta program improves access to safe and affordable water by:
- Expanding community representation in utility leadership
- Ensuring water service decision-makers reflect communities served
- Identifying and elevating diverse leaders
- Supporting the next generation of municipal water leaders

### Key Partners in Delta Network

- US Water Alliance
- Urban Sustainability Directors Network
- Green Infrastructure Leadership Exchange
- Rural Community Assistance Partnership
- WaterNow Alliance
- Black Water Professionals Alliance
- Texas Water Foundation
- Environmental Policy Innovation Center

### California Involvement

While headquartered in Philadelphia, Spring Point Partners maintains California involvement through:
- Broader work on water sustainability and utility leadership transformation
- Focus on inclusive infrastructure workforce development
- Collaboration with Funders Network on workforce equity

### Grant Process

**Critical:** Spring Point Partners does not accept unsolicited proposals, requests, or appeals. The organization actively seeks out compatible partners aligned with their values and goals.

For selected partners, support may include:
- Grant funding
- Impact investment capital
- Capacity building support
- Professional development opportunities
- Custom combinations based on demonstrated needs

## S.D. Bechtel, Jr. Foundation (Legacy - Concluded 2020)

The S.D. Bechtel, Jr. Foundation concluded operations in 2020 after contributing over $130 million in water program funds between 2009-2020.

### Historical Impact

The foundation envisioned a California that manages, stewards, and conserves its water and land for a resilient environment and healthy communities. Key contributions include:

- Over **$130 million** in program funds for sustainable water management
- Community Foundation Water Initiative (CFWI) launched in 2015
- Support for five regional community foundations addressing water
- Commissioned statewide report on land use, water, and equity integration

### Community Foundation Water Initiative (CFWI)

Brought together five regional community foundations:
1. San Francisco Foundation
2. Silicon Valley Community Foundation
3. Central Valley Community Foundation
4. California Community Foundation (Los Angeles)
5. The San Diego Foundation

### Key Lessons from CFWI

The foundation documented important lessons for future water philanthropy:
- Flexibility allows integration of water into existing work
- Multi-year investment allows initiatives to reach full potential
- Peer learning creates valuable knowledge sharing
- Initiative connections expand reach through collaboration
- Dedicated funding for coordination enables group learning

### Enduring Infrastructure

Though the foundation is closed, its legacy continues through:
- Strong organizations and partnerships carrying work forward
- Community foundations with water expertise
- Policy achievements supporting sustainable water management
- Documentation of lessons learned for future funders

## Resources Legacy Fund

Resources Legacy Fund (RLF) operates as architects of environmental progress through trusted partnerships and creative campaigns.

### Mission and Approach

RLF addresses three interconnected crises:
- Climate change
- Biodiversity loss
- Social inequities

The organization combines:
- Fiscal sponsorship of high-impact projects
- Campaign design and implementation
- Management of strategic grantmaking initiatives

### Water-Related Work

Water initiatives emerge from recognition that water challenges intersect with:
- Conservation goals (protecting water-dependent ecosystems)
- Climate resilience (adaptation and nature-based solutions)
- Environmental justice (community resilience to water impacts)

### Policy Achievements

RLF has contributed to:
- Over **$30 billion** in public funding for climate, water, and nature
- California's largest-ever climate bond (drought/flood protection)
- Hawaii's first "green fee" generating $100M/year for climate resilience

### Fiscally Sponsored Projects

Water-relevant projects include:
- Tribal Stewardship and Sovereignty Network
- National Ocean Protection Coalition
- Climate Mayors
- Center for Western Priorities

### Affiliated Organizations

- **Fund for a Better Future** (501(c)(4)): Expanded advocacy tools including lobbying and ballot measures
- **Shared Ascent Fund** (501(c)(3)): Voting rights, economic prosperity, health outcomes

### Financial Scale

- 2023 program expenses: approximately **$35.4 million**
- Hundreds of grants annually
- Focus on strategic partnerships rather than open applications

## Strategic Recommendations for Accessing Foundation Funding

### Understanding Foundation Fit

| Foundation | Best For | Application Type |
|-----------|----------|------------------|
| Water Foundation | Systems-level water work, California focus | Invitation only |
| Pisces Foundation | Urban One Water transformation, major metros | Open RFP (periodic) |
| Spring Point Partners | Utility leadership, workforce equity | Invitation only |
| Resources Legacy Fund | Policy campaigns, fiscal sponsorship | Strategic partnerships |

### Building Visibility

For foundations without open applications:
1. Build impact and visibility within monitored networks
2. Attend convenings and conferences where foundation staff participate
3. Connect with existing grantees for potential introductions
4. Demonstrate alignment with foundation values through public work
5. Engage with foundation communications and shared learnings

### Application Strength Factors

Successful applications typically demonstrate:
- Clear theory of change
- Community engagement and voice in planning
- Equity outcomes explicitly addressed
- Systems-level thinking
- Potential for replication or scaling
- Strong partnerships across sectors
