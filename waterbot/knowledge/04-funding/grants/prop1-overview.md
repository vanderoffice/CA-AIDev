# California Proposition 1 (2014 Water Bond)

Proposition 1, the Water Quality, Supply, and Infrastructure Improvement Act of 2014, authorized $7.545 billion in general obligation bonds for California's most comprehensive water infrastructure investment. Approved by voters with a 67-33 margin in November 2014, the bond has funded over 673 projects statewide.

## Bond Allocation by Chapter

| Chapter | Purpose | Amount | Committed |
|---------|---------|--------|-----------|
| Chapter 5 | Clean, safe, reliable drinking water | $520M | $508.5M |
| Chapter 6 | Rivers, lakes, streams, watersheds | $1.495B | $1.453B |
| Chapter 7 | Regional water security, climate | $810M | $789.9M |
| Chapter 8 | Water storage and drought preparedness | $2.7B | $2.646B |
| Chapter 9 | Water recycling | $725M | $706.4M |
| Chapter 10 | Groundwater sustainability | $900M | $798.1M |
| Chapter 11 | Flood management | $395M | $387.1M |

As of 2025, $7.519 billion (99.7%) has been committed, with only $25.7 million remaining unallocated.

## Administering Agencies

### State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB)

SWRCB administers Proposition 1 funds through the Division of Financial Assistance for:

- **Drinking Water Programs**: $520M for treatment facilities, distribution systems, source capacity, and contaminated water remediation
- **Groundwater Sustainability**: $670M to prevent and clean up groundwater contamination affecting drinking water
- **Water Recycling**: $725M for recycled water treatment, storage, distribution, and groundwater recharge
- **Stormwater Management**: $200M through Storm Water Grant Program for multi-benefit projects (69 projects funded)
- **Small Community Wastewater**: $260M through Clean Water State Revolving Fund

### Department of Water Resources (DWR)

- **IRWM Grants**: $510M across 12 funding areas for integrated regional water management
- **Water Storage Investment Program**: $2.7B (largest allocation) for public benefit storage projects
- **Flood Management**: $395M for Delta levees and statewide flood control
- **Agricultural Water Efficiency**: $3M pilot program (awarded to North San Joaquin WCD)

### California Water Commission

Administers the Water Storage Investment Program (WSIP) with eight active projects designed to add 2.5 million acre-feet of storage capacity:

| Project | WSIP Allocation |
|---------|-----------------|
| Sites Project | $1.094B |
| Harvest Water Program | $364.8M |
| Chino Basin Conjunctive Use | $269.1M |
| Willow Springs Water Bank | $128.3M |
| Kern Fan Groundwater Storage | $111.4M |

### Wildlife Conservation Board

- **Stream Flow Enhancement**: $200M for projects benefiting fish and wildlife
- Funded 128 projects totaling $134M from 2016-2021

### California Department of Fish and Wildlife

- **Watershed Restoration Grants**: Wildfire recovery, meadow protection, anadromous fish habitat
- **Delta Ecosystem Restoration**: Water quality and ecosystem improvement projects

## Eligibility and Application

### Eligible Applicants
- Local public agencies (cities, counties, water districts)
- 501(c)(3) nonprofit organizations
- Federally recognized Indian tribes
- State-listed California tribes
- Mutual water companies
- Public utilities

### Application Portal
Most applications submitted through **Financial Assistance Application Submittal Tool (FAAST)**:
- Website: faast.waterboards.ca.gov
- Help Desk: 1-866-434-1083
- Email: FAAST_ADMIN@waterboards.ca.gov

### Matching Requirements
- Standard: At least 50% local cost share
- Disadvantaged communities (MHI <80% statewide): Reduced or waived
- Severely disadvantaged communities (MHI <60% statewide): Full grants available

## Disadvantaged Community Prioritization

Proposition 1 explicitly prioritizes disadvantaged communities:
- 172 grants focused on communities lacking safe drinking water
- IRWM second round: $93M (46%) directed to DAC/tribal projects
- Principal forgiveness and grants replace loans for SDACs

## Current Status (2024-2026)

- **Fund Commitment**: 99.7% complete ($7.519B of $7.545B)
- **Implementation Phase**: Focus on project completion, not new allocations
- **Water Storage**: Largest projects (Sites Reservoir) continue permitting through 2026
- **Remaining Activity**: Construction completion, operational establishment
