# Emergency and Drought Water Funding Programs

California operates multiple funding programs to provide immediate relief during water emergencies while building long-term water security. These programs prioritize disadvantaged communities, small water systems, and rural areas.

## Urgent Drinking Water Needs Program (UDWN)

### Overview
California's primary mechanism for immediate interim water supplies during emergencies caused by drought, contamination, or other disasters.

### Funding Source
Cleanup and Abatement Account (CAA) - funded by court judgments and administrative civil liabilities from water quality violations.

### Authorization
Water Code Section 13442 - allows State Water Board to address urgent drinking water needs.

### Eligible Applicants
- Public agencies (counties, cities)
- Tribal governments on CA Tribal Consultation List serving DACs
- Nonprofit organizations serving DACs
- Community water systems serving DACs

### Eligible Projects

**Interim Water Supplies**:
| Type | Maximum Allocation |
|------|-------------------|
| Bottled water (residential) | 60 gallons/household/month |
| Bottled water (schools) | 0.25 gallons/student-staff/day |
| Hauled water | 50 gallons/person/day |
| Water vending machines | Case-by-case |

**Emergency Infrastructure**:
- Well rehabilitation or replacement
- Emergency interties to adjacent systems
- Service line extensions
- Consolidation projects
- Treatment systems
- Equipment purchase/rental

**Construction Projects** (if <$500K):
- Serving small DACs or low-income households
- Addressing urgent compliance issues
- CEQA complete or exempt

### Approval Authority
- ≤$500K: DFA Deputy Director or Executive Director
- >$500K or controversial: Full State Water Board

### Application
- **Continuous basis** - no deadlines
- Email: DFA-CAA@waterboards.ca.gov
- Or submit through FAAST
- Approval: 2-8 weeks depending on complexity

### Reimbursement Process
- Costs reimbursed after incurrence (not advance funding)
- Grant agreement execution required before payment
- Submit reimbursement requests with documentation

## Safe and Affordable Funding for Equity and Resilience (SAFER)

### Overview
Comprehensive program integrating emergency response, interim solutions, technical assistance, and long-term infrastructure development. Established by SB 200 (2019).

### Funding
- **Annual**: $130M from Safe and Affordable Drinking Water Fund
- **Supplemental**: DWSRF principal forgiveness, Prop 1, Prop 68

### Implementation Status (as of November 2024)
- Total assistance: $237.5M benefiting priority populations
- FY 2024: $76.4M (86% benefiting low-income communities)
- Projects funded: 119+

### Funding Priorities (in order)
1. Emergency/urgent funding needs
2. Water systems out of compliance or at-risk
3. Consolidation acceleration
4. Technical assistance for planning
5. Interim solutions for state small water systems and domestic wells
6. Racial equity distribution

### Eligible Applicants
- Public agencies and water utilities
- Nonprofits and mutual water companies
- Tribal governments
- State Water Board-appointed administrators
- Technical assistance providers
- Individual property owners (domestic wells)
- Groundwater sustainability agencies

### Eligible Projects
- Interim drinking water supplies
- Emergency repairs and improvements
- Technical assistance and capacity building
- Point-of-use/point-of-entry treatment (max 3 years)
- Operations and maintenance grants
- Long-term infrastructure development

### Application
- Continuous, year-round
- Pre-application through FAAST
- Staff guidance provided throughout process

## Small Community Drought Relief Program

### Funding
$305 million total:
- $200M: Substantive drought relief
- $20M: Household water storage tanks
- $5M: County drought resilience planning

### Eligibility
Communities NOT served by urban water suppliers (>3,000 customers or >3,000 AFY).

### Eligible Applicants
- Public agencies (counties, cities, special districts)
- Public utilities
- Colleges and universities
- Mutual water companies
- Nonprofit organizations
- Federally recognized tribes
- State-listed California tribes

### Eligible Projects

**Temporary Interim Solutions**:
- Hauled water delivery
- Temporary community water tanks
- Bottled water distribution
- Water vending machines

**Long-Term Infrastructure**:
- Water system storage improvements
- Infrastructure replacement (reducing losses)
- Backup power sources
- Well rehabilitation
- New well drilling
- Water recycling
- Conservation improvements
- System consolidation

### Application Process
- **First-come, first-served** (no competition)
- Email: SmallCommunityDrought@water.ca.gov
- Or mail to DWR Division of Regional Assistance
- Continuous review until funding depleted

## DWSRF and Long-Term Infrastructure

### Drinking Water State Revolving Fund
Primary mechanism for lasting infrastructure improvements.

**FY 2025-26 Targets**:
- Loan commitments: $300M
- Grant/Principal Forgiveness: ~$1B

**Loan Terms**:
- Interest: Half of GO bond rate (2-3%)
- Term: Up to 30 years

**Grant Eligibility**:
- Small DAC, SDAC
- Expanded small DAC/SDAC
- Small non-DAC with MHI <150% statewide

### Lead Service Line Replacement
**Federal Funding**: $15B nationally
**California Received**: $250M (FY22) + $29M (FY23)

| Community Type | Terms |
|----------------|-------|
| DAC systems | Up to 100% principal forgiveness |
| Non-DAC | 0% interest loans |

## Federal Programs

### USDA Emergency Community Water Assistance Grants (ECWAG)

**Purpose**: Help rural communities prepare for or recover from water emergencies.

**Eligible Emergencies** (no federal disaster declaration required):
- Drought or flood
- Earthquake, tornado, hurricane
- Disease outbreaks
- Chemical spills

**Grant Amounts**:
| Type | Maximum |
|------|---------|
| Water transmission line grants | $150,000 |
| Water source grants | $1,000,000 |

**Eligibility**:
- Rural areas (population ≤10,000)
- Tribal lands in rural areas
- Colonias
- MHI below state non-metropolitan median

**Application**: Year-round through RD APPLY or local USDA Rural Development offices

### Tribal Water Security

**Coordinating Agencies**:
- Indian Health Service (rapid short-term assistance)
- USDA (extended emergency water supply)
- State Water Board Division of Financial Assistance
- County Office of Emergency Services
- Cal OES Tribal Advisor

**California Programs for Tribes**:
- UDWN (serving DACs)
- SAFER program
- Small Community Drought Relief
- Urban Drought Relief (set-asides)

## Eligibility Definitions

### Disadvantaged Community (DAC)
Annual median household income <80% statewide MHI (~$56,982)

### Severely Disadvantaged Community (SDAC)
Annual median household income <60% statewide MHI (~$43,737)

### Small Community
- SCDW: ≤10,000 population OR ≤3,300 connections
- SCWW: <20,000 population
- Drought Relief: Not served by urban supplier (>3,000 customers)

### Urban Water Supplier
>3,000 customers OR >3,000 AFY (Water Code 10617)

## Key Points

1. **Emergency programs operate continuously** - no deadlines
2. **DAC/SDAC status unlocks additional funding** - verify community qualification
3. **Reimbursement model** - plan for working capital needs
4. **Multiple programs can layer** - combine for comprehensive solutions
5. **Federal IIJA funding available through 2026** - time-limited opportunity
6. **Prop 4 implementation begins Fall 2025** - new resources coming
