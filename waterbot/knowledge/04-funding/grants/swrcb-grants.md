# State Water Resources Control Board Grant Programs

The State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) Division of Financial Assistance administers California's comprehensive water infrastructure funding portfolio, coordinating state and federal funding sources to support water quality, supply, and infrastructure projects.

## Water Recycling Funding Program (WRFP)

### Purpose
Promotes beneficial use of treated municipal wastewater to augment California's fresh water supplies.

### Funding Sources

| Source | Amount | Status |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Proposition 1 (2014) | $625M | Largest current source |
| Proposition 68 (2018) | $72M | Loans and grants |
| Proposition 13 (2000) | Limited | Mostly committed |
| Clean Water SRF | Ongoing | Low-interest loans |

### FY 2025-26 Availability
- **Construction Grants**: $161.9M
- **Planning Grants**: $18.6M
- **Standard Maximum**: $15M construction / $300K planning

### Large-Scale Projects
Projects delivering 10,000+ acre-feet per year (AFY):
- Eligible for $15M per phase
- No user agreements required
- May apply for multiple phases

### Eligible Applicants
- Local public agencies
- 501(c)(3) nonprofits
- Public utilities
- Federally and non-federally recognized tribes
- Mutual water companies

### Eligible Projects
- Recycled water treatment facilities
- Storage, distribution, pumping infrastructure
- Groundwater recharge using recycled water
- Indirect potable reuse
- Surface water augmentation

### Match Requirement
- Standard: 50% local cost share
- Reduced for disadvantaged communities

### Application
- Continuous basis (no deadlines)
- Submit through FAAST
- Select "Water Recycling Funding Program" application

## Small Community Grant Programs

### Office of Sustainable Water Solutions
Established 2015 (AB 92) to promote permanent drinking water and wastewater solutions for small, disadvantaged communities.

### Small Community Wastewater Grant Fund

**Funding**: $260M from Proposition 1 (Section 79723)
- Annual appropriation: $8M
- Administered through Clean Water SRF

**Eligibility**:
- Population <20,000
- Disadvantaged community status prioritized

**Eligible Projects**:
- Wastewater treatment plants
- Sewer collectors and interceptors
- Septic-to-sewer conversions
- Regionalization/consolidation
- Stormwater treatment
- Water reclamation facilities

### Small Community Drinking Water (SCDW) Program

**Eligibility**:
- Population ≤10,000 OR ≤3,300 service connections
- DAC: MHI <80% statewide
- SDAC: MHI <60% statewide

**Eligible Projects**:
- Treatment systems (filtration, softening, disinfection)
- Distribution systems (pipes, pump stations, tanks)
- Interconnections and consolidations
- Pipeline extensions
- Water source development
- Water meters

**Financing Terms**:
- Interest rate: 0% available
- Repayment: Up to 40 years or useful life

**Application**: Continuous through FAAST

**Status**: **CLOSED** - All funding committed (September 2025)

**Rounds Completed**:
- Round 1: Fall 2018
- Round 2: Fall 2020
- Round 3: September 2025

**Ongoing Component**: SDAC drinking water and wastewater projects continue through DWSRF/CWSRF

### Proposition 68 Groundwater Treatment

**Funding**: $74M ($16M SDAC set-aside)
**Status**: **CLOSED** - No further solicitations planned

**Remaining Activity**: Drinking Water O&M funding extended timeline for previously funded projects

## Emerging Contaminants and PFAS Funding

### Federal IIJA Funding
- **California Allocation**: $700M+ for emerging contaminants
- **No match required** for EC-SDC grants
- **Annual Appropriation**: $1B nationally (FY 2022-2026)

### DWSRF Emerging Contaminant Funding
- **Maximum Principal Forgiveness**: $25M per water system
- **DAC/Small Systems (<25,000)**: No grant limit

### Eligible Activities
- Drinking water treatment for emerging contaminants
- Technical assistance for contamination evaluation
- Household water quality testing
- Local contractor training
- PFAS treatment infrastructure

### Application
- Continuous through existing DWSRF process
- Submit via FAAST

## State Revolving Funds

### Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF)

**Purpose**: Low-cost financing for water quality projects

**FY 2025-26 Availability**:
- Grant/Principal Forgiveness: $455.7M for Small Community Wastewater
- IIJA Supplemental: $176M (49% as principal forgiveness)

**Eligible Projects**:
- Wastewater treatment plants
- Sewer systems
- Nonpoint source projects
- Estuary projects

**Terms**:
- Interest: Half of GO bond rate
- Term: 30 years (common)

### Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF)

**Purpose**: Finance drinking water infrastructure for SDWA compliance

**FY 2025-26 Targets**:
- Loan commitments: $300M
- Grant/Principal Forgiveness: ~$1B available

**Eligible Systems**:
- Public community water systems
- Private community water systems
- Nonprofit non-community systems

**Eligible Projects**:
- Treatment systems
- Distribution systems
- Interconnections/consolidations
- Water sources and storage

**Grant Eligibility** (Principal Forgiveness):
- Small DAC
- Small SDAC
- Expanded small DAC/SDAC
- Small non-DAC with MHI <150% statewide

### Lead Service Line Replacement

**Federal Funding**: $15B nationally through DWSRF
**California Received**:
- FY 2022: $250M
- FY 2023: $29M

**Eligibility**:
- Community water systems
- Nonprofit non-community systems
- DAC systems: Up to 100% principal forgiveness
- Non-DAC: 0% interest loans

## Proposition 68 Regionwide Grants

### Funding
Part of $4B California Drought, Water, Parks, Climate Bond (2018)

### Eligible Applicants
- State and federal agencies
- Tribal entities (federal/state recognized)
- Local public agencies
- 501(c)(3) nonprofits
- Joint Powers Authorities

### Project Goals
- Public open space improvement
- Water quality and supply
- Species and habitat restoration

### Application Process
1. **Concept Proposal**: Initial review by RMC staff
2. **Full Application**: Invited applicants only
3. **Preference**: Projects with matching funding

## Application Portal

### FAAST (Financial Assistance Application Submittal Tool)
- **Website**: faast.waterboards.ca.gov
- **Authentication**: WBKey account required (as of July 2024)
- **Help Desk**: 1-866-434-1083
- **Email**: FAAST_ADMIN@waterboards.ca.gov

### Application Packages
Most applications include four components:
1. General Information
2. Technical
3. Environmental
4. Financial Security

### Environmental Requirements
DWSRF projects must comply with:
- California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA)
- Section 106 NHPA (Historic Preservation)
- Section 7 Federal ESA
- Federal Clean Air Act
