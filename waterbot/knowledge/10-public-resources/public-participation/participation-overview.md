# Public Participation in Water Board Proceedings

## Overview

The California Water Boards are committed to engaging impacted communities, stakeholders, California Native American Tribes, and the public in decision-making processes. Fair treatment, meaningful involvement, and social inclusion are core principles regardless of race, color, national origin, sexual orientation, or income.

## Types of Participation Opportunities

### Written Comments

- Comment on public documents noticed for comment
- Submit input on basin plan amendments, draft permits, enforcement orders
- Comment on CEQA documents and site cleanup plans

### Board Meetings

- Attend upcoming Board meetings (publicly noticed, open to public)
- Provide oral testimony on agenda items
- Submit speaker request cards to address the Board

### Email Notifications

- Sign up for email updates on specific topics
- Receive notice of upcoming proceedings

## State Water Board vs. Regional Board Processes

### State Water Board Focus

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Statewide standards | Water quality standards affecting all regions |
| General permits | Permits covering categories of dischargers statewide |
| Basin plan approval | Review and approval of regional basin plans |
| Policy development | Statewide policies and guidelines |
| Water rights | Sole authority over water rights matters |

### Regional Board Focus

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Individual permits | NPDES permits, WDRs for specific facilities |
| Basin plan development | Region-specific water quality objectives |
| Enforcement actions | Actions against specific dischargers |
| Local monitoring | Water quality monitoring in regional watersheds |

## Bagley-Keene Open Meeting Act

The Bagley-Keene Act (Government Code Section 11110 et seq.) governs Water Board meetings and establishes three duties:

1. **Timely notice**: At least one week advance notice of meetings
2. **Public comment opportunity**: Allow public to comment on agenda items
3. **Open sessions**: Conduct meetings openly except where closed session is authorized

### Notice Requirements

- **Mailed notice**: At least 10 days before meeting
- **Agenda content**: Date, time, location, and description of each item
- **Distribution**: To all directly affected persons and those requesting notice

### Meeting Format

All meetings include:

- Agenda listing all items to be considered
- Description of each item with proposed action
- Opportunity for public comment

### Teleconference Meetings

When the Board meets via teleconference:

- Agenda posted at least 10 days in advance
- At least one staff member present at physical location
- Public may attend physically or remotely
- All votes publicly reported

## Environmental Justice Principles

The Water Boards promote environmental justice through:

- Fair treatment of all people in environmental decisions
- Meaningful involvement regardless of race, color, national origin, or income
- Community-led solutions
- Recognition of historical inequities
- Explicit attention to current disparities in environmental protection

## Tribal Affairs

The Office of Public Engagement, Equity, and Tribal Affairs coordinates the Water Boards Tribal Affairs Program to ensure:

- Meaningful engagement with California Native American Tribes
- Recognition of tribal sovereignty
- Consultation on matters affecting tribal interests
- Protection of treaty rights
