# Commenting on Environmental Documents

## Comment Period Timelines

| Document Type | Minimum Review Period |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Negative Declaration | 20 days (30 days recommended) |
| Mitigated Negative Declaration | 20 days (30 days recommended) |
| Environmental Impact Report | 45 days |
| Draft EIR (state-circulated) | 45 days |
| Basin Plan amendments | 30+ days before State Board approval |

## Finding Current Comment Periods

### State Water Board

[Public Comments Page](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/public_notices/comments/)

- Lists all documents currently open for comment
- Specifies topic, deadline, and document links
- Includes basin plan amendments, permits, CEQA documents

### State Clearinghouse

[CEQAnet](https://ceqanet.lci.ca.gov)

- Search for documents by "End Review Periods"
- Browse by region or year
- Access full document text (post-March 2019)

### Regional Water Boards

- Check individual Regional Board websites
- Look under "Public Notices" section
- Central Valley example: [Public Notices](https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/centralvalley/public_notices/)

**Regional Board comments:**
- Submit to address specified in notice
- Typically due by 5:00 PM on deadline date

### Effective Comments

1. **Identify yourself and your interest**
2. **Be specific**: Reference page numbers, sections, language
3. **Explain your reasoning**: Why you agree or disagree
4. **Offer alternatives**: Specific language changes, mitigation measures
5. **Meet the deadline**: Late comments may not be considered

## CEQA Scoping Comments

### When to Comment

During the Notice of Preparation (NOP) period for EIRs:

- Typically 30 days
- Before draft EIR is prepared
- Opportunity to influence scope of analysis

### What to Address

- Environmental issues to analyze
- Alternatives to consider
- Level of analysis appropriate for impacts
- Resources or information relevant to review

### How to Submit

- Written comments to lead agency
- Attend public scoping meetings (if held)
- Include name, organization, contact information

## Comment on Draft Documents

### Negative Declarations and MNDs

Focus comments on:

- Whether impacts were correctly characterized
- Missing environmental impacts
- Adequacy of proposed mitigation
- Whether project qualifies for negative declaration (vs. EIR)

### Environmental Impact Reports

Focus comments on:

- Accuracy of environmental baseline
- Significance of identified impacts
- Effectiveness of proposed mitigation
- Range of alternatives considered
- Adequacy of cumulative impact analysis

## Response to Comments Process

### Agency Obligations

The lead agency must:

- Consider all public comments received
- Prepare written responses to significant comments
- Address substantive environmental issues raised
- Explain how input was considered

### What Happens After Comments

**For Negative Declarations:**
1. Lead agency considers comments
2. May adopt as proposed
3. May adopt mitigated ND in response
4. May determine EIR is needed

**For Environmental Impact Reports:**
1. Responses prepared to all comments
2. Final EIR includes draft + comments + responses
3. EIR must be certified before project approval

## Requesting Copies of Other Comments

To see what others have submitted:

1. Email: commentletters@waterboards.ca.gov
2. Identify the item (date, agenda number, topic)
3. Clerk will send link to access all comments

## Comment on Permits and Orders

### Draft NPDES Permits and WDRs

- 30-day comment period typical
- Comment on specific permit conditions
- Address monitoring requirements, effluent limits
- Cite applicable regulations

### Tentative Orders

- Regional Boards issue tentative orders for public review
- Comments due before board meeting
- Board considers comments at adoption hearing

## Tips for Different Document Types

### Basin Plan Amendments

- Comment on beneficial use designations
- Address water quality objectives
- Discuss implementation programs
- Consider economic and feasibility factors

### Water Rights CEQA Documents

- Comment on environmental impacts of proposed diversion
- Address effects on instream flows
- Consider cumulative impacts with other diversions

### Funding Project CEQA

- Comment on project-specific impacts
- Address alternatives analysis
- Consider mitigation adequacy

## Administrative Record

Comments become part of the official administrative record:

- May be cited in any legal challenge
- Available for public review
- Must be addressed in response-to-comments
