# Hearings and Workshops

## Types of Proceedings

### Uncontested (Consent) Items

Routine, non-controversial matters where all parties consent to staff recommendations.

**Procedure:**
1. Chair recognizes any late revisions
2. Motion and vote without discussion
3. If questions arise, item moved to contested calendar

### Informational Items

Presented for discussion without formal board action.

**Characteristics:**
- Staff presentations
- No voting required
- Public comments for clarification only (not testimonial/argumentative)

### Quasi-Legislative Matters

Rulemaking and policy proceedings.

**Examples:**
- Basin plan amendments
- Statewide policies
- General permits

**Procedure:**
- Staff presentation
- Public comments
- Board deliberation and vote

### Adjudicative (Contested) Hearings

Formal quasi-judicial proceedings with disputed issues.

**Examples:**
- Permit applications with unresolved protests
- Water rights disputes
- Enforcement actions
- Administrative civil liability cases

## Water Rights Hearings

### Purpose

Develop adequate administrative records for State Water Board decisions on:

- Applications to appropriate water
- Petitions to change existing permits/licenses
- Applications for extensions of time
- Complaints alleging waste and unreasonable use
- Public trust matters

### Triggering Events

Hearings required when:

- Protests not resolved through other means
- Petitions have unresolved objections
- Cancellation/revocation requests
- Complaints filed

### Hearing Officers

- State Water Board members conduct hearings
- One member serves as hearing officer
- Hearing team includes Division of Water Rights staff, attorneys, specialists

### Participant Categories

**Designated Parties:**
- Water right applicant/petitioner
- Persons who filed unresolved protests
- Persons who filed objections to temporary changes
- Persons who filed complaints

**Rights of designated parties:**
- Call and examine witnesses
- Introduce exhibits
- Cross-examine opposing witnesses
- Subject to cross-examination themselves

**Interested Persons:**
- May present policy statements or comments
- NOT testimonial or argumentative
- Not entitled to service of documents
- Cannot participate in cross-examination

### Hearing Process

1. **Notice of Hearing** - Specifies scope, deadlines, rules
2. **Pre-hearing disclosures** - Witness lists, testimony subjects, expert qualifications
3. **Evidentiary hearing** - Testimony, exhibits, cross-examination
4. **Stenographic record** - Court reporters document proceedings
5. **Draft decision** - Staff prepares analysis
6. **Board consideration** - Final decision or order

### Ex Parte Rules

Once proceeding is pending:
- No ex parte communications with board members
- Violation can invalidate decision

### Reconsideration

- Petition within 30 days of adoption
- Causes: New evidence, procedural irregularities
- Board may grant or deny, hold additional hearings

## Workshop Procedures

### Purpose

- Policy discussions without formal action
- Educational presentations
- Information gathering
- Preliminary deliberations

### Format

- Less formal than regular meetings
- More flexible discussion
- May include external presenters
- No voting on substantive matters

## Public Hearing Participation

### For Contested Items

**Designated Parties:**
1. File for party status by deadline in notice
2. Disclose witnesses and testimony subjects
3. Submit written testimony in advance
4. Present evidence at hearing
5. Cross-examine opposing witnesses

**Interested Persons:**
1. Submit written comments by deadline
2. Request time to speak
3. Provide clarification, not testimonial statements

### Time Limits

| Speaker Type | Typical Allocation |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Designated parties | ~10 minutes direct |
| Interested persons | ~3 minutes |

Timer may be used; honor limits to allow all speakers.

### Written Submissions

- Due by dates in notice
- Comments received by deadline included in record
- Late submissions may be excluded unless justification provided

### Presentation Materials

- Coordinate slides/media with staff in advance
- Must be consistent with oral testimony
- PowerPoint not part of formal record unless shown during meeting

## Administrative Civil Liability Proceedings

### Waiving Hearing

Dischargers may waive right to hearing on ACLs or Mandatory Minimum Penalties.

**If waived:** No hearing unless substantial new information emerges.

### Petitions for Review

Any person adversely affected may petition State Water Board within 30 days.

**Limitations:**
- Must raise issues/objections previously presented
- Substantive issues only
