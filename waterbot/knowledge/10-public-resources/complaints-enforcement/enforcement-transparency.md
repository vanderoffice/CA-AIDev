# Enforcement Transparency and Public Access

## Overview

California Water Boards operate under strong transparency mandates, making enforcement actions, penalty calculations, and compliance status publicly accessible. This transparency supports public participation in environmental protection and regulatory accountability.

## Legal Foundation for Transparency

### Public Records Act

The California Public Records Act (Government Code Section 6250 et seq.) establishes:
- Presumption of public access to government records
- Right to inspect and copy public documents
- Limited exemptions for specific categories
- Response timeline requirements

**Applicable to Water Board Records**:
- Enforcement orders and decisions
- Penalty calculations
- Inspection reports
- Correspondence with regulated entities
- Settlement agreements

### Open Meeting Requirements

The Bagley-Keene Open Meeting Act requires:
- Public notice of board meetings
- Public access to meeting materials
- Opportunity for public comment
- Recording of votes and decisions

**Enforcement Matters at Public Meetings**:
- ACL orders presented for board adoption
- Settlement agreements approved publicly
- Compliance status reports
- Significant enforcement updates

## Public Access to Enforcement Documents

### Accessing Board Orders

All adopted board orders are public documents:

**State Water Board Orders**:
- Available on State Board website
- Searchable by order number, date, party
- PDF format for download

**Regional Board Orders**:
- Posted on Regional Board websites
- Included in board meeting agendas/minutes
- Accessible through CIWQS

### Inspection Reports

Inspection reports document facility compliance status:

**Types of Inspections**:
- Routine compliance inspections
- Response to complaints
- Pre-enforcement inspections
- Follow-up inspections

**Obtaining Reports**:
- Request through Regional Board
- Public Records Act request if not readily available
- Some posted in CIWQS facility records

### Penalty Calculation Worksheets

Water Board penalty calculations follow published methodologies:

**Penalty Policy Components**:
- Base fine per violation
- Adjustment factors (culpability, history, cooperation)
- Economic benefit calculation
- Ability to pay considerations

**Accessing Calculations**:
- Included as attachments to ACL orders
- Available through Public Records Act
- Presented at enforcement hearings

## Public Participation in Enforcement

### Commenting on Proposed Enforcement

Many enforcement actions include public comment opportunities:

**Tentative Orders**:
- Published before final adoption
- 30-day comment period typical
- Public hearing may be requested
- Written comments considered by Board

**Significant Enforcement Actions**:
- Presented at public board meetings
- Oral comment opportunity
- Board deliberation in public session

### Petitioning Enforcement Decisions

Affected parties and interested persons can challenge decisions:

**Administrative Review**:
- Petition to State Water Board (for Regional Board actions)
- Timeline: 30 days from action
- Must demonstrate error or improper procedure

**Judicial Review**:
- After exhausting administrative remedies
- Superior court petition
- Writ of mandate procedure

### Whistleblower Protections

California law protects employees who report violations:

**Labor Code Section 1102.5**:
- Protection from retaliation
- Applies to employees who report to government agencies
- Remedies for wrongful termination

## Transparency Initiatives

### Open Data Efforts

Water Boards participate in open data initiatives:

**AB 1755 (Open and Transparent Water Data Act)**:
- Requires accessible water data
- Supports data integration
- Promotes public understanding

**CIWQS Public Access**:
- No registration required for basic access
- Downloadable datasets
- API access for developers

### Environmental Justice Integration

Enforcement transparency supports EJ goals:

**CalEnviroScreen**:
- Integrates pollution and enforcement data
- Identifies disadvantaged communities
- Informs enforcement prioritization

**Community Right to Know**:
- Accessible violation information
- Penalty assessment transparency
- Compliance tracking for local facilities

## Requesting Records

### Public Records Act Process

**Submitting Requests**:
- Written request to appropriate Water Board
- Describe records sought with reasonable specificity
- No requirement to explain purpose

**Timeline**:
- Initial response: 10 days
- Determination to disclose: 10 additional days (may be extended)
- Production: Prompt after determination

**Fees**:
- Copies: $0.10-0.25 per page (varies)
- Electronic records: May be free
- No fee for inspection only

### What May Be Exempt

Limited exemptions may apply to:
- Ongoing investigations (until concluded)
- Attorney-client privileged communications
- Trade secrets and confidential business information
- Personal privacy in certain circumstances

**Challenging Denials**:
- Request written explanation
- Appeal to agency head
- Petition superior court
