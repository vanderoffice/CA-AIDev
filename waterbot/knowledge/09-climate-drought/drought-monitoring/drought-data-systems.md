# California Drought Data Systems

## California Data Exchange Center (CDEC)

### Overview

The California Data Exchange Center is a centralized database administered by the Department of Water Resources that stores, processes, and exchanges real-time hydrologic information.

### Data Partners

CDEC aggregates data from numerous cooperating agencies:
- National Weather Service
- U.S. Army Corps of Engineers
- U.S. Bureau of Reclamation
- U.S. Geological Survey
- California Department of Fish and Wildlife
- Sacramento Municipal Utility District
- Pacific Gas and Electric Company
- East Bay Municipal Utility District
- Other federal, state, and local agencies

### Data Types Available

- Precipitation (real-time and historical)
- River forecasts
- River stages and flow rates
- Snowpack conditions
- Reservoir storage levels
- Groundwater data

### Primary Uses

- Water forecasters prepare flood and water supply forecasts
- Water supply operators schedule reservoir releases
- Anticipate available water supplies
- Support emergency response decisions

## California Water Watch

### Overview

Publicly accessible monitoring platform offering current local and statewide water conditions, updated daily as new data becomes available.

### Key Features

- Regional and neighborhood-scale data
- Comparison to historical averages
- Multiple indicator integration
- Public-facing dashboard interface

### Indicators Tracked

| Indicator | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Precipitation | Percentage of historical average |
| Temperature | Current vs. long-term normals |
| Streamflow | Compared to historical ranges |
| Snowpack | Percentage of historical average |
| Reservoir Storage | Current vs. historical averages |
| Groundwater Elevation | Trend analysis |
| Soil Moisture | Satellite-derived at 100cm depth |
| Vegetation Stress | Evaporative stress measurements |

**Access:** cww.water.ca.gov

## California Irrigation Management Information System (CIMIS)

### Overview

Operated by DWR's Water Use and Efficiency Branch, CIMIS manages 145+ weather stations throughout California collecting publicly available agricultural weather data.

### Data Collected

- Soil temperature
- Air temperature
- Relative humidity
- Precipitation
- Evapotranspiration (ET)

### Data Access Methods

- CIMIS website direct access
- Email scheduling services
- FTP sites (hourly and daily data)
- Web services via SOAP and REST protocols
- Query by weather station, zip code, coordinates, or street address

**Access:** cimis.water.ca.gov

## California Statewide Groundwater Elevation Monitoring (CASGEM)

### Overview

Established by Legislature through SB X7-6 (2009) to collect groundwater level information statewide through collaboration between local monitoring parties and DWR.

### Historical Significance

First effort to make groundwater data publicly available on a statewide basis.

### Measurement Schedule

Most monitoring wells measured twice yearly:
- Spring measurement
- Fall measurement

## SGMA Data Viewer

### Purpose

Provides access to groundwater-related datasets organized according to:
- Sustainable Groundwater Management Act requirements
- Groundwater Sustainability Plan regulations

### Uses

- Support GSP development
- Monitor GSP implementation
- Track basin sustainability indicators

## Natural Communities Commonly Associated with Groundwater (NCCAG) Dataset Viewer

### Purpose

Compiles publicly available state and federal agency datasets mapping:
- Vegetation dependent on groundwater
- Wetlands
- Springs
- Seeps

### Uses

- Assess ecological health
- Evaluate water availability for natural systems
- Support SGMA environmental considerations

### Automated Stations

## Data Integration for Drought Assessment

### Multi-Indicator Approach

Effective drought assessment requires integrating:
1. Precipitation (input)
2. Snowpack (natural storage)
3. Reservoir storage (managed storage)
4. Groundwater levels (underground storage)
5. Streamflow (availability)
6. Temperature (demand modifier)
7. Vegetation stress (impact indicator)

### Temporal Considerations

- Short-term indicators (1-3 months): Agriculture impacts
- Long-term indicators (6-60 months): Hydrological system impacts
- Real-time data: Emergency response
- Historical comparison: Context for current conditions
