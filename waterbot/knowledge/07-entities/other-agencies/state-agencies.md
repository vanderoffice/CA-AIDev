# California State Agencies with Water-Related Authority

**Structure:**
- Umbrella agency housing multiple boards and departments
- Provides coordination and policy direction
- Constituent agencies operate with statutory independence

**CalEPA Boards and Departments:**

| Entity | Primary Role |
|--------|-------------|
| State Water Resources Control Board | Water quality, water rights |
| Regional Water Quality Control Boards (9) | Regional water quality |
| Air Resources Board | Air quality |
| Department of Pesticide Regulation | Pesticide registration |
| Department of Toxic Substances Control | Hazardous waste |
| Office of Environmental Health Hazard Assessment | Risk assessment |

**Coordination Functions:**
- Policy alignment across environmental programs
- Shared resources and information systems
- Cross-media environmental issues
- CalEnviroScreen development (environmental justice mapping)

## Department of Water Resources (DWR)

### Mission and Scope

**Primary Functions:**
- Operate and maintain State Water Project
- Water supply planning and forecasting
- Flood management
- Dam safety
- Climate change adaptation

### State Water Project

**System Overview:**
- Nation's largest state-owned water and power system
- Extends 400 miles through central California
- 20+ dams and reservoirs
- Serves 27 million Californians (two-thirds of population)

**Major Facilities:**

| Facility | Capacity |
|----------|----------|
| Oroville Dam/Lake | 3,537,577 AF |
| San Luis Reservoir | 2,027,840 AF |
| Castaic Lake | 323,702 AF |
| Pyramid Lake | 171,200 AF |

**Operations:**
- Annual allocation announcements (typically December)
- Forecast Informed Reservoir Operations
- Coordinated Operations Agreement with Bureau of Reclamation
- Delta export operations

### Water Quality Monitoring

**DWR Monitoring Program:**
- Division of Operations and Maintenance oversight
- Environmental Assessment Branch management
- Parameters: nutrients, salinity, metals, pathogens
- Drinking water, recreation, and wildlife parameters

### Current Priorities (2024-2025)

**"Elevate to '28" Strategy:**
- Risk-informed strategic approach through 2028
- Climate change adaptation
- Infrastructure preservation and restoration
- Delta Conveyance Project development

**December 2024:**
- Initial 2025 SWP allocation: 5% of requested supplies
- Record summer heatwaves impacted forecasting
- Soil moisture critical to water year success

**November 2024:**
- New Incidental Take Permit from CDFW
- Covers 5 species under CESA
- 10-year operational flexibility

### Stream Gage Improvement Program

- $7 million investment
- Improved data collection
- Enhanced forecasting capabilities

## Department of Fish and Wildlife (CDFW)

### Lake and Streambed Alteration Program

**Authority:** Fish and Game Code Section 1602

**Notification Requirement:**
Any entity must notify CDFW before:
- Diverting or obstructing natural flow
- Changing bed, channel, or bank
- Using material from rivers, streams, or lakes
- Depositing material into waterways

**Applies To:**
- Perennial AND ephemeral streams
- All rivers, streams, and lakes
- Dry waterways

**Process Timeline:**

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Completeness determination | 30 days |
| Draft agreement (if needed) | 60 days after complete |
| Default if no response | Applicant may proceed |

**Agreement Requirements:**
- CEQA compliance required
- May include project modifications
- Onsite inspections possible
- Measures to reduce fish/wildlife impacts

### Incidental Take Permits

**Authority:** California Endangered Species Act (CESA)

**State Water Project ITP (November 2024):**
- Covers 5 species:
  - Delta smelt
  - Longfin smelt
  - White sturgeon
  - Winter-run Chinook salmon
  - Spring-run Chinook salmon
- Enables 10-year operation regardless of federal policy
- Science-based adaptive management

**ITP Process:**
- Analysis of proposed operations
- Measures to minimize, avoid, and mitigate impacts
- Monitoring and reporting requirements
- Adaptive management provisions

## California Coastal Commission

### Coastal Development Permits

**Authority:** California Coastal Act

**Jurisdiction:**
- Coastal zone (varies, generally mean high tide to ~1,000 yards inland)
- "Development" broadly defined
- Includes intensity-of-use changes

**Water Quality Requirements:**

**All Projects Must:**
- Minimize pollutant transport in runoff
- Minimize post-development flow changes
- Incorporate BMPs during and after construction

**Developments of Water Quality Concern:**
- Larger or high-impact projects
- Proximity to coastal waters
- Require enhanced BMPs

**Design Standards:**
- 85th percentile 24-hour storm (volume-based BMPs)
- 2x 85th percentile 1-hour storm (flow-based BMPs)

**Low Impact Development Emphasis:**
- Site design protecting natural retention
- Small-scale distributed BMPs
- Infiltration, evapotranspiration, harvesting
- Precedence over traditional stormwater controls

## Department of Pesticide Regulation (DPR)

### Surface Water Protection

**Core Functions:**
- Evaluate and sample surface water for pesticides
- Model pesticide risk before registration
- Implement mitigation measures
- Coordinate with Water Boards

**Monitoring Programs:**

**Agricultural Monitoring:**
- Statewide agricultural areas
- Runoff patterns from agricultural operations
- Coordination with ILRP programs

**Urban Monitoring (Since 2007):**
- Over 5 million pounds pesticides in urban areas (2022)
- Frequent detections in urban surface waters
- Aquatic invertebrate toxicity documented
- Study 329: Northern California urban monitoring

### SURF Database

**Coverage:**
- 1.58 million water sample results
- 379,000 sediment sample results
- Data through February 2025
- Multiple agency sources integrated

**Data Sources:**
- DPR monitoring studies
- CEDEN (Water Board data)
- NWIS (USGS)
- Water Quality Exchange (EPA)

### Management Agency Agreement

**DPR-State Water Board Agreement (Updated 2019):**
- Coordinates interactions
- Facilitates staff and executive communication
- Promotes problem-solving
- Replaces original 1997 agreement

### Registration Evaluation

**PREM Model:**
- Pesticide Registration Evaluation Model
- Assesses aquatic impacts before registration
- Protects aquatic and benthic organisms
- Prevents problematic registrations

## Office of Environmental Health Hazard Assessment (OEHHA)

### Drinking Water Standards Development

**Public Health Goals (PHGs):**
- Level with no significant health risk
- Non-regulatory (scientific basis only)
- Used to support MCL development
- More protective than federal standards

**Notification Levels:**
- Health-based advisory levels
- Response triggers for contamination
- Precautionary guidance

### Recent PHG Initiatives (October 2025)

**New PHGs Under Development:**
- Perfluorohexane Sulfonic Acid (PFHxS)
- Arsenic (update)
- Requested by State Water Board
- Public comment periods active

### California-Specific Standards

**More Stringent Than Federal:**

| Contaminant | California MCL | Federal MCL |
|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Hexavalent chromium | 10 ppb (pending) | None |
| Perchlorate | 6 ppb | None |
| 1,2,3-TCP | 5 ppt | None |

## Delta Stewardship Council

### Statutory Authority

**Established:** Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta Reform Act of 2009

**Role:**
- Create comprehensive, legally enforceable Delta Plan
- Oversight of State and local agency Delta activities
- Regulatory and appellate authority over covered actions

### Delta Plan

**Coequal Goals:**
1. Reliable statewide water supply
2. Protected, restored Delta ecosystem
3. Preserve Delta as a place

**Plan Components:**
- Regulatory policies
- Recommendations
- Eight chapters covering:
  - Reliable water supply
  - Ecosystem protection/restoration
  - Cultural/natural resource values
  - Water quality
  - Flood risk reduction
  - Funding principles

### Reviews and Oversight

**2024 Five-Year Review:**
- Report cards rating 10-year progress
- Regulatory and appellate role effectiveness
- Next five-year priority recommendations
- Next review: 2029

**Covered Actions:**
- Activities in Delta or Suisun Marsh
- Must be consistent with Delta Plan
- Council certification required

## State Lands Commission

### Public Trust Doctrine

**Sovereign Lands:**
- California acquired at statehood (1850)
- Tide and submerged lands
- Beds of navigable waterways
- Held in trust for the people

**Public Trust Purposes (Evolved):**
- Traditional: Commerce, navigation, fisheries
- Expanded: Recreation, swimming, boating
- Further: Preserving natural state, scenic values, wildlife habitat

### Jurisdiction

**Commission Authority (Since 1938):**
- Administrator of Public Trust lands
- Oversight of granted lands (80+ local trustees)
- Review of trustee operations
- Residual state authority

**Granted Lands:**
- 300+ legislative grants
- 80+ local municipalities as trustees
- Governed by specific granting statutes
- Must conform to Public Trust Doctrine

### Land Types

**Coastal:**
- Tidelands (mean high to mean low tide)
- Submerged lands (to 3 nautical miles)
- Filled former tidelands

**Inland:**
- Beds of navigable lakes
- Beds of navigable rivers

**Restrictions:**
- Constitution prohibits sale
- Termination of trust rights extremely rare
- Must be consistent with trust purposes and needs
