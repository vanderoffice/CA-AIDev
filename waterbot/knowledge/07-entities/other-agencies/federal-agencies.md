# Federal Agencies with California Water Jurisdiction

## U.S. Army Corps of Engineers

### Section 404 Permit Authority

**Statutory Basis:** Clean Water Act Section 404

**Jurisdiction:**
- Discharges of dredged or fill material
- Waters of the United States including wetlands

**Activities Requiring 404 Permits:**
- Structure construction requiring fill
- Site development fills (recreational, industrial, commercial, residential)
- Causeways and road fills
- Dams and dikes
- Artificial islands
- Riprap, groins, seawalls, breakwaters
- Beach nourishment
- Levees
- Intake/outfall pipes and utility lines
- Pond creation
- Any dredge or fill discharge

**Important:** Permits required for both permanent AND temporary work

### California Districts

**Los Angeles District:**
- Southern California jurisdiction
- Heavy urban development focus

**Sacramento District:**
- Central Valley and Northern California
- Agricultural and rural areas
- Major flood control responsibilities

**San Francisco District:**
- Bay Area and Central Coast
- Coastal and estuarine focus

### Regulatory Framework

**Regulations:** 33 CFR Parts 320-332

**Permit Types:**
- Individual permits (site-specific review)
- General permits (categorical coverage)
- Nationwide permits

**Coordination Requirements:**
- Section 401 certification from State/Regional Boards
- ESA Section 7 consultation
- NHPA Section 106 compliance

### Flood Control Mission

**Infrastructure:**
- Levee systems throughout California
- Flood control reservoirs
- Channel improvements

**Coordination:**
- Central Valley Flood Protection Board
- Local flood control districts
- DWR partnership

## U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service

### Endangered Species Act Authority

**Section 7 Consultation:**
- "Interagency Cooperation"
- Federal agencies must consult on listed species
- Applies to federal actions, permits, funding

**Section 7(a)(1):** Federal agencies must carry out conservation programs

**Section 7(a)(2):** Ensure actions won't:
- Jeopardize listed species
- Destroy/adversely modify critical habitat

### Consultation Process

**Informal Consultation:**
1. Contact local Ecological Services field office
2. Information exchange about proposed action
3. Determine if action may affect species
4. Use Information for Planning and Consultation (IPaC) tool

**Formal Consultation:**
- Required if "may affect" determination
- 90-day consultation period
- 45-day biological opinion preparation
- May include reasonable and prudent measures

**Biological Opinion Contents:**
- Analysis of action consequences
- Jeopardy/no jeopardy determination
- Critical habitat effects
- Incidental take statement (if applicable)
- Reasonable and prudent measures

### California-Relevant Species

**Freshwater Species:**
- Delta smelt
- Various salmon populations
- Steelhead trout
- Freshwater mussels

**Coordination:**
- Joint operations with NOAA Fisheries
- State ITP coordination
- Water project consultations

## USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service

### Environmental Quality Incentives Program (EQIP)

**Purpose:** Flagship conservation program for working lands

**Assistance Types:**
- Technical assistance (free)
- Financial assistance
- Resource assessment
- Practice design
- Resource monitoring

**Application Process:**
1. Visit local NRCS field office
2. Complete AD 1026 form
3. Provide tax ID, deed/lease, farm number
4. Applications ranked by resource concerns
5. Sign contract if selected
6. Implement practices to NRCS standards
7. Inspection and payment

### National Water Quality Initiative

**Purpose:** USDA's premiere water quality initiative

**Focus:**
- Accelerate voluntary on-farm conservation
- Targeted watershed investments
- Water quality monitoring
- Greatest clean water benefits

**California Implementation:**
- Priority watersheds identified
- NRCS-Water Board coordination
- Agricultural BMP funding
