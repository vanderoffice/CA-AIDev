### Geographic Coverage

**Portions of 20 counties** including: Modoc, Shasta, Lassen, Plumas, Butte, Glenn, Colusa, Lake, Sutter, Yuba, Sierra, Nevada, Placer, Yolo, Napa (northeast), Solano (west), Sacramento, El Dorado, Amador, Calaveras, San Joaquin, Contra Costa (east), Stanislaus, Tuolumne, Merced, Mariposa, Madera, Kings, Fresno, Tulare, Kern

**Major Water Bodies:**
- Sacramento River
- San Joaquin River
- Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta
- Feather River
- American River
- Mokelumne, Tuolumne, Merced, Kings rivers

### Key Water Quality Issues

**Salt and Nitrate Accumulation**
- Elevated concentrations in surface and groundwater
- Agricultural practices contributing loading
- Drinking water supplies threatened

**Agricultural Runoff**
- California's most productive agricultural region
- Pesticides, fertilizers, salts, sediment
- Irrigation return flows

**Dairy Operations**
- Large concentrated animal feeding operations
- Nutrient and pathogen loading
- Groundwater contamination concerns

**Groundwater Contamination**
- Nitrate levels exceeding safe standards
- Rural households and small systems affected
- Thousands of impacted wells

### Major Regulatory Programs

**CV-SALTS (Central Valley Salinity Alternatives for Long-Term Sustainability)**
- Initiated 2006 through joint State/Regional Board effort
- Central Valley Salinity Coalition formed 2008
- Salt and Nitrate Management Plan adopted 2017
- Control Program adopted 2018, approved 2019-2020
- Management zone-based approach
- Individual pathway compliance options

**CV-SALTS Nitrate Control Program**
- Free replacement water for 1,800+ nitrate-impacted wells
- Expanded to 8 additional geographic zones
- February 2025: Priority 2 basin expansion
- Notices to Comply issued
- Pathway selection and NOI filing required

**Irrigated Lands Regulatory Program (ILRP)**
- Prevents agricultural runoff impairments
- Waste discharge requirements
- Management practice implementation
- Monitoring and reporting
- Coalition and individual enrollment options

**Confined Animal Facility Program**
- Dairies, feedlots, poultry operations
- Waste discharge requirements
- General permits
- Nutrient management plans

### Current Priorities

- CV-SALTS implementation
- Nitrate control program expansion
- ILRP compliance and enforcement
- Dairy facility oversight
- Groundwater quality restoration
- Safe drinking water for affected communities

### CV-SALTS Timeline

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|

### Geographic Coverage

**Counties:** Modoc (east side), Lassen (east side and Eagle Lake), Sierra, Nevada (eastern), Placer (eastern), El Dorado (eastern), Alpine, Mono, Inyo, Kern (east), San Bernardino (northern), Los Angeles (northeast corner)

**Major Water Bodies:**
- Lake Tahoe
- Mono Lake
- Owens River
- Truckee River
- Walker River
- Carson River

### Key Water Quality Issues

**Lake Tahoe Clarity**
- Outstanding National Resource Water
- Declining deep water transparency since 1960s
- Fine sediment particles and nutrients
- Phytoplankton productivity increasing

**Nearshore Water Quality**
- Changes in nearshore environment observed
- Increased attached and floating algae
- Invasive species presence
- Nearshore clarity reduction

**High Desert Groundwater**
- Mojave Desert regions
- Limited recharge
- Contamination concerns

**Owens Valley**
- Historical water diversions
- Dust mitigation
- Ecosystem impacts

### Major Regulatory Programs

**Lake Tahoe TMDL** (adopted 2011)
- Joint effort with Nevada DEP
- Addresses fine sediment particles and nutrients
- Goal: Restore deep water transparency
- Load reductions from point and nonpoint sources

**Lake Tahoe Basin Management**
- Solid and liquid waste disposal oversight
- Road maintenance regulation
- Construction permitting
- Stormwater management
- Development impact mitigation

**Nearshore Water Quality Protection Plan**
- Addresses shoreline to 20-meter depth
- Monitoring programs
- Scientific research support
- Shorezone activity regulation
- Partnership with TRPA and other agencies

**Outstanding National Resource Waters**
- Lake Tahoe designated ONRW
- 2025 Triennial Review: Hot Creek and Little Hot Creek under consideration
- Highest-level protection through anti-degradation policy
- Water quality must be maintained at existing high levels

### Current Priorities

- Lake Tahoe clarity restoration
- Nearshore water quality protection
- ONRW designations consideration
- High desert groundwater protection
- Climate change adaptation
