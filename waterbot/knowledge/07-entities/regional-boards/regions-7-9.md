### Key Water Quality Issues

**Salton Sea Ecological Crisis**
- Enclosed water body with no outlet
- Increasing salinity (57 ppt in 2015 vs. 32-37 ppt for ocean)
- Diminishing inflows due to drought and transfers
- Bioaccumulation of pesticides and selenium
- Air quality concerns from exposed lakebed

**Agricultural Contamination**
- Imperial Valley intensive agriculture
- Coachella Valley operations
- Fertilizer and pesticide runoff
- Sediment loading

**Transboundary Issues**
- New River flows from Mexico
- Contamination from Mexican sources
- Border coordination challenges

### Major Regulatory Programs

**Salton Sea Management Programs**
- TMDL Program for impairments
- Surface Water Ambient Monitoring Program (SWAMP)
- Irrigated Lands Regulatory Program
- New River pollution coordination
- Partnership with CA Natural Resources Agency

**Pupfish Connectivity Project**
- Most recent approval: March 2025
- Restores shallow water habitat
- ~5,000 acres of aquatic pond habitat
- Ponds and islands system
- Supports piscivorous birds
- Provides fish habitat
- Minimizes selenium and disease risks

**Agricultural Conditional Waivers**
- Imperial Valley agricultural operations
- Coachella Valley operations
- Management requirements
- Recognizes economic feasibility constraints

**New River/Mexicali Issues**
- Monitoring transboundary flows
- Coordination with federal agencies
- Contamination assessment

### Current Priorities

- Salton Sea restoration support
- Pupfish habitat creation
- Agricultural runoff management
- Transboundary pollution coordination
- New River water quality improvement

### Key Water Quality Issues

**Tijuana River Transboundary Pollution**
- Large volumes of sewage from Mexico
- Solid waste and sediment
- Impacts Imperial Beach, San Ysidro, Coronado
- Estuary contamination

**Urban Development**
- Rapid growth pressures
- Stormwater runoff
- Habitat loss
- Groundwater impacts

**Coastal Water Quality**
- Beach closures from bacteria
- Tourism and recreation impacts
- Public health concerns

**Military Installations**
- Navy and Marine facilities
- Historical contamination
- Remediation oversight

### Major Regulatory Programs

**Tijuana River Valley Recovery Strategy**

**History:**
- 2009: Recovery Team convened
- 2012: Board endorsed "Living with the Water" strategy
- Collaborative multi-agency approach
- Some progress on specific projects

**Recent Escalation:**
- Collaborative approach insufficient for stopping flows
- More aggressive regulatory approach adopted
- TMDLs developed for bacteria and trash
- Investigative order to USIBWC

**August 2025:** Cease and Desist Order (R9-2025-0139)
- Issued to US International Boundary and Water Commission
- South Bay International Wastewater Treatment Plant
- Requires expansion from 25 MGD to 35 MGD
- Final effluent limits by June 30, 2026

**Lower Tijuana River Advance Restoration Plan**
- Implementation plan for water quality restoration
- Proposed MOU: Water Board, USIBWC, EPA
- Defines roles, responsibilities, funding
- Specific timeframes for source control

**October 2025:** Superfund Consideration
- Board requested $1.4 million for sediment assessment
- Responds to County's Superfund designation proposal
- Would evaluate contamination severity
- Could trigger federal involvement

**2025 Binational Resilience Initiative**
- ~$650,000 awarded
- Joint water quality monitoring systems
- Public data platforms for Tijuana watershed

**MS4 Permits**
- San Diego County municipalities
- Bacteria and trash TMDLs

### Transboundary Challenges

The Tijuana River situation illustrates the complexity of managing water quality across international borders:

- **Volume:** Large flows of untreated/partially treated sewage
- **Jurisdiction:** Requires US-Mexico coordination
- **Agencies:** USIBWC, EPA, State/Regional Water Boards, local governments
- **Infrastructure:** Treatment capacity insufficient
- **Enforcement:** Limited authority over Mexican sources
- **Community impact:** Beaches closed, health risks, property values
