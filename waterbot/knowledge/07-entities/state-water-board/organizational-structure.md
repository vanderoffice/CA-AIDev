## Major Divisions

### Division of Water Quality (DWQ)

**Responsibilities:**
- NPDES permitting and compliance
- Stormwater programs (MS4, industrial, construction)
- Section 401 Water Quality Certification
- Nonpoint source pollution control
- Underground storage tank oversight
- Irrigated Lands Regulatory Program (ILRP)
- Cannabis cultivation water quality
- Water quality planning and standards

**Key Programs:**
- State Implementation Policy (SIP)
- Ocean Plan implementation
- Inland Surface Waters Plan
- Trash provisions
- Bacteria objectives
- Nutrient management

**Coordination Role:**
- Establishes consistency among Regional Boards
- Develops statewide general permits
- Provides policy guidance and training

### Division of Water Rights (DWR)

**Responsibilities:**
- Water rights applications and permits
- Licensing of developed water rights
- Water transfers and changes
- Groundwater recharge permits
- Drought response and curtailments
- Water availability analysis
- Bay-Delta water rights

**Key Functions:**
- Evaluate new appropriation applications
- Process petitions for change
- Maintain eWRIMS database
- Conduct water rights hearings
- Issue temporary permits

**Databases:**
- Electronic Water Rights Information Management System (eWRIMS)
- Statements of Water Diversion and Use
- Water rights permits and licenses

### Division of Drinking Water (DDW)

**Responsibilities:**
- Regulate ~7,500 public water systems
- Enforce Safe Drinking Water Act (state and federal)
- SAFER program administration
- Water system permits
- Compliance monitoring
- Technical assistance

**Field Operations:**
- Multiple regional offices
- Inspections and sanitary surveys
- Enforcement actions
- Emergency response

**Special Programs:**
- SAFER Drinking Water (Senate Bill 200)
- Small water system support
- Consolidation assistance
- Water system partnerships

### Division of Financial Assistance (DFA)

**Responsibilities:**
- Loan and grant program administration
- Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF)
- Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF)
- Underground storage tank cleanup funding
- Proposition bond fund administration
- Technical assistance grants

**Key Programs:**

| Program | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **CWSRF** | Wastewater treatment, stormwater, water recycling |
| **DWSRF** | Drinking water infrastructure |
| **SAFER** | Safe drinking water for underserved communities |
| **UST Cleanup Fund** | Underground storage tank remediation |
| **Water Recycling Funding** | Recycled water projects |

**Operator Certification Program:**
- Certifies wastewater treatment plant operators
- Certifies drinking water system operators
- Training and examination
- Continuing education requirements

## Specialized Offices

### Office of Chief Counsel (OCC)

**Functions:**
- Legal advice to Board and staff
- Litigation support and coordination
- Administrative hearing preparation
- Regulatory development assistance
- Public Records Act compliance
- CEQA review

**Practice Areas:**
- Water quality permitting
- Enforcement actions
- Water rights adjudication
- Underground storage tank cleanup
- Administrative procedure
- Environmental justice

### Office of Sustainable Groundwater Management

**Responsibilities:**
- SGMA implementation support
- Groundwater Sustainability Plan review
- State intervention authority
- Probationary basin management
- Technical assistance to GSAs

### Office of Research, Planning and Performance

**Functions:**
- Strategic planning
- Performance metrics
- Data management
- Research coordination
- Policy analysis

### Office of Public Participation

**Responsibilities:**
- Public meeting coordination
- Comment period management
- Environmental justice outreach
- Tribal consultation support
- Stakeholder engagement

### Office of Information Management and Analysis (OIMA)

**Functions:**
- Data systems management
- GeoTracker database
- CIWQS (California Integrated Water Quality System)
- SDWIS (Safe Drinking Water Information System)
- Public data portals

### Administrative Hearings Office

**Functions:**
- Conduct water rights hearings
- Adjudicate disputes
- Issue hearing officer decisions
- Support Board adjudicatory proceedings

### Division of Administrative Services

**Functions:**
- Human resources
- Budget and accounting
- Contracts and procurement
- Facilities management
- Information technology

## Regional Board Oversight

The State Water Board provides oversight and coordination for nine Regional Water Quality Control Boards:

| Region | Headquarters | Staff Size (Approx.) |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| 1 - North Coast | Santa Rosa | 65 |
| 2 - San Francisco Bay | Oakland | 200 |
| 3 - Central Coast | San Luis Obispo | 75 |
| 4 - Los Angeles | Los Angeles | 225 |
| 5 - Central Valley | Rancho Cordova | 350 |
| 6 - Lahontan | South Lake Tahoe & Victorville | 75 |
| 7 - Colorado River | Palm Desert | 30 |
| 8 - Santa Ana | Riverside | 150 |

- Basin plan approval
- Petition review (appeals)
- Statewide policy compliance
- Enforcement coordination
- Funding allocation
