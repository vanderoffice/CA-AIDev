# Childcare for Migrant Farmworker Families

**Purpose:** Specialized childcare programs serving California's agricultural worker families.

---

## Overview

California offers specialized childcare programs for families working in agriculture:
- **Migrant Head Start** — Federal program for migrant/seasonal workers
- **CDSS Migrant Child Care** — State-funded programs
- **Local APP services** — Standard subsidies with agricultural flexibility

These programs understand the unique challenges of agricultural work:
- Seasonal employment
- Geographic mobility
- Irregular hours
- Language needs
- Rural locations

---

## Who Qualifies as "Migrant"

### Federal Definition (Migrant Head Start)

A family qualifies as **migrant** if:
- A parent/guardian works in agriculture (crops, livestock, fishing, food processing)
- The family has moved within the past 24 months for agricultural work
- The work is seasonal or temporary

### Seasonal Agricultural Workers

A family qualifies as **seasonal** if:
- A parent/guardian works in agriculture
- The work is seasonal (not year-round)
- The family has NOT moved for work in the past 24 months

### Industries That Qualify

| Qualifies | Examples |
|-----------|----------|
| ✅ Crop production | Picking, planting, harvesting |
| ✅ Nurseries | Greenhouse work, plant production |
| ✅ Livestock | Dairy, cattle, poultry |
| ✅ Food processing | Packing houses, canneries |
| ✅ Agricultural transportation | Hauling crops/goods |
| ✅ Bee operations | Beekeeping, pollination services |
| ✅ Spirulina harvesting | Algae farming |
| ❌ Retail grocery | Not agricultural |
| ❌ Restaurant work | Not agricultural |

## Migrant Head Start

### What It Is

Federally funded childcare and education for children of migrant and seasonal agricultural workers.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Ages served** | 6 weeks to 5 years |
| **Cost** | FREE |
| **Hours** | Extended hours (often 10+ hours) |
| **Services** | Education, health, nutrition, family support |
| **Locations** | Agricultural regions throughout California |

### Services Provided

Beyond childcare, Migrant Head Start offers:
- Early childhood education
- Health screenings and referrals
- Dental care coordination
- Nutrition services (meals and education)
- Family engagement activities
- Transportation (at many sites)
- Parent training and support
- Connections to community resources

### Eligibility

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Work requirement** | Parent works in migrant/seasonal agriculture |
| **Income** | Based on Federal Poverty Guidelines |
| **Child age** | 6 weeks through 5 years |
| **Move requirement** | Moved for work in past 24 months (migrant) OR seasonal work (seasonal) |

### Income Guidelines

Migrant Head Start uses Federal Poverty Guidelines:

| Family Size | Annual Income Limit (2024) |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| 2 | ~$20,440 |
| 3 | ~$25,820 |
| 4 | ~$31,200 |
| 5 | ~$36,580 |
| 6 | ~$41,960 |

**Note:** Up to 10% of enrollment may be from over-income families.

### Disability Enrollment

**10% of enrollment** is reserved for children with disabilities, ensuring inclusive access.

### How to Find Programs

1. **Head Start Locator:** [eclkc.ohs.acf.hhs.gov/center-locator](https://eclkc.ohs.acf.hhs.gov/center-locator)
   - Search by zip code
   - Select "Migrant/Seasonal Head Start"

2. **Local CCR&R:** [rrnetwork.org](https://rrnetwork.org)
   - Ask about migrant-serving programs
   - Get referrals to local options

3. **County Office of Education**
   - Many run Migrant Head Start programs
   - Contact early childhood department

## CDSS Migrant Child Care Programs

### What They Are

State-funded childcare programs specifically for migrant families, administered through CDSS contracts.

### How They Differ from Migrant Head Start

| Feature | Migrant Head Start | CDSS Migrant Programs |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Funding | Federal | State |
| Ages | 0-5 | 0-12 |
| Focus | Comprehensive services | Childcare |
| Administration | Head Start grantees | APP contractors |

### Accessing CDSS Migrant Services

Contact your local APP and:
1. Identify yourself as a migrant/seasonal agricultural worker
2. Ask about migrant-specific programs or funding
3. Inquire about flexible scheduling for seasonal work

## Standard Subsidies for Agricultural Workers

### APP Subsidies

Agricultural workers can access standard APP subsidies like any other family:

| Requirement | How Agricultural Work Qualifies |
|-------------|--------------------------------|
| Income | Must be below 85% SMI |
| Need | Employment in agriculture counts |
| Documentation | Pay stubs, employer letter, or farm labor contractor records |

### Challenges and Solutions

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Seasonal work ends | Apply for "seeking employment" childcare (60-80 days) |
| Variable hours | Variable schedule authorization |
| Cash payment | Self-certification of income |
| Multiple employers | Document all employment |
| Moving between counties | Transfer services (may need new application) |

---

## Documentation for Agricultural Workers

### What You May Need

| Document | Source |
|----------|--------|
| **Pay stubs** | Employer, farm labor contractor |
| **Employer letter** | Grower, farm, packing house |
| **Contractor records** | Farm labor contractor |
| **Self-certification** | If other docs unavailable |
| **Prior year taxes** | If available |

### If Documentation Is Difficult

Many agricultural workers face challenges documenting income:
- Paid in cash
- Multiple short-term employers
- Farm labor contractors
- Informal arrangements

**Solutions:**
- Self-certification under penalty of perjury
- Bank statements showing deposits
- Written statement from farm labor contractor
- Any available pay records

## Seasonal Patterns and Planning

### Peak Seasons by Region

| Region | Peak Season | Crops |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Imperial Valley | Winter (Nov-Mar) | Lettuce, vegetables |
| Coachella Valley | Spring (Feb-May) | Dates, grapes, citrus |
| Central Valley | Summer (May-Oct) | Stone fruit, grapes, nuts |
| Salinas Valley | Year-round (peaks spring-fall) | Lettuce, strawberries |
| Central Coast | Spring-Fall | Strawberries, vegetables |

### Planning Childcare Around Seasons

**Peak season:**
- Secure childcare early (programs fill up)
- Confirm extended hours availability
- Arrange backup care

**Off-season:**
- Some programs reduce hours
- May need to transition to different care
- Some families return to home base

## Health and Safety

### Field Work Exposure Concerns

Migrant Head Start and migrant childcare programs understand:
- Pesticide exposure risks
- Heat-related illness
- Dust and allergens
- Work-related injuries affecting families

### Health Services Provided

- Health screenings
- Dental referrals
- Immunization tracking
- Connection to migrant health clinics
- Nutrition education

### Migrant Health Clinics

Many agricultural areas have federally qualified migrant health centers:
- Low-cost or free health care
- Bilingual services
- Understanding of agricultural worker needs

Find one: [findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov](https://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov)

---

## Moving Between Locations

### Following the Crops

If your family moves for work:

**Before moving:**
- Get copies of all childcare records
- Get immunization records
- Ask about transferring eligibility

**At new location:**
- Contact local Migrant Head Start or CCR&R immediately
- Bring documentation from previous program
- Apply for local services

### Portability

Migrant Head Start programs coordinate across regions:
- Records can transfer
- Previous enrollment facilitates new enrollment
- Programs communicate about families
