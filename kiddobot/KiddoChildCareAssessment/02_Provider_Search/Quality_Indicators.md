# How to Evaluate Childcare Quality

**Purpose:** Understand what makes quality childcare and how to assess it.

---

## Why Quality Matters

Research consistently shows that high-quality early care:
- Improves school readiness
- Supports social-emotional development
- Reduces need for later remedial services
- Has lasting effects into adulthood

**Quality isn't just about safety** ‚Äî it's about whether the environment actively supports your child's development.

---

## The Three Pillars of Quality

Quality childcare includes:

| Pillar | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Health & Safety** | Physical safety, supervision, cleanliness |
| **Developmental Environment** | Activities, interactions, learning opportunities |
| **Staff Quality** | Training, stability, responsiveness |

## Staff-to-Child Ratios

### Why Ratios Matter

Lower ratios mean:
- More individual attention for each child
- Better supervision and safety
- More responsive interactions
- More learning opportunities

### California Requirements by Age

| Age Group | Title 22 (Minimum) | Title 5 (Subsidized) |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Infants (0-18 mo)** | 1:4 | 1:3 |
| **Toddlers (18-36 mo)** | 1:6 | 1:4 |
| **Preschool (3-5 yrs)** | 1:12 | 1:8 |
| **School-age (K+)** | 1:14 | 1:14 |

### Family Child Care Ratios

| Home Type | Capacity |
|-----------|----------|
| **Small FCCH** | Up to 8 children (1 provider) |
| **Large FCCH** | 9-14 children (provider + assistant) |

**Note:** Provider's own children under 10 count in the ratio.

### What to Look For

```
When you visit:
‚ñ° Count adults and children in each group
‚ñ° Are ratios being maintained?
‚ñ° Is there backup when staff take breaks?
‚ñ° What happens during transitions (meals, naps)?
```

## Teacher Qualifications

### California Requirements

| Position | Title 22 | Title 5 |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Teacher** | 12 ECE units + 6 mo. experience | 24 ECE units + 16 GE units |
| **Associate Teacher** | 6 ECE units | 12 ECE units |
| **Aide** | No units required | No units required |
| **Director** | Varies by program size | Higher requirements |

### Understanding Credentials

California issues Child Development Permits through the Commission on Teacher Credentialing:

| Permit Level | Requirements | Can Do |
|--------------|--------------|--------|
| **Assistant** | 6 ECE units | Work under supervision |
| **Associate** | 12 ECE units + 50 days experience | Work with less supervision |
| **Teacher** | 24 ECE + 16 GE units + experience | Lead a classroom |
| **Master Teacher** | Bachelor's degree + experience | Lead, mentor, coordinate |
| **Site Supervisor** | Higher units + admin coursework | Supervise programs |
| **Program Director** | Bachelor's or higher + extensive experience | Direct multiple sites |

### Beyond Minimum Requirements

Some indicators of higher quality:
- Teachers with Bachelor's degrees in ECE
- National credentials (CDA, NAEYC certifications)
- Ongoing professional development
- Low staff turnover
- Staff who stay with the same children over time

### Questions to Ask

```
‚ñ° What education/training do your teachers have?
‚ñ° Do staff have Child Development Permits?
‚ñ° What ongoing training do staff receive?
‚ñ° How long have your teachers worked here?
‚ñ° What is your staff turnover rate?
```

## National Accreditation

### What Accreditation Means

Voluntary process where programs demonstrate they exceed state licensing minimums.

### Major Accrediting Bodies

| Organization | Type | What It Means |
|--------------|------|---------------|
| **NAEYC** | Centers | National Association for Education of Young Children |
| **NAFCC** | Family Child Care | National Association for Family Child Care |
| **NAC** | Centers | National Accreditation Commission |
| **NECPA** | Centers | National Early Childhood Program Accreditation |
| **ACA** | Camps/OST | American Camp Association |

### What NAEYC Accreditation Requires

- Self-study process
- Meeting 10 program standards
- Validation visit from assessors
- Annual reporting and recertification

**NAEYC Standards:**
1. Relationships
2. Curriculum
3. Teaching
4. Assessment of Child Progress
5. Health
6. Staff Competencies, Preparation, and Support
7. Families
8. Community Relationships
9. Physical Environment
10. Leadership and Management

### How to Verify Accreditation

- **NAEYC:** [naeyc.org/accreditation](https://www.naeyc.org/accreditation)
- **NAFCC:** [nafcc.org](https://www.nafcc.org)
- **Ask the program** for their accreditation certificate

## Quality Counts California

### What It Is

California's statewide Quality Rating and Improvement System (QRIS). Participating programs are rated on quality indicators.

### How It Works

- Programs undergo assessment
- Receive ratings on quality areas
- Get support for improvement
- Ratings help families make informed choices

### What Quality Counts Assesses

| Quality Area | What's Measured |
|--------------|-----------------|
| **Child Development** | Curriculum, learning activities, readiness |
| **Teachers & Teaching** | Qualifications, interactions, CLASS scores |
| **Program & Environment** | Health, safety, learning environment |

### How to Use Quality Counts

1. Visit [qualitycountsca.net](https://qualitycountsca.net)
2. Search for programs in your area
3. Review ratings and quality information
4. Use as one factor in your decision

**Note:** Not all programs participate. Non-participation doesn't mean low quality.

## Classroom Interactions (CLASS)

### What CLASS Measures

CLASS (Classroom Assessment Scoring System) is a research-based tool measuring teacher-child interactions:

| Domain | What It Measures |
|--------|------------------|
| **Emotional Support** | Positive climate, teacher sensitivity, regard for child perspective |
| **Classroom Organization** | Behavior management, productivity, learning formats |
| **Instructional Support** | Concept development, quality of feedback, language modeling |

### Why Interactions Matter

Research shows the quality of teacher-child interactions is one of the strongest predictors of child outcomes ‚Äî more than materials, curriculum, or environment.

### What Good Interactions Look Like

```
‚úì Warm, positive tone
‚úì Teachers at child level
‚úì Back-and-forth conversations
‚úì Open-ended questions
‚úì Responsive to children's interests
‚úì Extending children's thinking
‚úì Encouraging effort, not just results
```

### What to Watch For During Visits

```
‚ñ° How do teachers greet children?
‚ñ° How do teachers respond when children are upset?
‚ñ° Do teachers have conversations WITH children (not just TO them)?
‚ñ° Do teachers ask questions that require thinking?
‚ñ° Is the atmosphere warm or stressed?
```

## Physical Environment

### Indoor Space

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Space per child** | 35 sq ft indoor (centers) |
| **Learning areas** | Distinct areas for different activities |
| **Materials** | Age-appropriate, varied, accessible |
| **Safety** | No hazards, childproofed |

### Outdoor Space

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| **Space per child** | 75 sq ft outdoor (centers) |
| **Equipment** | Safe, well-maintained |
| **Activities** | Varied gross motor opportunities |
| **Shade/shelter** | Protection from elements |

### What Quality Environments Include

```
‚úì Materials organized and accessible to children
‚úì Different activity areas (blocks, dramatic play, art, books)
‚úì Materials rotated to maintain interest
‚úì Child-created work displayed
‚úì Natural light when possible
‚úì Soft elements (rugs, cushions)
‚úì Outdoor time daily
```

## Curriculum & Activities

### What to Look For

| Element | Signs of Quality |
|---------|------------------|
| **Daily schedule** | Posted, consistent, balanced |
| **Play-based learning** | Children explore through play |
| **Teacher-guided activities** | Intentional learning experiences |
| **Child-led exploration** | Following children's interests |
| **Developmentally appropriate** | Matches children's abilities |


## Health & Safety Practices

### Supervision

| Practice | Standard |
|----------|----------|
| **Visual supervision** | Adults can see all children at all times |
| **Ratios maintained** | Even during transitions |
| **Attendance tracking** | Head counts throughout day |
| **Check-in/out** | Controlled access, parent verification |

### Health Practices

```
‚ñ° Handwashing routines (children and staff)
‚ñ° Diapering/toileting procedures
‚ñ° Food safety (temperature, allergies, sanitation)
‚ñ° Illness policies (when to stay home)
‚ñ° Medication administration procedures
‚ñ° Immunization requirements
```

### Emergency Preparedness

```
‚ñ° Posted emergency plans
‚ñ° Regular drills (fire, earthquake, lockdown)
‚ñ° Emergency supplies
‚ñ° Parent notification procedures
‚ñ° Staff first aid/CPR training
```

## Quality Assessment Checklist

### Before Your Visit

```
‚ñ° Check license status (CCLD database)
‚ñ° Review any violations
‚ñ° Check accreditation status
‚ñ° Look up Quality Counts rating (if participating)
‚ñ° Read online reviews (with healthy skepticism)
```

### During Your Visit

```
ENVIRONMENT:
‚ñ° Clean and organized?
‚ñ° Age-appropriate materials?
‚ñ° Safe indoor and outdoor spaces?
‚ñ° Child work displayed?

INTERACTIONS:
‚ñ° Warm, positive atmosphere?
‚ñ° Teachers engaged with children?
‚ñ° Children seem happy?
‚ñ° How are conflicts handled?

SUPERVISION:
‚ñ° Ratios maintained?
‚ñ° Adults attentive?
‚ñ° Children accounted for?

STAFF:
‚ñ° Seem experienced and confident?
‚ñ° Responsive to children and you?
‚ñ° Knowledgeable about child development?
```

### After Your Visit

```
‚ñ° Did children seem engaged and happy?
‚ñ° Could I see my child here?
‚ñ° Did staff answer questions openly?
‚ñ° Were any red flags apparent?
‚ñ° What does my gut tell me?
```
