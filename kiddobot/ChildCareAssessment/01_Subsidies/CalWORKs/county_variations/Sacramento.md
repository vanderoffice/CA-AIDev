# CalWORKs Child Care: Sacramento County Guide

**Population:** ~1.5 million
**Region:** 2 (Lower Cost of Living)
**Primary Agency:** Sacramento County Department of Human Assistance (DHA)

---

## Overview

Sacramento County, as the seat of California state government, often serves as a model for policy implementation. Agencies here tend to be well-versed in regulations and processes tend to be "by the book." The moderate cost of living means subsidy vouchers stretch further, though provider wages are also lower, contributing to staffing shortages. Waitlists are generally shorter than in the major coastal metros.

## Sacramento-Specific Advantages

### 1. Regulatory Expertise

As the state capital, Sacramento agencies:
- Are often first to implement new policies
- Have direct relationships with state agencies
- Staff tend to be knowledgeable about nuances
- Documentation processes are clear and consistent

### 2. Affordable Market = Voucher Value

The gap between state reimbursement rates and market rates is smaller in Sacramento:

| Age Group | State Rate | Sacramento Market |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Infant Center | ~$1,500/month | ~$1,400-1,800/month |
| Preschool Center | ~$1,100/month | ~$1,000-1,400/month |

**Result:** More providers accept subsidies than in Bay Area or LA.

### 3. Shorter Waitlists

Sacramento waitlists typically move faster than coastal metros:

| Priority | Typical Wait |
|----------|--------------|
| CPS Involvement | Days to weeks |
| CalWORKs Stage 1 | 1-2 weeks |
| Stage 2 Transition | 2-4 weeks |
| Stage 3 Income-Eligible | **3-9 months** |

### 4. Balanced Provider Mix

Sacramento has a healthy balance of provider types:
- Licensed centers in urban and suburban areas
- Family Child Care Homes throughout
- School-based programs
- License-exempt options

---

## Sacramento-Specific Challenges

### 1. Provider Staffing Shortages

Lower wages than coastal areas mean:
- Higher staff turnover at childcare centers
- Some providers operating below capacity
- Quality can vary more widely

### 2. Geographic Spread

Sacramento County is large and diverse:

| Area | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **Downtown/Midtown** | Urban; more centers; walkable |
| **Elk Grove** | Suburban; growing demand; good providers |
| **Rancho Cordova** | Mix; moderate availability |
| **Folsom** | Higher income area; fewer subsidized slots |
| **North Highlands/Arden** | More FCCHs; higher need |
| **South Sacramento** | Higher need; more providers accepting subsidies |

### 3. Heat-Related Considerations

Sacramento's hot summers affect childcare:
- Many programs limit outdoor time in summer
- Ask about air conditioning and heat policies
- Some FCCHs may have limited cooling

## Application Process

### Stage 1: Through DHA

```
1. Apply for CalWORKs at DHA office or online
2. Attend orientation and enroll in CalWORKs employment services
3. Request childcare from your CalWORKs caseworker
4. Complete Form CCP 7 and gather documentation
5. Select your provider
6. Submit for authorization
7. Wait for approval (typically 2-4 weeks in Sacramento)
```

**DHA Service Centers:**

| Location | Address |
|----------|---------|
| **Main Office** | 2433 Marconi Ave, Sacramento |
| **Citrus Heights** | 5801 Sunrise Blvd |
| **Florin** | 7000 Franklin Blvd |
| **Rancho Cordova** | 10401 Old Placerville Rd |

### Stages 2/3: Transition

When leaving cash aid:
```
1. DHA refers your case to Stage 2/3 services
2. Receive intake appointment
3. Verify income and continued need
4. Maintain or update provider
5. Ongoing renewals typically annual
```

## Sacramento Tips

### For Faster Processing

1. **Apply online when possible:**
   - [BenefitsCal](https://benefitscal.com) for cash aid application
   - Faster initial processing than in-person

2. **Be thorough with documentation:**
   - Sacramento agencies are detail-oriented
   - Missing documents = delays
   - Bring originals AND copies

3. **Follow up proactively:**
   - Call your caseworker if you haven't heard in 2 weeks
   - Be polite but persistent
   - Document your contacts

### Finding Providers

**Use Child Action referrals:**
- Call (916) 369-0191
- Specify zip code, child's age, hours needed
- Ask for providers currently accepting subsidies

**Consider:**
- Family Child Care Homes (often more availability)
- Before/after school programs (ASES, ExCEL)
- License-exempt care while waiting

**Search tools:**
- MyChildCare.ca.gov (state database)
- Child Action website
- 211 Sacramento

### For State Employees

Sacramento has many state workers. If you're a state employee:
- Check if your department has a childcare referral program
- Some state buildings have on-site childcare
- CalPERS benefits don't include childcare subsidies, but you may income-qualify

## Documentation Requirements

Sacramento follows state standards closely. Be prepared with:

**Required for ALL:**
- Valid ID for parent(s)
- Birth certificate for each child
- Social Security cards (or SSA application receipt)
- Proof of Sacramento County residence (utility bill, lease)
- Income documentation (60 days of pay stubs)

**If employed:**
- Employer verification letter
- Work schedule
- Recent pay stubs

**If self-employed:**
- Prior year tax return
- Current Profit & Loss statement
- Business license (if applicable)

**If in school:**
- Enrollment verification
- Class schedule
- Financial aid documentation

## Special Populations

### Gig Economy Workers

Growing population in Sacramento (delivery, rideshare):
- Track income weekly throughout the year
- Prepare a monthly P&L statement
- Be ready to explain income fluctuations
- Income averaging over 12 months is available

### Immigrant Families

- Child's eligibility based on the CHILD, not parent
- DHA does not share information with immigration enforcement
- Spanish-language services available
- Alternative documentation accepted for identity/residence

### Homeless Families

- Shelter letter accepted as proof of residence
- Sacramento Steps Forward can provide support documentation
- May qualify for priority placement
- Multiple entry points: DHA, 211, Sacramento Housing Alliance

### Children with Disabilities

- Connect with Alta California Regional Center: (916) 978-6400
- May qualify for specialized care arrangements
- Higher reimbursement rates available
- Inclusion support services exist

### Foster/Kinship Families

- Often qualify for emergency childcare
- Contact Sacramento County Child Protective Services
- Kinship caregivers frequently eligible but may not know to apply

## Family Fee Information (Region 2)

Sacramento is in **Region 2** (lower cost of living), which affects MAP amounts:

| Family Size | MAP (Non-Exempt) | MAP (Exempt) |
|-------------|------------------|--------------|
| 3 | $1,064 | $1,181 |
| 4 | $1,263 | $1,411 |
| 5 | $1,444 | $1,616 |

**Family Fee Exemption:** If income is below 75% SMI, you pay NO family fee.

| Family Size | 75% SMI (Monthly) |
|-------------|-------------------|
| 3 | $6,869 |
| 4 | $7,959 |
| 5 | $9,232 |
