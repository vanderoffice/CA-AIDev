# CalWORKs Child Care: San Diego County Guide

**Population:** ~3.3 million (2nd largest in CA)
**Region:** 1 (Higher Cost of Living)
**Primary Agency:** County of San Diego Health and Human Services Agency (HHSA)

---

## Overview

San Diego County benefits from a well-organized childcare system, with the YMCA Childcare Resource Service as a central coordinator. The county has a good mix of licensed centers and family childcare homes, though infant care remains a bottleneck. Military families are a significant population, and the proximity to the Mexican border creates some unique documentation considerations.

## San Diego-Specific Advantages

### 1. Well-Organized System

San Diego's CCR&R (YMCA CRS) is consistently rated as one of the strongest in California:
- Responsive staff
- Clear processes
- Good bilingual (English/Spanish) support
- Effective coordination between county and APP

### 2. Centralized Waitlist

Unlike LA County's fragmented system, San Diego has a more centralized eligibility list:
- One primary point of contact (YMCA CRS) for Stage 2/3
- Clearer tracking of your place in line
- More predictable process

### 3. Good Provider Mix

San Diego has a reasonable mix of provider types:

| Provider Type | Availability |
|---------------|--------------|
| Licensed Centers | Moderate |
| Family Child Care Homes | Good |
| License-Exempt (FFN) | Available |
| Infant Care | Limited (still a bottleneck) |
| School-Age Programs | Good |

## San Diego-Specific Challenges

### 1. Military Population Impact

San Diego has a large military presence (Navy, Marines, Coast Guard). This affects childcare in several ways:

**For Military Families:**
- You may qualify for Military Child Care in Your Neighborhood (MCCYN)
- If MCCYN waitlist is too long, you CAN apply for California state subsidies
- Deployments can suddenly change family income and "need" documentation

**For Civilian Families:**
- Some providers primarily serve military families
- Military families have separate funding sources, which can affect slot availability

### 2. Border Proximity Considerations

For families living near the Mexican border:
- Cross-border commuting families may have complex schedule documentation needs
- Some families have relatives in Mexico who cannot serve as license-exempt providers (must be in CA)
- Immigration status concerns exist, though remember: child's eligibility is based on the CHILD

### 3. North vs. South County Disparities

| Area | Characteristics |
|------|-----------------|
| **North County (Oceanside, Carlsbad, Escondido)** | More suburban; good mix of providers |
| **Central (San Diego city)** | Urban; more options but higher demand |
| **South County (Chula Vista, National City)** | More FCCHs; strong Spanish-language services |
| **East County (El Cajon, Santee)** | More suburban/rural; fewer providers |

## Application Process

### Stage 1: Through HHSA

```
1. Apply for CalWORKs cash aid at a Family Resource Center
2. Enroll in CalWORKs employment services
3. Request childcare from your CalWORKs caseworker
4. Complete Form CCP 7 and gather documentation
5. Select your provider
6. Submit for authorization
7. Wait for approval (target: 45 days)
```

**Family Resource Centers by Region:**
- **Central:** 1255 Imperial Ave, San Diego
- **Metro:** 5256 Mission Center Rd, San Diego
- **North Coastal:** 649 W Mission Ave, Escondido
- **North Inland:** 649 W Mission Ave, Escondido
- **South:** 690 Oxford St, Chula Vista

### Stages 2/3: Through YMCA CRS

When transitioning off cash aid:
```
1. HHSA refers your case to YMCA CRS
2. YMCA CRS contacts you for intake
3. Verify income and need documentation
4. Continue with existing provider or find new one
5. YMCA CRS manages ongoing payments and renewals
```

## San Diego Tips

### Getting Off the Waitlist Faster

1. **Apply to multiple places:**
   - County centralized list
   - YMCA CRS
   - Direct with providers you like
   - Head Start/Early Head Start

2. **Know your priority tier:**
   - CPS involvement = top priority
   - CalWORKs recipients = high priority
   - Income-eligible with need = standard priority

3. **Update your information:**
   - If income drops, report it â€” you may move to a higher priority
   - If you have another child, update your file
   - If CPS becomes involved, inform the agency

### Finding Providers

**Use YMCA CRS referral service:**
- Call (800) 481-2151
- Provide your zip code, age of child, hours needed
- They'll give you a list of providers accepting subsidies

**Search online:**
- MyChildCare.ca.gov for licensed providers
- YMCA CRS website for participating providers

**Consider:**
- Family Child Care Homes if centers are full
- License-exempt care with a relative while waiting
- Before/after school programs for school-age kids

### For Military Families

1. **Check MCCYN first:** Fee Assistance program for military
2. **If MCCYN wait is too long:** Apply for CA state subsidies simultaneously
3. **Deployment documentation:** Keep orders handy for income/schedule changes
4. **PCS moves:** Notify your APP immediately; may need to transfer to new county

## Special Populations

### Spanish-Speaking Families

San Diego has excellent Spanish-language services:
- YMCA CRS has bilingual staff
- Many Family Resource Centers have Spanish-speaking caseworkers
- South County providers often are bilingual

### Immigrant Families

- Child's eligibility is based on the CHILD's status, not the parent's
- San Diego agencies do NOT share information with immigration enforcement
- Documentation alternatives exist if you lack standard ID

### Homeless Families

- Can use shelter letter as proof of residence
- San Diego Regional Task Force on the Homeless can provide support letters
- Priority may apply for subsidized childcare

### Children with Disabilities

- Connect with San Diego Regional Center: (858) 576-2996
- YMCA CRS can help identify inclusive providers
- Higher reimbursement rates may be available for specialized care

---

## Documentation Requirements

San Diego follows state requirements, but be prepared for:

**Standard Requirements:**
- ID for all adults
- Birth certificates for children
- Social Security cards (or application receipt)
- Proof of residence (utility bill, lease)
- Income verification (pay stubs, tax returns)
- Work/school schedule

**San Diego-Specific:**
- Military families: copy of orders, LES (Leave and Earnings Statement)
- Self-employed: current Profit & Loss statement
- Gig workers: bank statements + declaration
