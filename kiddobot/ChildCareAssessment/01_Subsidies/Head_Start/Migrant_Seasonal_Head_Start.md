# Migrant and Seasonal Head Start (MSHS)

**Purpose:** Understand Head Start services specifically designed for California farmworker families.

---

## What Is Migrant and Seasonal Head Start?

**Migrant and Seasonal Head Start (MSHS)** is a specialized Head Start program serving children of farmworker families. Established in 1969, MSHS adapts to the unique needs of agricultural workers, including seasonal schedules, mobility, and language.

### Quick Facts

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Ages served** | 6 weeks to 5 years |
| **Cost** | **FREE** |
| **Schedule** | Aligned with harvest seasons (10 weeks to 7 months) |
| **Hours** | Extended, evening, and weekend options |
| **Language** | Bilingual (Spanish-English common) |

---

## Who Qualifies?

### Migrant vs. Seasonal Definition

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Migrant** | Family travels across county lines for agricultural work |
| **Seasonal** | Family works in agriculture but stays in one area |

### Eligibility Requirements

To qualify, families must meet **all four criteria**:

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Age** | Child is 6 weeks to 5 years old |
| **Work type** | At least 50% of family income from agriculture |
| **Income** | Family income at or below federal poverty level |
| **Status** | Migrant (travels) or seasonal (local) farmworker |

### What Counts as Agricultural Work?

| Included | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Field work | Planting, harvesting, irrigation |
| Dairy | Milking, farm operations |
| Packing/processing | Packing houses, canneries |
| Nursery | Plant nurseries, greenhouses |
| Livestock | Ranching, meat processing |
| Fishing | Commercial fishing operations |

### Exceptions

| Category | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| **10% disability slots** | Children with disabilities, any income |
| **Over-income exception** | Up to 10% of enrollment can be above poverty |
| **Homeless farmworkers** | Priority enrollment |

---

## MSHS vs. Regular Head Start

| Feature | MSHS | Regular Head Start |
|---------|------|-------------------|
| **Target population** | Farmworker families | General low-income |
| **Schedule** | Harvest-aligned (seasonal) | School-year based |
| **Duration** | 10 weeks to 7 months | 9+ months |
| **Hours** | Extended/evening available | Typically daytime |
| **Language** | Strong bilingual focus | Varies |
| **Transportation** | Commonly provided | Sometimes provided |
| **Infant care** | Often included | Less common |

---

## Special Services for Farmworker Families

### Flexible Scheduling

| Feature | How It Helps |
|---------|--------------|
| **Harvest-aligned calendar** | Opens when work begins, closes when it ends |
| **Extended day care** | 10+ hours during peak season |
| **Evening/weekend hours** | Accommodates field schedules |
| **Flexible enrollment** | Join mid-season as families arrive |

### Transportation

Many MSHS programs provide:
- Bus pickup from home or central location
- Transportation to and from childcare
- Help with emergency transportation needs

### Bilingual Services

| Service | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Instruction** | Dual-language (Spanish-English) |
| **Staff** | Bilingual teachers and family workers |
| **Materials** | Books and activities in home language |
| **Parent communication** | In preferred language |

### Family Support

| Service | What's Provided |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Health screenings** | Medical, dental, vision, hearing |
| **Nutrition** | Meals meeting USDA standards |
| **Immigration resources** | Referrals to legal services |
| **Benefits enrollment** | Help accessing CalFresh, Medi-Cal, WIC |
| **Housing assistance** | Emergency and transitional resources |

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Find Your Local Program

- Contact programs above based on where you live/work
- Call **Head Start California:** [headstartca.org](https://headstartca.org)
- Ask your employer or labor contractor about local programs
- Check with community health centers

### Step 2: Gather Documentation

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Proof of agricultural work | Pay stubs, employer letter, H-2A visa |
| Income verification | Tax return, pay stubs |
| Child's birth certificate | Age verification |
| Immunization records | Health compliance |
| Proof of address | Even temporary housing counts |

### Step 3: Complete Enrollment

| Step | What Happens |
|------|--------------|
| **Interview** | Meet with family worker |
| **Eligibility screening** | Verify agricultural work and income |
| **Health assessment** | Developmental and health screenings |
| **Start care** | Begin attending as slots available |

## When Programs Operate

### Typical Season Schedule

| Region | Peak Season | Typical Duration |
|--------|-------------|------------------|
| **Imperial Valley** | January - May | ~5 months |
| **Central Valley** | March - October | ~7 months |
| **Salinas/Coastal** | April - November | ~7 months |
| **North Coast** | June - October | ~4 months |

*Schedules vary by program and crop cycle.*

### What Happens Between Seasons?

| Option | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Transfer** | Move to MSHS program in new location |
| **Regular Head Start** | Enroll in year-round Head Start |
| **Records transfer** | Information follows the child |
| **Continuous enrollment** | Some programs operate year-round |

---

## If You're Moving

MSHS programs help families who move:

| Service | What's Provided |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Records transfer** | Health, education info sent to new program |
| **Referrals** | Connected to programs in new area |
| **Coordination** | Staff communicate to ease transition |
| **Portability** | Eligibility follows the family |
