# Early Head Start Program Overview

**Purpose:** Understand how Early Head Start serves pregnant women and infants/toddlers from low-income families.

---

## What Is Early Head Start?

**Early Head Start (EHS)** is the infant-toddler component of the federal Head Start program, providing comprehensive child development and family support services from pregnancy through age 3.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                 EARLY HEAD START TIMELINE                   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PREGNANCY  →  BIRTH  →  INFANT  →  TODDLER  →  HEAD START │
│  Prenatal      0-12 mo    1-2 yrs    2-3 yrs     3-5 yrs   │
│  services                                        transition │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Quick Facts

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Ages served** | Birth to 3 years |
| **Also serves** | Pregnant women |
| **Cost to families** | **FREE** |
| **Focus** | Early brain development, parent-child attachment |
| **Settings** | Center-based, home-based, or combination |

---

## Why Early Head Start Matters

The first three years of life are critical for brain development. EHS focuses on:

| Focus Area | Why It Matters |
|------------|----------------|
| **Attachment** | Secure relationships support all future learning |
| **Language** | 80% of brain development happens by age 3 |
| **Social-emotional** | Foundation for behavior and relationships |
| **Health** | Early screenings catch issues early |
| **Family well-being** | Healthy parents = healthy children |

## Program Options

### Center-Based Early Head Start

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Setting** | Licensed childcare center |
| **Hours** | Full-day (6-10 hours) typical |
| **Ratio** | 1:4 (adult to child) — among the best in the industry |
| **Group size** | Maximum 8 infants/toddlers |
| **Best for** | Working parents, children who thrive in groups |

### Home-Based Early Head Start

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Setting** | Your home |
| **Format** | Weekly 1.5-hour home visits |
| **Additional** | Monthly group socializations |
| **Focus** | Parent as primary teacher |
| **Best for** | Parents who want hands-on involvement |

### Combination Option

Some programs offer both:
- Center-based care some days
- Home visits on other days
- Flexibility for varying schedules

## Services for Pregnant Women

EHS begins support before baby arrives:

| Service | What's Provided |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Prenatal education** | Healthy pregnancy information |
| **Health connections** | Link to prenatal care, WIC |
| **Mental health** | Support for perinatal mood disorders |
| **Fatherhood engagement** | Involving both parents from the start |
| **Birth planning** | Preparation for labor and delivery |
| **Postpartum support** | Transition to parenting |

---

## Services for Infants and Toddlers

### Child Development

| Service | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Developmental screenings** | Within 45 days of enrollment |
| **Individualized care** | Plans based on each child's needs |
| **Nurturing environment** | Safe, stimulating settings |
| **Play-based learning** | Age-appropriate activities |
| **Language support** | Talking, reading, singing |

### Health Services

| Service | What's Included |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Well-child exams** | Tracking growth and development |
| **Immunizations** | Ensuring up-to-date vaccines |
| **Dental** | First dental visit by age 1 |
| **Vision & hearing** | Screenings |
| **Special needs** | Referrals and support |

### Nutrition

| Service | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Breastfeeding support** | Encouragement and assistance |
| **Formula/food** | Provided at center-based programs |
| **Nutrition education** | For parents |
| **Feeding support** | Addressing feeding challenges |

## Family Services

EHS invests heavily in supporting the whole family:

| Service | What's Offered |
|---------|----------------|
| **Family goal-setting** | Education, employment, housing |
| **Parent education** | Child development, parenting skills |
| **Mental health** | Individual and family support |
| **Emergency assistance** | Crisis intervention, referrals |
| **Community resources** | CalFresh, Medi-Cal, WIC enrollment |
| **Parent leadership** | Policy Council participation |

### Parent-Staff Partnership

- **Family Partnership Agreement:** Collaborative goal-setting
- **Home visits:** Even center-based families get some home visits
- **Parent meetings:** Connect with other families
- **Volunteering:** Opportunities to contribute to the program

---

## Transition to Head Start

When children turn 3, EHS helps families transition:

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Early planning** | Begins 6 months before 3rd birthday |
| **Family meeting** | Discuss options (Head Start, preschool, other) |
| **Records transfer** | Developmental information shared |
| **Visit new program** | Child and family orientation |
| **Follow-up** | Ensuring smooth adjustment |

## Who Is Eligible?

### Automatic Eligibility

| Category | Documentation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Family income ≤ federal poverty level** | Tax return, pay stubs |
| **Receiving CalWORKs (TANF)** | Benefit verification |
| **Receiving SSI** | Award letter |
| **Foster child** | Placement documentation |
| **Experiencing homelessness** | Self-declaration |

### Priority Categories

Programs must reserve seats for:
- **10% for children with disabilities**
- Children experiencing homelessness
- Children in foster care

### Over-Income Families

Programs may enroll families above income limits when:
- Slots remain after serving priority families
- Child has documented disability
- Other compelling circumstances

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Find Programs

- **Head Start California:** [headstartca.org](https://headstartca.org)
- **ECLKC Locator:** Search "Early Head Start"
- **Local CCR&R:** Ask specifically about EHS

### Step 2: Contact Early

EHS has limited slots — apply as soon as possible:
- Many programs accept applications year-round
- Apply during pregnancy for earliest enrollment
- Waitlists are common, especially for center-based

### Step 3: Gather Documents

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Income verification | Eligibility determination |
| Child's birth certificate | Age verification |
| Immunization records | Health tracking |
| Proof of address | Service area verification |
| Proof of pregnancy | For prenatal enrollment |

## EHS vs. Other Infant Care Options

| Feature | Early Head Start | Licensed Center | Family Child Care |
|---------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Cost** | FREE | $1,200-$2,500/mo | $900-$1,800/mo |
| **Ratio** | 1:4 | 1:4 (Title 22) | Varies |
| **Family services** | Comprehensive | Minimal | Minimal |
| **Health services** | Included | Not typically | Not typically |
| **Income requirement** | Yes (low-income) | No | No |
