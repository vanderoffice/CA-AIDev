# California Childcare Provider Types

**Purpose:** Understand your childcare options — from licensed centers to informal family care.

---

## Quick Comparison

| Provider Type | Setting | Max Children | Regulation | Best For |
|--------------|---------|--------------|------------|----------|
| **Licensed Child Care Center** | Commercial building | Varies (50-200+) | Title 22 or Title 5 | Structured environment, socialization |
| **Large Family Child Care Home** | Provider's home | 9-14 children | Title 22 | Home setting with small groups |
| **Small Family Child Care Home** | Provider's home | Up to 8 children | Title 22 | Intimate care, flexible hours |
| **License-Exempt (FFN)** | Your home or relative's | 1 family only* | TrustLine optional | Flexibility, family caregivers |
| **Head Start** | Various | Program-dependent | Federal + Title 5 | Low-income families, comprehensive services |
| **State Preschool (CSPP)** | Schools/centers | Class-size based | Title 5 | Ages 3-5, income-eligible families |
| **Transitional Kindergarten** | Public schools | Class-size limits | Education Code | 4-year-olds, free, school-based |

*License-exempt providers can care for children from ONE other family only (in addition to their own).

## Licensed Child Care Centers

### What They Are

Large programs operating in commercial buildings (not homes) with multiple classrooms, structured curricula, and separate areas for different age groups.

### Key Features

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Ages served** | Infants through school-age (varies by center) |
| **Hours** | Typically 6 AM–6 PM weekdays |
| **Staff qualifications** | ECE units + experience required |
| **Adult-child ratios** | Strictly regulated by age group |
| **Space requirements** | 35 sq ft indoor + 75 sq ft outdoor per child |

### Regulation Types

**Title 22 Centers (Private/Voucher)**
- Meet minimum California licensing standards
- Can accept subsidy vouchers or private pay
- Ratios: 1:4 (infants), 1:12 (preschool)

**Title 5 Centers (State-Subsidized)**
- Meet BOTH Title 22 AND stricter Title 5 standards
- Serve families receiving state subsidies
- Better ratios: 1:3 (infants), 1:8 (preschool)
- Higher teacher qualifications

### Pros and Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Structured learning environment | Less flexibility in hours |
| Multiple teachers/backup staff | May feel less personal |
| Consistent curriculum | Exposure to more illnesses |
| Licensed and inspected | Typically more expensive |
| Good for socialization | Waitlists common |

## Licensed Family Child Care Homes

### What They Are

Care provided in the provider's own home, creating a family-like setting with mixed-age groups and home activities.

### Small Family Child Care Home

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Capacity** | Up to 8 children |
| **Staffing** | One provider |
| **Ages** | Mixed ages typical |
| **Provider's own children** | Count toward capacity if under 10 |

### Large Family Child Care Home

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Capacity** | 9-14 children |
| **Staffing** | Provider + assistant (when over 8 children) |
| **Space** | More room required |
| **Ages** | Mixed ages typical |

### Pros and Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Home-like environment | Single provider (no backup if sick) |
| Smaller group sizes | Less structured curriculum |
| Often more flexible hours | Limited space/resources |
| Mixed-age socialization | Less supervision oversight |
| May offer nontraditional hours | Provider's own children present |

## License-Exempt Care (FFN)

### What It Is

Informal childcare arrangements that don't require state licensing. Includes:

- **Nannies/babysitters** in YOUR home
- **Relatives** (grandparents, aunts, uncles, adult siblings)
- **Family friends or neighbors** (limited to ONE other family)

### When a License Is NOT Required

```
Your own children ONLY → No license needed
Care by a relative → No license needed
Children from ONE other family → No license needed
In the child's own home → No license needed
```

### When a License IS Required

```
Children from TWO or more unrelated families → License REQUIRED
Operating a "business" serving multiple families → License REQUIRED
```

**Important:** Operating without a license when required is a misdemeanor with fines of $200/day.

### TrustLine Registry

For license-exempt providers, **TrustLine** provides background checks using:
- California DOJ Criminal History System
- Child Abuse Central Index
- FBI Criminal History System

**Verify a provider:** Call 1-800-822-8490 or visit [trustline.org](https://trustline.org)

**Note:** TrustLine is optional for family members but required for license-exempt providers receiving subsidy payments.

### Pros and Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Maximum flexibility | No state oversight |
| Familiar caregiver | No required training |
| One-on-one attention | No backup care |
| Often most affordable | Quality varies widely |
| Cultural/language match | Parents must verify safety |

## School-Age Programs

### After School Education and Safety (ASES)

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Grades** | TK–9th grade |
| **Hours** | School dismissal to 6 PM |
| **Funding** | Proposition 49 (2002) |
| **Focus** | Literacy, academic enrichment, safe alternatives |
| **Cost** | Free or low-cost |
| **Location** | School campuses |

### 21st Century Community Learning Centers

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Grades** | TK–9th grade (ASSETs for high school) |
| **Funding** | Federal (Every Student Succeeds Act) |
| **Focus** | Academic support, especially for low-performing schools |
| **Services** | Tutoring, enrichment, college prep |

### How to Find School-Age Programs

1. Contact your child's school directly
2. Ask the school district's after-school coordinator
3. Search [afterschoolalliance.org](https://www.afterschoolalliance.org)
4. Contact your local CCR&R agency

## Head Start & Early Head Start

### What They Are

Federally funded programs providing comprehensive child development services to low-income families.

| Program | Ages Served | Services |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Early Head Start** | Birth to 3 years | Home visiting, center-based, combination |
| **Head Start** | 3-5 years | Center-based preschool |

### More Than Just Childcare

Head Start provides:
- Education and developmental services
- Health screenings and referrals
- Nutrition (meals meeting federal standards)
- Family support services
- Parent engagement opportunities

### Eligibility

- Family income at or below federal poverty level
- Children in foster care
- Families receiving TANF/CalWORKs or SSI
- Homeless families
- Some seats for above-income families

### How to Apply

1. Find your local Head Start: [headstartca.org](https://headstartca.org)
2. Call California Head Start Collaboration Office: 916-263-1718
3. Contact your local CCR&R agency

## California State Preschool Program (CSPP)

### What It Is

State-funded preschool for income-eligible families, operated at schools and childcare centers.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Ages** | 3-5 years |
| **Hours** | Part-day or full-day options |
| **Schedule** | School year (typically) |
| **Income limit** | At or below 100% State Median Income |
| **Cost** | Subsidized (fees based on income) |

### Focus Areas

- Literacy and language development
- Number and math concepts
- Social-emotional skills
- Health and safety awareness
- Kindergarten readiness

### How to Find CSPP

1. Contact your local school district
2. Search [Find Child Care](https://rrnetwork.org/family-services/find-child-care)
3. Call your local CCR&R agency

## Transitional Kindergarten (TK)

### What It Is

The first year of a two-year kindergarten program in California public schools. **Free for all eligible children.**

### 2025-26 Eligibility

As of 2025-26, ALL children who turn 4 by September 1 are eligible.

| School Year | Eligible Birth Dates |
|-------------|---------------------|
| 2025-26 | Turn 4 by September 1, 2025 |

### Key Features

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Location** | Public school campuses |
| **Cost** | FREE |
| **Hours** | School day (varies by district) |
| **Ratio** | 1:12 adult-to-child (state target: 1:10) |
| **Curriculum** | Developmentally appropriate, kindergarten prep |

### How to Enroll

1. Contact your local school district
2. Register during open enrollment (usually spring)
3. Provide proof of age and residency

### TK vs. Preschool

| TK | Preschool |
|----|-----------|
| Free | May have fees |
| School hours only | Extended hours available |
| School calendar | Year-round options |
| No income requirements | May be income-based |
| Credentialed teachers | ECE-trained staff |

## Understanding Title 22 vs. Title 5

### Quick Comparison

| Standard | Title 22 | Title 5 |
|----------|----------|---------|
| **Applies to** | All licensed programs | State-subsidized programs |
| **Teacher ECE units** | 12 units + 6 mo. experience | 24 units + 16 GE units |
| **Infant ratio** | 1:4 | 1:3 |
| **Preschool ratio** | 1:12 | 1:8 |
| **Quality level** | Minimum standard | Higher standard |

### What This Means for Parents

- **Title 5 programs** have more teachers per child
- **Title 5 programs** have more qualified teachers
- **Title 5 programs** typically serve subsidized families
- **Private programs** usually operate under Title 22 only

## Choosing the Right Provider Type

### Consider Your Child's Needs

```
Does your child thrive in structured environments?
→ Licensed center or school-based program

Does your child need one-on-one attention?
→ Small family child care or nanny

Does your child have developmental delays?
→ Head Start or inclusive center (coordinate with Regional Center)

Is your child shy or overwhelmed by large groups?
→ Small or large family child care home
```

### Consider Your Family's Needs

```
Do you need flexible or nontraditional hours?
→ Family child care or license-exempt care

Do you need help with other family services?
→ Head Start (comprehensive services)

Is cost your primary concern?
→ TK (free), Head Start, or subsidized programs

Do you want educational curriculum?
→ Licensed center, CSPP, or TK
```

### Questions to Ask Any Provider

1. What is your license type and number?
2. What are your adult-to-child ratios?
3. What are your teachers' qualifications?
4. What is your daily schedule/curriculum?
5. How do you handle discipline?
6. What is your sick child policy?
7. How do you communicate with parents?
8. Can I visit anytime during operating hours?
9. Do you accept subsidies/vouchers?
10. What is your emergency plan?
