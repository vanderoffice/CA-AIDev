## Types of School-Age Programs

### On-Site School Programs

| Program Type | Provider | Typical Hours | Cost |
|--------------|----------|---------------|------|
| ASES (After School Education & Safety) | School district | After school until 6 PM | FREE |
| 21st Century CCLC | School/community partner | After school + summer | FREE |
| School-run extended care | School district | Before and/or after | $200-600/month |
| PTA/PTO programs | Parent organizations | Varies | Varies |

### Community-Based Programs

| Provider | Ages | Features |
|----------|------|----------|
| Boys & Girls Clubs | 6-18 | Low-cost, comprehensive programming |
| YMCA/YWCA | 5-12+ | Before/after school, camps |
| Parks & Recreation | 5-12 | Seasonal programs, camps |
| 4-H | 5-18 | Project-based learning |
| Police Athletic League | Varies | Sports, mentorship |

### Private Licensed Centers

| Type | Features | Cost Range |
|------|----------|------------|
| Licensed childcare centers | Full regulation, trained staff | $400-1,000/month |
| Licensed school-age programs | Dedicated school-age focus | $300-800/month |
| Enrichment centers | Academic focus, tutoring | $500-1,500/month |

### License-Exempt Care (FFN)

Family, friends, and neighbors providing care before/after school.
- No licensing required
- Must be TrustLine registered for subsidies
- Flexible, often lower cost

## Free Programs: ASES & 21st Century

### ASES (After School Education and Safety Program)

**What it is:** State-funded after-school program providing homework help, enrichment, and physical activity.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Cost | **FREE** |
| Grades | K-9 |
| Hours | End of school until 6 PM |
| Days | School days only |
| Location | On school campus |

**What's included:**
- Homework assistance
- Academic tutoring
- Physical activity (30 minutes minimum)
- Nutritional snack
- Enrichment activities

**Who's eligible:** Any student at a participating school (priority to schools with high free/reduced lunch rates).

**How to enroll:** Contact your child's school office.

### 21st Century Community Learning Centers (21st CCLC)

**What it is:** Federal program providing after-school and summer programming.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Cost | **FREE** |
| Grades | K-12 |
| Hours | After school + summer options |
| Days | School days + summer (some programs) |
| Location | Schools and community sites |

**Additional features:**
- Summer programming (some sites)
- Family literacy activities
- STEM enrichment
- Arts and recreation

**How to find:** Ask your school or search [CDE After School Programs](https://www.cde.ca.gov/ls/ex/ast/)

## Before-School Care

### Options

| Option | Typical Hours | Cost |
|--------|---------------|------|
| School-run program | 6:30-8:00 AM | $100-300/month |
| Community program | 6:30-8:00 AM | $100-400/month |
| Licensed center | 6:00-8:30 AM | Included in full rate |
| FFN (family/friends) | Flexible | Variable |

### Considerations

- **Transportation:** Does program transport to school?
- **Breakfast:** Is breakfast provided?
- **Arrival time:** Does it match your work start time?
- **Location:** Near school or on your commute?

## Subsidies for School-Age Care

### Who Qualifies

School-age care subsidies follow the same rules as early childhood:
- Income under 85% State Median Income
- Work/training activity requirement
- Child under age 13

### Programs That Cover School-Age

| Program | School-Age Eligible? | Notes |
|---------|---------------------|-------|
| CalWORKs Stages 1-3 | Yes, through age 12 | Same as preschool |
| APP | Yes, through age 12 | Same eligibility |
| CSPP | No | Preschool only |
| Head Start | No | Through age 5 |
| ASES/21st Century | Yes | Free, no income test |

### Applying for School-Age Subsidies

1. Contact your local CCR&R or APP contractor
2. Verify your child's age is covered (under 13)
3. Submit same documentation as preschool care
4. Get authorization for specific school-age provider

### Subsidy + Free Programs

**You can combine:**
- ASES (free, school days) + subsidized summer care
- 21st CCLC (free, after school) + subsidized before-school
- Free programs don't affect subsidy eligibility

## Children with Disabilities (Ages 5-12)

### Additional Options

| Program | What It Offers |
|---------|----------------|
| Regional Center | May fund after-school care/respite |
| Special Recreation | Adaptive programs through parks |
| Extended School Year | Summer services for IEP students |
| Specialized camps | Disability-focused programming |

### Inclusion Rights

School-age programs must:
- Make reasonable accommodations
- Modify activities for participation
- Not exclude based on disability
- Coordinate with IEP if relevant

### Finding Inclusive Programs

1. Ask Regional Center for recommendations
2. Contact CCR&R for experienced providers
3. Check if school's ASES has inclusion support
4. Look for programs with disability experience

## Evaluating School-Age Programs

### Quality Checklist

| Category | What to Look For |
|----------|------------------|
| **Staff** | Training, ratios (1:10-1:15 typical), background checks |
| **Environment** | Age-appropriate space, safety measures |
| **Programming** | Balance of homework, activities, free play |
| **Policies** | Discipline, communication, emergency procedures |
| **Transportation** | Safe pickup from school, parent handoff |

### Questions to Ask

**About care:**
- What's the daily schedule?
- How is homework time structured?
- What activities are offered?
- How much outdoor/physical time?

**About safety:**
- How do children get from school to program?
- What's the pickup procedure?
- How do you handle emergencies?
- What's the sick child policy?

**About communication:**
- How will I know how my child's day was?
- How do you handle behavior issues?
- How can I reach you during program hours?

- Know emergency transport protocols

## Middle School (Ages 11-14)

### Changing Needs

As children enter middle school:
- Less structured supervision may be appropriate
- Self-care ("latchkey") becomes option
- Teen programs focus on enrichment, not childcare
- Subsidies end at age 13 for most programs

### Middle School Options

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Teen centers | Drop-in programs, less structured |
| Sports leagues | After-school activities |
| Tutoring/homework clubs | Academic focus |
| 21st CCLC | Continues through grade 9 |
| Self-care | Home alone with check-ins |

### Self-Care Readiness

Consider if your child can:
- Follow safety rules consistently
- Handle minor emergencies
- Reach you by phone
- Complete homework independently
- Stay home alone for needed duration
