# Sacramento County Childcare Guide

**Population:** ~1.5 million
**Region:** 2 (Moderate Cost of Living)
**Primary Agency:** Sacramento County Department of Human Assistance (DHA)

---

## Overview

Sacramento County serves the state capital region, including Sacramento, Elk Grove, Folsom, and Citrus Heights. As Region 2, childcare costs are moderate compared to Bay Area and LA, with generally shorter waitlists. The county has strong state worker access to benefits and a well-organized subsidy system.

## County-Specific Features

### State Worker Benefits

Many Sacramento residents are state employees with access to:
- **FlexElect (DCFSA):** Pre-tax childcare savings
- **State backup care programs:** Through CalHR benefits
- **Sacramento 2-1-1:** State worker-specific resources

**Note:** State workers still qualify for public subsidies if income-eligible.

SETA (Sacramento Employment & Training Agency) operates comprehensive Head Start across the county with many sites.

## Application Process

### CalWORKs (Stages 1-3)

```
STAGE 1 (While on Cash Aid)
──────────────────────────
1. Apply for CalWORKs at DHA
2. Meet with employment services
3. Request childcare with your worker
4. Complete CW 101 form
5. Choose provider
6. DHA authorizes Stage 1

STAGE 2/3 (After Cash Aid)
──────────────────────────
1. DHA refers you to Child Action
2. Child Action contacts you (1-2 weeks)
3. Submit updated documentation
4. Continue care (same or new provider)
5. Annual recertification with Child Action
```

### Non-CalWORKs Subsidies

1. Contact Child Action: (916) 369-0191
2. Complete application online or in-person
3. Submit income and work documentation
4. Get placed on Centralized Eligibility List
5. Wait for slot (typically 2-6 months)
6. Choose approved provider when selected

## Tips for Sacramento County

### Finding Providers

1. **Start with Child Action:** They maintain comprehensive provider lists
2. **Check school district programs:** Many districts have pre-K
3. **SETA Head Start:** Multiple locations, free for qualifying families
4. **State worker connections:** Some state buildings have on-site or nearby care

### Navigating the System

- **Centralized system:** Child Action handles most programs
- **Moderate wait times:** Generally easier than Bay Area or LA
- **Bilingual services:** Spanish and other languages available
- **In-person help:** Child Action has walk-in hours

### Quality Indicators

- Quality Counts California rated providers
- NAEYC and NAFCC accreditation
- Child Action quality improvement programs
