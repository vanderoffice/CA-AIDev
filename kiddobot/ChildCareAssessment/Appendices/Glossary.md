## State Agencies

### CDSS — California Department of Social Services
The state agency overseeing social services including CalWORKs, child welfare, foster care, and subsidized childcare programs. CDSS administers CCDF funding and oversees the Community Care Licensing Division (CCLD).
- **Website:** [cdss.ca.gov](https://cdss.ca.gov)

### CDE — California Department of Education
The state agency managing public education, including preschool programs like CSPP, Title 5 childcare funding, and Transitional Kindergarten.
- **Website:** [cde.ca.gov](https://www.cde.ca.gov)

### CDPH — California Department of Public Health
The state agency regulating public health standards, including immunization requirements for licensed childcare facilities and the CAIR-ME immunization registry.
- **Website:** [cdph.ca.gov](https://www.cdph.ca.gov)

### CCLD — Community Care Licensing Division
The division within CDSS responsible for licensing and overseeing childcare facilities, family child care homes, and community care programs. Administers Title 22 regulations.
- **License lookup:** [ccld.dss.ca.gov/carefacilitysearch](https://www.ccld.dss.ca.gov/carefacilitysearch/)

### DDS — Department of Developmental Services
California state agency coordinating services for individuals with developmental disabilities through 21 Regional Centers. Oversees Early Start program for infants/toddlers.
- **Website:** [dds.ca.gov](https://dds.ca.gov)

## Federal Programs

### CCDF — Child Care Development Fund
Federal block grant funding state childcare subsidies for low-income working families. California allocates CCDF through programs like Alternative Payment Programs (APPs). Administered by CDSS as California's lead agency.

### TANF — Temporary Assistance for Needy Families
Federal cash assistance program implemented in California as CalWORKs, supporting low-income families with children. Provides foundation for CalWORKs childcare (Stages 1-3).

### Head Start / Early Head Start
Federally funded comprehensive early childhood development programs providing free education, health, nutrition, and family support services:
- **Head Start:** Ages 3-5
- **Early Head Start:** Infants, toddlers, and pregnant women

Administered locally by California grantees (non-profits, school districts) through the federal Office of Head Start.

### IDEA — Individuals with Disabilities Education Act
Federal law ensuring free appropriate public education (FAPE) for children with disabilities ages 3-21, including early intervention and special education services in childcare/preschool contexts.

### SNAP — Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program
Federal food assistance program (formerly food stamps) providing EBT card benefits to low-income households. SNAP receipt can indicate eligibility for childcare subsidies.

### VFC — Vaccines for Children
Federal program providing no-cost vaccines to eligible children (birth through 18) who are Medicaid-eligible, uninsured, underinsured, or American Indian/Alaska Native. Ensures childcare immunization requirements can be met regardless of income.

## California Programs

### CalWORKs — California Work Opportunity and Responsibility to Kids
California's implementation of federal TANF, offering cash aid, childcare subsidies (Stages 1-3), and employment services to needy families. Administered by county welfare departments.

**CalWORKs Childcare Stages:**
- **Stage 1:** For families currently receiving CalWORKs cash aid (entitlement)
- **Stage 2:** For families transitioning off cash aid (entitlement)
- **Stage 3:** For former CalWORKs families (fund-dependent)

### CSPP — California State Preschool Program
State-funded preschool program offering early education to income-eligible children ages 3-5. Promotes school readiness through part-day or full-day programs. Administered by CDE under Title 5 standards.

### TK — Transitional Kindergarten
Free public school program bridging preschool to kindergarten. As of 2025-26, all children turning 4 by September 1 are eligible. Administered by school districts under CDE.

### ASES — After School Education and Safety
California state-funded program offering free afterschool academic enrichment, nutrition, and supervision for children in elementary/middle schools. Funded through Proposition 49 (2002).

### GAIN — Greater Avenues for Independence
California's welfare-to-work program under CalWORKs, providing job training, education, and employment services to cash aid recipients.

## Subsidy & Eligibility Terms

### APP — Alternative Payment Program
California program providing childcare subsidies to eligible families through contracts with local agencies. APPs administer CalWORKs Stages 2 & 3 and serve families using FFN (informal) care.

### SMI — State Median Income
Benchmark used to determine eligibility for California childcare and social services programs. Key thresholds:
- **85% SMI:** Maximum income for most subsidized childcare
- **75% SMI:** Threshold for family fee exemption
- **100% SMI:** CSPP priority enrollment limit

Income limits updated annually via CDSS Management Bulletins.

### RMR — Regional Market Rate
Survey-based rate cap for reimbursing licensed childcare providers, varying by region to reflect local costs. California uses RMR surveys to set maximum subsidy payment rates.

### MAP — Maximum Aid Payment
The highest monthly cash aid amount for CalWORKs families, adjusted by family size. Used in certain eligibility calculations.

### MBSAC — Minimum Basic Standard of Adequate Care
Income threshold used in CalWORKs eligibility calculations, representing minimum needs for families.

## Licensing & Regulation

### Title 22
California Code of Regulations administered by CCLD/CDSS governing licensed childcare facilities. Sets minimum health and safety standards for private licensed care. Key ratios:
- Infants (0-18 mo): 1:4
- Preschool: 1:12

### Title 5
California Education Code regulations administered by CDE for education-focused childcare and preschool programs (e.g., CSPP). Stricter standards than Title 22:
- Infants (0-18 mo): 1:3
- Preschool: 1:8

Higher teacher qualification requirements.

### FCC / FCCH — Family Child Care / Family Child Care Home
Licensed in-home childcare provided by an individual in their residence:
- **Small FCC:** Up to 8 children
- **Large FCC:** 9-14 children (requires assistant)

### FFN — Family, Friend, and Neighbor Care
Informal, unlicensed childcare provided by relatives or close acquaintances. Can receive subsidy payments through APPs if provider is TrustLine-registered.

### TrustLine
California's background check registry for license-exempt (unlicensed) childcare providers. Screens for criminal history and child abuse records. Required for FFN providers receiving subsidies.
- **Verify a provider:** 1-800-822-8490 or [trustline.org](https://trustline.org)

### LIC Forms
Licensing forms used by CCLD for childcare facility applications, inspections, and operations:
- **LIC 700:** Childcare center application
- **LIC 103:** Criminal record clearance
- **LIC 9182:** Personnel report

## Special Needs Services

### Regional Center
One of 21 nonprofit centers contracted statewide to coordinate services for individuals with developmental disabilities. Regional Centers assess eligibility, develop IPPs, and connect families with services including specialized childcare.
- **Directory:** [dds.ca.gov](https://dds.ca.gov)

### IPP — Individual Program Plan
Personalized plan developed by Regional Centers outlining services, supports, and goals for individuals with developmental disabilities. Updated at least annually.

### IEP — Individualized Education Program
Written plan for children with disabilities (ages 3+) outlining special education services, accommodations, and goals. Relevant for preschool-aged children in inclusive childcare settings.

### Early Start
California's early intervention program for infants and toddlers (birth to 36 months) with disabilities or at risk of developmental delays. Administered through Regional Centers.

## Financial Terms

### FSA / DCFSA — (Dependent Care) Flexible Spending Account
Tax-advantaged employer benefit allowing pre-tax contributions to pay for eligible childcare expenses for children under 13. Annual limit: $5,000 (married filing jointly).

### CDCTC — Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit
Federal non-refundable tax credit (up to 35% of qualifying expenses) for employment-related childcare costs. California offers a smaller conforming state credit.

### SSI — Supplemental Security Income
Federal program providing monthly cash payments to low-income individuals who are aged, blind, or disabled. SSI receipt qualifies children for automatic Head Start eligibility.

## Other Key Terms

### CCR&R — Child Care Resource & Referral
Local networks providing families with childcare referrals, provider support, and resource information. CCR&Rs are the primary entry point for finding childcare in California.
- **Find your local CCR&R:** [rrnetwork.org](https://rrnetwork.org/about/r-r-directory)

### MCCYN — Military Child Care in Your Neighborhood
Federal program subsidizing childcare fees for military families using civilian (non-base) providers in California communities.

### RFA — Resource Family Approval
California's unified process for approving foster, adoptive, or kinship caregivers through assessment, training, and home approval.

### CPS — Child Protective Services
County-level agency (often part of DCFS) responsible for investigating child abuse/neglect reports and providing family services. CPS involvement qualifies families for childcare priority enrollment.

### NAEYC — National Association for the Education of Young Children
National professional organization setting standards and accreditation for high-quality early childhood education programs. NAEYC accreditation indicates higher quality standards.

### ECE — Early Childhood Education
Programs and training focused on learning and development for children birth to age 5.

### CAIR-ME — California Immunization Registry (Medical Exemptions)
The state's electronic registry tracking immunization records, required for childcare enrollment to verify compliance with California immunization requirements.
