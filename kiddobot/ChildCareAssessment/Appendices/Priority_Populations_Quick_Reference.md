## Foster Children & Kinship Care

### Automatic Eligibility

| Program | Income Required? | Documentation |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Head Start** | ❌ NO | Foster placement letter |
| **CSPP** | ❌ NO | Placement documentation |
| **APP subsidies** | ❌ NO | CPS/foster verification |

### Key Points

- **Priority 1** enrollment in all state-subsidized childcare
- Foster care status alone qualifies — caregiver income doesn't matter
- Applies to: licensed foster, kinship, Resource Family (RFA)
- Childcare can be part of court-ordered case plan

### Who to Contact

- **County child welfare agency** (for placement-linked services)
- **Local CCR&R** (for other subsidized care)
- **Head Start grantee** (for Head Start enrollment)

*Full guide: [Foster_Care.md](../05_Special_Situations/Foster_Care.md)*

## Homeless Families

### Your Rights (McKinney-Vento Act)

| Right | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Immediate enrollment** | Cannot delay for documents |
| **No address required** | Shelter address or no address OK |
| **Self-declaration** | You state your status |
| **Transportation** | Help getting to school/childcare |

### Definition of Homeless

You qualify if living in:
- Shelter (emergency or transitional)
- Hotel/motel (due to lack of housing)
- Doubled up with others (due to hardship)
- Car, park, or public space
- Substandard housing (no utilities)

### Automatic Eligibility

| Program | Income Required? | Documentation |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Head Start** | ❌ NO | Self-declaration |
| **CSPP** | ❌ NO | Self-declaration |
| **APP subsidies** | ❌ NO | Self-declaration or shelter letter |

### Who to Contact

- **School district homeless liaison** (every district has one)
- **Local CCR&R** (childcare referrals)
- **211** (dial 2-1-1 for local resources)

*Full guide: [Homeless_Families.md](../05_Special_Situations/Homeless_Families.md)*

## Children with Disabilities

### Priority Enrollment

| Program | Disability Provision |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Head Start** | 10% of slots reserved for children with disabilities |
| **Regional Center** | Specialized childcare funding for eligible children |
| **CSPP** | Priority 2 enrollment |
| **APP** | Priority 2 enrollment |

### Income Exceptions

- Head Start's 10% disability slots can enroll over-income families
- Regional Center services not income-limited
- IEP/IFSP can include childcare as related service

### Who to Contact

- **Regional Center** (21 centers statewide): [dds.ca.gov](https://dds.ca.gov)
- **School district** (for IEP services, ages 3+)
- **Head Start** (for comprehensive early childhood)

*Full guides: [Special_Needs_Providers.md](../02_Provider_Search/Special_Needs_Providers.md), [Regional_Center](../01_Subsidies/Regional_Center/)*

## Military Families

### Available Programs

| Program | Who's Eligible | How It Works |
|---------|----------------|--------------|
| **On-base CDCs** | Active duty, priority order | Apply via MilitaryChildCare.com |
| **MCCYN** | Active duty, Reserve, Guard | Fee assistance for off-base civilian care |
| **State subsidies** | All CA residents meeting criteria | Apply through APP/CCR&R |

### MCCYN (Military Child Care in Your Neighborhood)

- Reduces out-of-pocket costs for civilian childcare
- Can use with licensed providers anywhere in CA
- Each branch has its own program

### Dual Eligibility

Military families can access BOTH:
1. Military programs (MCCYN, CDCs)
2. California state subsidies (if income-eligible)

### Who to Contact

- **MilitaryChildCare.com:** [militarychildcare.csd.disa.mil](https://militarychildcare.csd.disa.mil)
- **Base Family Support Center**
- **Local CCR&R** (for state programs)

*Full guide: [Military_Families.md](../05_Special_Situations/Military_Families.md)*

## Migrant & Seasonal Farmworkers

### Available Programs

| Program | Ages | Key Feature |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **Migrant Head Start** | 6 weeks - 5 years | Harvest-aligned schedules |
| **Migrant Education** | All ages | School support services |
| **APP subsidies** | Birth - 12 | Standard state subsidies |

### Eligibility

- At least 50% of income from agricultural work
- Migrant (travels across counties) OR seasonal (local)
- Income at or below federal poverty level

### Special Services

- Extended/evening hours during harvest
- Transportation often provided
- Bilingual (Spanish-English) services
- Help with benefits enrollment

### Who to Contact

- **Local Migrant Head Start program** (see [Migrant_Seasonal_Head_Start.md](../01_Subsidies/Head_Start/Migrant_Seasonal_Head_Start.md))
- **County Office of Education** (Migrant Education)
- **Community health center**

*Full guide: [Migrant_Farmworker_Families.md](../05_Special_Situations/Migrant_Farmworker_Families.md)*

## What "Priority 1" Actually Means

### In Practice

1. When a childcare slot opens, Priority 1 families are served first
2. Even if other families have been waiting longer
3. You skip ahead of the standard income-based waitlist

### How to Claim Priority

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Tell the agency your qualifying status |
| 2 | Provide documentation (or self-declare for homeless) |
| 3 | Request Priority 1 placement explicitly |
| 4 | Follow up regularly on waitlist status |

### Documentation by Category

| Category | Acceptable Documentation |
|----------|-------------------------|
| **Foster/CPS** | Placement letter, social worker statement |
| **Homeless** | Self-declaration, shelter letter, case manager statement |
| **Disability** | IEP, IFSP, Regional Center letter, doctor's statement |
| **Military** | Military ID, LES (Leave and Earnings Statement) |
| **Migrant** | Employer letter, pay stubs showing agricultural work |
