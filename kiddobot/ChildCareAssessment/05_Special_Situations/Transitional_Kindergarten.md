# Transitional Kindergarten (TK) in California

**Purpose:** Everything parents need to know about California's free TK program for 4-year-olds.

---

## What Is Transitional Kindergarten?

TK is the **first year of a two-year kindergarten program** offered through California public schools.

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Cost** | FREE |
| **Where** | Public elementary schools |
| **Age** | 4-year-olds (turning 5 during school year) |
| **Hours** | 3.5-6 hours/day (varies by district) |
| **Required?** | No — voluntary |

---

## California's Universal TK Expansion

### The Timeline

California is expanding TK to serve ALL 4-year-olds:

| School Year | Who's Eligible |
|-------------|----------------|
| **2022-23** | Children turning 5 between Sept 2 - Feb 2 |
| **2023-24** | Children turning 5 between Sept 2 - April 2 |
| **2024-25** | Children turning 5 between Sept 2 - June 2 |
| **2025-26** | **All children turning 4 by Sept 1** |

### What "Universal" Means

Starting **2025-26**:
- Every 4-year-old is eligible
- No income requirement
- No waitlists (though some areas may phase in)
- Available statewide

---

## 2024-25 Eligibility

For the **2024-25 school year**, children are eligible if they turn 5 between:
- **September 2, 2024** and **June 2, 2025**

| If Your Child's 5th Birthday Is... | TK Eligible? |
|------------------------------------|--------------|
| Before September 2, 2024 | ❌ No (eligible for Kindergarten) |
| September 2, 2024 - June 2, 2025 | ✅ Yes |
| After June 2, 2025 | ❌ Not yet (eligible 2025-26) |

---

## 2025-26 Eligibility (Universal TK)

For the **2025-26 school year**, children are eligible if they turn 4 by September 1, 2025.

| If Your Child's 4th Birthday Is... | TK Eligible 2025-26? |
|------------------------------------|---------------------|
| September 1, 2025 or before | ✅ Yes |
| After September 1, 2025 | ❌ Not yet |

## TK vs. Other Preschool Options

### TK vs. CSPP (State Preschool)

| Feature | TK | CSPP |
|---------|-----|------|
| **Cost** | Free | Free (if eligible) |
| **Income requirement** | None | Up to 100% SMI |
| **Hours** | 3.5-6 hours/day | Up to 10+ hours/day |
| **Days** | School days only | Year-round options |
| **Ages** | 4-5 | 3-5 |
| **Location** | Public schools | Schools + contractors |

### TK vs. Head Start

| Feature | TK | Head Start |
|---------|-----|------------|
| **Cost** | Free | Free |
| **Income requirement** | None | Federal poverty level |
| **Hours** | 3.5-6 hours | Varies (half to full day) |
| **Services** | Education | Education + health + family services |
| **Ages** | 4-5 | 3-5 (Early Head Start: 0-3) |

### Key Difference: Hours

**TK programs typically run 3.5-6 hours per day** — similar to regular school hours.

For working families, this may not provide enough coverage. Options include:
- Before/after school programs
- CSPP wraparound care
- Private after-school programs

## Do I Have to Enroll My Child in TK?

**No.** TK is voluntary.

You can choose:
1. **Enroll in TK** — Free public school option
2. **Continue in preschool** — If currently enrolled
3. **Enroll in CSPP** — If income-eligible and prefer full-day
4. **Enroll in Head Start** — If income-eligible
5. **Keep child home** — Your choice
6. **Private preschool** — If you prefer

### Why Some Families Choose TK

- Free
- School-based (familiar environment for kindergarten)
- Credentialed teachers
- Prepares for kindergarten expectations
- No income verification

### Why Some Families Choose Other Options

- Need full-day care (TK often part-day)
- Prefer specific curriculum or approach
- Child thrives in smaller, home-like setting
- Transportation is easier with current provider
- Already enrolled in program they love

## What TK Covers

### Curriculum

TK uses a modified kindergarten curriculum focused on:
- Social-emotional development
- Language and literacy
- Math foundations
- Physical development
- Creative expression

### Teacher Qualifications

TK teachers must have:
- Teaching credential
- Early childhood education training (as of August 2025)
- Either: Child Development Permit or 24 ECE units

### Class Size

| Year | Adult:Student Ratio |
|------|---------------------|
| 2024-25 | 1:12 |
| 2025-26 | 1:10 (proposed) |

## Enrollment Process

### Step 1: Find Your School

TK is typically offered at:
- Your neighborhood elementary school
- Designated TK centers (some districts)

**How to find it:**
1. Visit your school district's website
2. Call the district enrollment office
3. Contact your neighborhood school

### Step 2: Check Enrollment Period

Most districts open enrollment in **spring** for the following school year:
- February - April is typical
- Some districts have later deadlines

### Step 3: Gather Documents

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **Birth certificate** | Verify age/eligibility |
| **Proof of residence** | Verify you live in district |
| **Immunization records** | Required for enrollment |
| **Parent/guardian ID** | For registration |

### Step 4: Complete Registration

- Online registration (many districts)
- In-person at school office
- District enrollment center

### Step 5: Attend Orientation

Most schools offer:
- TK orientation sessions
- Meet the teacher events
- School tours

## Before and After School Care

### The Gap Problem

If TK runs 8:30am - 2:30pm (6 hours) and you work 8am - 5pm (9 hours):
- You need 3+ additional hours of care

### Solutions

| Option | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **School-based extended care** | Many schools offer before/after programs |
| **ASES programs** | Free after-school at Title I schools |
| **CSPP wraparound** | Some CSPP sites provide wraparound for TK |
| **Private after-school** | YMCAs, Boys & Girls Clubs, etc. |
| **Family/friend care** | Relative picks up from school |

### CSPP + TK Combination

Some families:
1. Enroll child in TK (free, morning)
2. Use CSPP for before/after school (if income-eligible)

**Check if your school/district offers combined programs.**

## TK for Children with Disabilities

### IEP Services

Children with IEPs can:
- Attend TK with support services
- Receive accommodations
- Access special education resources

### Early Intervention to TK

If your child receives:
- Regional Center services (0-3)
- Early Start
- Special education preschool

**Plan the transition:**
1. Contact school district special education office
2. Schedule IEP meeting before TK starts
3. Ensure services transfer

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Is TK the same as preschool?

**A:** TK is school-based and uses modified kindergarten curriculum. It's part of the public school system, while preschool can be public or private with varied approaches.

### Q: Will my child have to do TK AND kindergarten?

**A:** Yes. TK is the first year of a two-year kindergarten program. Your child will:
- Year 1: TK (age 4 turning 5)
- Year 2: Kindergarten (age 5 turning 6)

### Q: What if my child is already in preschool I love?

**A:** You can keep them there! TK is optional. If you're happy with current care, no need to switch.

### Q: Is TK full day?

**A:** It varies by district:
- Some offer half-day (3-4 hours)
- Some offer full day (6 hours)
- Rarely matches full workday (8+ hours)

Check your specific district.

### Q: Can my 3-year-old attend TK?

**A:** No. TK has specific age eligibility based on birth date. Three-year-olds should look at:
- CSPP (if income-eligible)
- Head Start (if income-eligible)
- Private preschool

### Q: What if there's no room in TK?

**A:** As TK expands, districts are required to provide access. If waitlisted:
- Ask about other TK sites in district
- Request information on when spots will open
- Consider alternatives while waiting

### Q: Is TK available in summer?

**A:** Typically no. TK follows the school calendar (September-June). For summer care:
- Summer programs through school
- Summer day camps
- Continued preschool/childcare
