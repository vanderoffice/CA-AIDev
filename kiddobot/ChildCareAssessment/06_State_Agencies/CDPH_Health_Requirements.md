# California Department of Public Health (CDPH) - Childcare Health Requirements

**Purpose:** Health and immunization requirements administered by CDPH for childcare enrollment.

---

## Overview

The **California Department of Public Health (CDPH)** sets and enforces health requirements for children entering childcare and school. While CDPH doesn't administer childcare subsidies, their requirements affect every family enrolling in licensed care.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Website** | [cdph.ca.gov](https://www.cdph.ca.gov) |
| **Immunization Branch** | [cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CID/DCDC/Pages/Immunization-Branch](https://www.cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CID/DCDC/Pages/Immunization-Branch.aspx) |
| **Role** | Health standards, immunizations, disease prevention |
| **Key Law** | California Health & Safety Code §120325-120380 |

## Required Immunizations by Age

### Before Age 18 Months (Childcare Entry)

| Vaccine | Doses Required |
|---------|----------------|
| **DTaP** (Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis) | 4 doses |
| **Polio** | 3 doses |
| **MMR** (Measles, Mumps, Rubella) | 1 dose |
| **Hib** (Haemophilus influenzae type b) | Varies by age at first dose |
| **Hepatitis B** | 3 doses |
| **Varicella** (Chickenpox) | 1 dose |

### Ages 18 Months - 4 Years (Preschool)

Same as above, with age-appropriate boosters.

### Kindergarten Entry (Age 5)

| Vaccine | Doses Required |
|---------|----------------|
| **DTaP** | 5 doses (4 if 4th dose after age 4) |
| **Polio** | 4 doses (3 if 3rd dose after age 4) |
| **MMR** | 2 doses |
| **Hepatitis B** | 3 doses |
| **Varicella** | 2 doses |

## California Immunization Registry (CAIR)

### What It Is

CAIR is California's statewide immunization tracking system.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Consolidate immunization records |
| **Access** | Healthcare providers, schools, childcare |
| **Family Access** | Through healthcare provider or local health department |

### Benefits for Families

- No need to track paper records
- Providers can verify immunizations directly
- Easier transfers between schools/childcare
- Reduces duplicate vaccinations

### How to Access Your Child's Records

1. **Ask your pediatrician** to print CAIR records
2. **Contact local health department** for immunization records
3. **School or childcare** may be able to access directly

## Exemptions from Immunization Requirements

### Medical Exemptions

As of **January 1, 2021** (SB 276):

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Who can grant** | Licensed physician (MD or DO) |
| **Form required** | Submitted through CAIR Medical Exemption Request |
| **Review process** | CDPH may review for medical basis |
| **Duration** | May be permanent or temporary |

**Valid reasons for medical exemption:**
- Documented adverse reaction to previous vaccine
- Compromised immune system
- Specific contraindications per ACIP guidelines

### Personal Belief Exemptions

**No longer available** as of January 1, 2016 (SB 277).

- Personal or religious belief exemptions are NOT permitted for childcare or school entry
- Only medical exemptions are accepted

## Conditional Enrollment

### What It Is

Children can enroll while completing their immunization series if they are on schedule.

| Situation | Allowed? |
|-----------|----------|
| Child has started series but not completed | ✅ Conditional enrollment |
| Child needs boosters not yet due | ✅ Conditional enrollment |
| Child has no immunizations | ❌ Must begin series |
| Child is behind schedule | ❌ Must catch up before enrollment |

### Requirements for Conditional Enrollment

1. Child must have received at least one dose of each required vaccine
2. Parent must show written schedule from healthcare provider
3. Child must complete series by specified dates
4. Provider may require periodic verification

## Health Examination Requirements

### Required Before Childcare Entry

| Requirement | Timing | Form |
|-------------|--------|------|
| **Health examination** | Within 30 days before or 30 days after enrollment | LIC 701 (Physician's Report) |
| **TB screening** | If required by county | Health provider documentation |
| **Dental assessment** | Recommended, not required for childcare | — |

### What the Health Exam Includes

- General health status
- Immunization verification
- Vision screening
- Hearing screening
- Developmental assessment
- Any health conditions affecting care

## Special Populations

### Homeless Children

Under McKinney-Vento Act:
- **Cannot be denied enrollment** for lack of immunization records
- Must be given reasonable time to obtain records
- Liaison assists with obtaining health documentation

### Foster Children

- Social worker assists with obtaining health records
- Foster children often have records in state system
- Cannot be denied enrollment while records are located

### Migrant/Immigrant Children

- Accepted vaccines from country of origin count
- May need documentation translated
- CAIR can consolidate international + US records

## Outbreak Response

### When Outbreaks Occur

CDPH may require:

| Action | When |
|--------|------|
| **Exclusion of unvaccinated children** | During active outbreak |
| **Additional vaccinations** | For specific populations |
| **Reporting requirements** | Providers must report cases |

### Recent Examples

- Measles outbreaks: Unvaccinated children excluded from care
- Whooping cough: Booster requirements enhanced
- COVID-19: Specific protocols during pandemic (now reduced)

## Where to Get Immunizations

### Free or Low-Cost Options

| Resource | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Medi-Cal** | All required immunizations covered |
| **Local health departments** | Often offer free clinics |
| **Federally Qualified Health Centers** | Sliding scale fees |
| **Vaccines for Children (VFC)** | Free vaccines for eligible children |

### VFC Eligibility

Children qualify for free vaccines through VFC if they are:
- Enrolled in Medi-Cal
- Uninsured
- American Indian or Alaska Native
- Underinsured (insurance doesn't cover vaccines)

## Checklist for Childcare Enrollment

### Immunizations
- [ ] Child is up-to-date on required vaccines
- [ ] CAIR records accessible or printed copy obtained
- [ ] If conditional enrollment, catch-up schedule documented

### Health Documentation
- [ ] Physician's Report (LIC 701) completed within 30 days
- [ ] TB screening if required by county
- [ ] Any special health needs documented

### If Seeking Exemption
- [ ] Consult with physician about medical basis
- [ ] Physician submits exemption through CAIR
- [ ] Understand exclusion risks during outbreaks

---

*CDPH health requirements exist to protect all children in childcare settings. Most children complete their immunizations on schedule with routine pediatric care. If you're behind or need assistance, free vaccines are available through Medi-Cal and VFC programs.*
