# California Department of Education (CDE)

**Purpose:** Overview of CDE's role in early childhood education and childcare programs.

---

## Overview

The **California Department of Education (CDE)** oversees education-focused childcare and preschool programs. While CDSS handles subsidies, CDE focuses on early learning and school readiness.

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Website** | [cde.ca.gov](https://www.cde.ca.gov) |
| **Early Education Division** | [cde.ca.gov/sp/cd](https://www.cde.ca.gov/sp/cd/) |
| **Annual Budget** | ~$3.5 billion (early education programs) |
| **Focus** | Educational quality, school readiness |

---

## Programs Administered by CDE

### California State Preschool Program (CSPP)

The largest state-funded preschool program in California.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Ages** | 3-5 years |
| **Income Limit** | Up to 100% SMI |
| **Cost** | Free for eligible families |
| **Hours** | Part-day (3 hrs) or full-day (6.5+ hrs) |
| **Focus** | Kindergarten readiness |

**Who operates CSPP:**
- School districts
- County Offices of Education
- Community-based organizations (under contract)

### Transitional Kindergarten (TK)

Free public school program for 4-year-olds.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Ages** | 4-year-olds (turning 5 during school year) |
| **Income Limit** | None (universal) |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Hours** | School day (varies by district) |
| **Location** | Public elementary schools |

**Expansion Timeline:**
- 2024-25: Children turning 5 by June 2
- 2025-26: **All 4-year-olds** (universal)

### Head Start State Collaboration

CDE coordinates with federally-funded Head Start programs:
- **Head Start:** Ages 3-5
- **Early Head Start:** Ages 0-3
- **Migrant Head Start:** Agricultural worker families

### Child Development Permits

CDE issues credentials for early childhood educators:

| Permit Level | Requirements | Supervises |
|--------------|--------------|------------|
| Assistant | 6 units ECE | N/A |
| Associate | 12 units ECE | N/A |
| Teacher | 24 units ECE + 16 general | Aides |
| Master Teacher | 24 units ECE + 16 general + 6 specialization | Teachers |
| Site Supervisor | AA degree + experience | Site staff |
| Program Director | BA degree + experience | Programs |

## CDE vs. CDSS: Key Differences

| Aspect | CDE | CDSS |
|--------|-----|------|
| **Primary Mission** | Education | Social services |
| **Main Programs** | CSPP, TK, Head Start | CalWORKs, CCDF, APP |
| **Funding Model** | Contract-based | Voucher-based |
| **Provider Type** | Schools, licensed centers | Any licensed provider + FFN |
| **Income Threshold** | 100% SMI (CSPP) | 85% SMI (CCDF) |
| **Quality Focus** | Curriculum, teacher credentials | Access, affordability |

## Quality Standards

### Desired Results Developmental Profile (DRDP)

CDE requires CSPP and Head Start programs to assess children using DRDP:
- Social-emotional development
- Language and literacy
- Math cognition
- Physical development

### Program Quality Rating

CDE is implementing a statewide Quality Rating and Improvement System (QRIS):
- Rates programs on 1-5 scale
- Considers teacher qualifications, environment, curriculum
- Higher-rated programs may receive enhanced funding

### Teacher-to-Child Ratios

| Age Group | CSPP Ratio | TK Ratio |
|-----------|------------|----------|
| 3-year-olds | 1:8 | N/A |
| 4-year-olds | 1:8 | 1:12 (moving to 1:10) |
| Mixed age | 1:8 | N/A |

## Recent Changes and Initiatives

### Universal TK Expansion

| Year | Eligibility |
|------|-------------|
| 2022-23 | Birthday Sept 2 - Feb 2 |
| 2023-24 | Birthday Sept 2 - April 2 |
| 2024-25 | Birthday Sept 2 - June 2 |
| **2025-26** | **All 4-year-olds (turning 4 by Sept 1)** |

### CSPP Expansion

- Increased income eligibility to 100% SMI
- Added full-day options
- Aligned with TK for seamless transitions

### Educator Workforce Development

- New TK credential requirements (24 ECE units)
- Expanded professional development
- Loan forgiveness programs for ECE teachers

## CSPP vs. CCDF Subsidies: Which to Choose?

| Factor | CSPP (CDE) | CCDF/APP (CDSS) |
|--------|------------|-----------------|
| **Best for** | Preschool education focus | Flexible provider choice |
| **Income limit** | 100% SMI | 85% SMI |
| **Hours** | 3-6.5 hours/day | Based on work schedule |
| **Provider** | Contracted program | Any licensed + FFN |
| **Curriculum** | State-mandated | Provider's choice |
| **Year-round** | Many programs yes | Based on work need |

**You may be able to combine:**
- TK (morning) + APP-subsidized after-school care
- CSPP (part-day) + APP wraparound hours

---

*CDE programs focus on education and school readiness. If your priority is kindergarten preparation in a structured learning environment, CDE programs (CSPP, TK, Head Start) may be the best fit.*
