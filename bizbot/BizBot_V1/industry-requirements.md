## 1. Medical & Healthcare Businesses

### State Requirements

**California Department of Public Health - Licensing & Certification**
- Portal: https://www.cdph.ca.gov/
- Email: License.and.Certification@cdph.ca.gov
- Phone: (916) 558-1784

**Primary Care Clinic Licensing** (Health & Safety Code Section 1200):
- For independent clinics providing outpatient primary care
- Processing time: 30-90 days
- Must complete facility inspections
- Certificate of Occupancy for medical use required
- Regular compliance inspections required

**Medical Board of California**
- Portal: https://www.mbc.ca.gov/
- Phone: (916) 263-2382

**Physician Licensing Requirements**:
- All physicians must hold current Medical Board license
- Majority physician ownership required for medical corporations
- Special Faculty Permits available (part-time arrangements)

### Local Requirements

**City/County Requirements**:
- Health permits (in addition to CDPH)
- Zoning clearance (medical use verification)
- Building permits and inspections
- Certificate of Occupancy (medical)
- Parking and accessibility compliance

### Additional Documentation Required

- [ ] Current Medical Board license (all physicians)
- [ ] CDPH License & Certification application
- [ ] Health facility documentation
- [ ] Proof of professional liability insurance ($1M+ typical)
- [ ] Background checks/fingerprinting (all staff)
- [ ] Ownership documentation (must verify physician majority ownership)
- [ ] Facility layout/floor plans
- [ ] Equipment and safety compliance documentation

### Integration with Standard Process

1. Complete Phase 1 (Entity Formation) - Medical corporation structure
2. Complete Phase 2 (State Licensing) - Includes CDPH application
3. Begin CDPH facility licensing early (longest phase, 30-90 days)
4. Complete Phase 3 (Local Licensing) - After CDPH approval
5. Phase 4 (Renewal/Compliance) - Annual CDPH compliance, staff licensing

**Timeline Addition**: +4-12 weeks total (CDPH process often 60-90 days)

**Processing Times**:
- Medical Board physician license: 2-8 weeks
- CDPH facility licensing: 30-90 days
- Local health permits: 2-4 weeks

## 2. Food Service & Restaurants

### State Requirements

**California Department of Public Health**
- Required: Retail Food Code compliance (Title 21 of CCR)
- Nutrition labeling (if applicable)
- Contact local county health department (oversees most requirements)

### County/City Requirements

**County Health Department**:
- Health permit required (for every location)
- Food facility inspection required (before opening)
- Ongoing annual inspections
- Food handler certifications for all staff
- Health permit valid 3 years in most counties

**Local City Requirements**:
- General business license (as part of Phase 3)
- Zoning clearance (commercial/food service zone)
- Fire suppression system documentation
- Wheelchair accessibility compliance

### Additional Documentation Required

- [ ] Health permit application
- [ ] Food facility floor plan/layout
- [ ] Menu (for health department review)
- [ ] Equipment list (ovens, refrigeration, etc.)
- [ ] Kitchen setup documentation
- [ ] Fire suppression system certificate
- [ ] Food handler certifications (all staff)
- [ ] Proof of food storage temperature compliance

### Important Requirements

**Food Handler Certification**:
- All food handlers must complete training (2-4 hours)
- Valid 3 years
- Multiple providers: ServSafe, ProctorU, county health depts
- Cost: $25-$50 per person

**Health Inspection Timeline**:
- Initial inspection: Before you can open (2-4 weeks after application)
- Annual inspections: After opening (1-2 per year)
- Violations must be corrected (timeline varies)
- Closure possible if critical violations found

**Grease Trap Compliance** (restaurants with cooking):
- Grease trap installation and maintenance required
- Monthly or quarterly cleaning/disposal
- Compliance documentation at inspection

### Integration with Standard Process

1. Complete Phase 1: Entity Formation
2. Complete Phase 2: State Licensing (if selling prepared food)
3. Phase 3: Local Licensing includes:
   - Zoning clearance (food service zone verification)
   - Health permit application (first major step)
   - Health inspection scheduling (2-4 weeks wait)
   - Fire inspection (if cooking/grease trap)
   - General business license

**Timeline Addition**: +2-4 weeks (health inspection scheduling)

## 3. Alcohol Sales

### State Requirements

**California Department of Alcoholic Beverage Control (ABC)**
- Portal: https://www.abc.ca.gov/
- Phone: (916) 419-2500

**License Types**:
- On-Sale Licenses: Bars, restaurants (drink on premises)
- Off-Sale Licenses: Liquor stores, convenience stores (bottle sales)
- Specific licenses for beer/wine vs. spirits
- Processing time: 60-90 days
- Fee range: $100-$15,000+ depending on license type

**ABC Application Requirements**:
- Previous business experience (some applicants)
- Local approval from city/county (prerequisite)
- Notice posted at premises for 30 days
- Public hearing may be required (some jurisdictions)
- Background check clearance required
- Responsible Beverage Service (RBS) training

### Local Requirements

**City/County Approval FIRST**:
- Local approval required BEFORE ABC application
- Many cities have limited number of licenses available
- Some areas are "saturated" (no new licenses allowed)
- Local approval letter required with ABC application
- Contact city before spending time on ABC process

**Other Local Requirements**:
- Zoning clearance (alcohol sales zone)
- Distance from schools, parks, residential areas (varies by city)
- Security plan (camera, lighting, staff training)
- Police approval (some jurisdictions)

### Additional Documentation Required

- [ ] ABC application (Form 226)
- [ ] Local city/county approval letter
- [ ] ABC license fee payment
- [ ] Background check clearance
- [ ] Notice of application (public notice posted 30 days)
- [ ] Responsible Beverage Service (RBS) training completion
- [ ] Premises diagram/floor plan
- [ ] Security plan documentation
- [ ] Federal liquor permit (if applicable)

### Responsible Beverage Service Training

- Mandatory for all alcohol business applicants and staff
- Training covers: Age verification, intoxication recognition, liability
- Multiple certified providers
- Cost: $15-$50 per person
- Valid 3 years
- Must be completed BEFORE ABC approval

### Integration with Standard Process

1. Complete Phase 1: Entity Formation
2. Phase 2: Apply to city/county for local approval (not state licensing)
3. Phase 3: Local Licensing includes:
   - Zoning clearance (alcohol sales zone)
   - Local city/county approval (prerequisite for ABC)
   - General business license
4. After Phase 3: Submit ABC application (starts 60-90 day process)
5. ABC approval (separate from business license)

**Timeline Addition**: +8-12 weeks total (ABC processing 60-90 days)

**Critical**: Verify local city/county allows alcohol sales BEFORE investing in licensing process (many areas are "saturated")

## 4. Construction & Contractors

### State Requirements

**Contractors State License Board (CSLB)**
- Portal: https://www.cslb.ca.gov/
- Phone: (800) 321-2752

**CSLB License Required**:
- For all jobs exceeding $500 in labor/materials
- Sole proprietors and companies both need license
- License type: General Contractor, Specialty, or Sole Proprietor
- Processing time: 2-6 weeks AFTER exam passage

**Exam & Requirements**:
- Must pass CSLB trade exam (written test)
- Experience requirements: 4 years (2 years if apprentice)
- Application fee: $200-$300
- License fee: $550-$850 renewal (biennial)

**Restricted Activities Without License**:
- Cannot sign contracts over $500
- Penalties: $5,000-$15,000 fines + criminal charges possible

### Insurance & Bonding

**Workers' Compensation Insurance**: MANDATORY if hiring employees
- Workers' comp statement required for CSLB
- Cost: 2-40% of payroll (depends on trade)
- Failure to carry: Criminal penalties + civil liability

**Performance/Payment Bonds**:
- Often required for public or large projects
- Cost: 1-5% of project value
- Protects project owner if contractor fails

**General Liability Insurance**:
- Recommended: $1M minimum coverage
- Cost: $300-$1,500+ annually

### Local Requirements

**City/County Requirements**:
- Zoning clearance (if office location)
- General business license
- Some areas require additional contractor permits
- Home Improvement Salesperson registration (if applicable)

### Additional Documentation Required

- [ ] CSLB Contractor License (after exam passage)
- [ ] Workers' compensation insurance statement
- [ ] General liability insurance proof
- [ ] Performance/payment bonds (if required)
- [ ] Proof of experience/apprenticeship
- [ ] Criminal background clearance (if applicable)

### Integration with Standard Process

1. Complete Phase 1: Entity Formation
2. Register with CSLB (can start before Phase 1 complete)
3. Pass CSLB exam
4. Receive CSLB license (2-6 weeks after exam)
5. Phase 2: State Licensing (standard process)
6. Phase 3: Local business license
7. Phase 4: Renewal (CSLB renewal every 2 years)

**Timeline Addition**: 4-12 weeks (includes exam prep time)

## 5. Cannabis Businesses

### CRITICAL: Verify Local Allowance FIRST

**Many cities/counties BAN cannabis businesses**. Before any other steps:
1. Contact city/county planning department
2. Ask: "Do you allow cannabis retail/cultivation/manufacturing?"
3. If NO: Cannot operate there legally
4. If YES: Proceed to state requirements

### State Requirements

**California Department of Cannabis Control (DCC)**
- Portal: https://cannabis.ca.gov/
- Phone: (833) 622-6362

**License Types**:
- Retail (storefronts, delivery)
- Cultivation (small-scale, medium-scale, large-scale)
- Manufacturing (products, extracts)
- Distribution
- Microbusiness (cultivation + retail combo)
- Testing laboratories

**State Application Requirements**:
- Processing time: 90-180 days (extensive review)
- State application fee: $1,000-$5,000 (varies by license type)
- Local approval MUST come BEFORE state application
- Extensive background checks (all owners, managers, employees)
- Security plan required
- Track & trace system (METRC) compliance

### Local Requirements

**City/County Authorization REQUIRED FIRST**:
- Most cities require local approval before state application
- Some areas have competitive application processes (lottery)
- Application fees: $500-$5,000+ depending on city
- Local approval letter required with state application

**Local Zoning Requirements**:
- Distance from schools (typically 600-1,000 feet)
- Distance from parks, youth centers, libraries
- Retail must be in commercial zones
- Cultivation must meet land use requirements
- Some neighborhoods explicitly prohibited

**Security Requirements**:
- Video surveillance system (24/7 recording)
- Alarm systems
- Lighting requirements
- Access controls and logs
- Guard presence (some areas)

### Additional Documentation Required

- [ ] Local city/county approval letter
- [ ] State cannabis application
- [ ] Security plan and diagram
- [ ] Extensive background checks (all principals, staff)
- [ ] Track & Trace (METRC) system enrollment
- [ ] Proof of financial resources
- [ ] Insurance (general liability + product liability)
- [ ] Standard operating procedures manual
- [ ] Lab testing documentation (if manufacturing)
- [ ] Cultivation plan (if growing)

### Key Challenges

**Background Checks**:
- Strict scrutiny of all owners and managers
- Financial history reviewed
- Arrests/convictions can disqualify
- Takes 60-90 days

**Competitive Licensing**:
- Many jurisdictions limit number of licenses
- May require community support letters
- Local review/approval process lengthy
- Rejection possible even with all documentation

**Track & Trace System (METRC)**:
- Mandatory system for all cannabis licensees
- Real-time inventory tracking
- Cannabis products tagged and tracked from seed to sale
- Requires compliance officer appointment

### Integration with Standard Process

1. Verify local allowance with city/county (FIRST STEP)
2. If allowed: Begin Phase 1 (Entity Formation)
3. Simultaneously: Apply for local cannabis permit/approval
4. Wait for local approval (30-90 days)
5. Phase 2: Submit state cannabis application (complex process, professional help recommended)
6. Wait for state approval (90-180 days)
7. Phase 3: General business license
8. Phase 4: Ongoing compliance

**Timeline Addition**: 6-12 months total (local approval + state processing)

**Professional Help Recommended**: Cannabis licensing complex; consider hiring cannabis compliance consultant

## 6. Child Care Facilities

### State Requirements

**California Department of Social Services - Community Care Licensing (CCL)**
- Portal: https://www.ccld.dss.ca.gov/
- Phone: (844) 529-5453

**Types of Facilities**:
- Family Child Care Home (residence, up to 14 children)
- Small Family Child Care Home (residence, up to 8 children)
- Child Care Center (non-residential, any size)

**CCL Licensing Timeline**: 60-120 days
**Facility Inspections**: Required before license issuance

### Licensing Requirements

**General Requirements**:
- Background clearances for all staff and household members (18+)
- Live Scan fingerprinting (all adults)
- First aid and CPR certifications (all caregivers)
- Health examination
- TB test
- Child abuse and neglect index clearance

**Facility Requirements**:
- Fire and safety inspection
- Health inspection (disease control measures)
- Building code compliance inspection
- Specific square footage per child (varies by facility type)
- Outdoor play space requirements
- Equipment and toy safety compliance

**Documentation Required for License**:
- [ ] CCL application (Form LIC 807)
- [ ] Background clearance documentation
- [ ] Live Scan fingerprinting results
- [ ] First aid/CPR certifications (current)
- [ ] Health examination certificate
- [ ] TB test documentation
- [ ] Disaster preparedness plan
- [ ] Child abuse prevention training (staff)
- [ ] Parent contracts and policies
- [ ] Immunization records
- [ ] Staff training documentation

### Additional Considerations

**Family Childcare Exemptions** (in some jurisdictions):
- Family daycares with â‰¤14 children may be exempt from city zoning clearance (e.g., Oakland)
- Still need CCL license from state
- Verify with your city

**Staffing Requirements**:
- Staff-to-child ratios: 1:3 (infants) to 1:6 (preschool)
- Lead teachers must have childcare credentials
- All staff must complete training hours (annually)

**Parent Notifications**:
- Background check authorization from parents
- Liability waiver from parents
- Emergency contact information
- Health screening authorization

### Integration with Standard Process

1. Verify local zoning allows childcare (may be exempt)
2. Phase 1: Entity Formation
3. Simultaneously: Begin CCL application
4. Complete background checks/fingerprinting (5-10 business days)
5. Facility inspections (fire, health, building)
6. CCL license issuance (60-120 days total)
7. Phase 3: Local business license
8. Phase 4: Annual CCL license renewal + background rechecks

**Timeline Addition**: 60-120 days (CCL process)

## 7. Personal Care Services

### Barbershops, Salons, Nail Salons

**California Board of Barbering and Cosmetology**
- Portal: https://www.barbercosmo.ca.gov/
- Phone: (916) 575-7170

**Licensing Requirements**:
- Individual practitioner licenses required (Barber, Cosmetologist, Esthetician, Manicurist)
- Establishment permit required for business location
- Sanitation inspections required

**Individual Licenses**:
- Barber: 1,500 hours training + exam
- Cosmetologist: 1,600 hours training + exam
- Esthetician: 600 hours training + exam
- Manicurist: 400 hours training + exam
- Nail technician: 350 hours training + exam

**Establishment Permits**:
- Required for every salon/barbershop location
- Renewal: Every 2 years
- Fee: $35-$100
- Sanitation compliance documentation

**Requirements**:
- [ ] Individual practitioner license (each employee)
- [ ] Establishment permit
- [ ] Health permit (county/city)
- [ ] Sanitation compliance documentation
- [ ] Equipment and facility standards met
- [ ] Proof of workers compensation (if hiring)

## 9. Home-Based Businesses

### General Requirements

**Zoning Verification**:
- Residential zoning must allow home-based business
- Contact city Planning Department first
- Get written zoning clearance

**Home Occupation Permit**:
- Required in most cities ($50-$200 annually)
- Separate from general business license
- Submit form to city Planning Department

**General Business License**:
- Still required even if home-based
- May have lower fee for home-based
- Filed with city

**Physical Address**:
- Must use physical residence address (NO PO Box or PMB)
- Address appears on business license

### Common Restrictions

**Standard Restrictions** (check with your city):
- Business secondary to residential use
- No structural modifications
- No visible signage or display
- Limited to one room, garage, or accessory structure
- No employees (or maximum 1 non-resident employee)
- Limited customer visits (no foot traffic retail)
- No commercial vehicle parking

### City-Specific Rules

**Los Angeles**:
- Max 1 non-resident employee
- Max 2 deliveries/pickups per day
- No commercial vehicles on-site
- No visible commercial activity from outside

**Mountain View**:
- Business confined to one room/garage/structure
- No window displays or identification signs
- Must complete "Home Occupations Regulations Acknowledgement Form"

**San Francisco**:
- Home Office/Workspace Permit required
- Must be lawfully used as residence
- Restrictions on customer meetings

### HOA Considerations

**Check CC&Rs**:
- Homeowner associations may restrict or prohibit home businesses
- HOA restrictions supersede city allowance
- HOA violation can result in fines or forced closure

**Approval Process**:
- May need HOA approval before city approval
- Submit home business plan to HOA
- HOA can require insurance or additional documentation

### Seller's Permit for Home-Based Retail

**Trigger**: 3 or more sales of tangible goods in 12 months
- Home-based craft sellers must register if meeting threshold
- Includes Etsy sellers, handmade goods, resellers
- CDTFA Seller's Permit required
- Sales tax must be collected and filed

## 10. Nonprofits 501(c)(3)

### Important Clarification

**Misconception**: 501(c)(3) status eliminates business licensing requirements

**Reality**: License required; some FEES may be waived

### What IS Exempt (with proof)

- Business license **FEES** (not license itself)
- Some income and property taxes
- Sales tax on qualifying transactions ONLY
- Exemptions vary by jurisdiction

### What IS REQUIRED

- Business license must still be obtained
- Annual Statement of Information filing
- Compliance with zoning regulations
- Health/safety requirements if applicable

### Documentation Required

**With Business License Application**:
- [ ] IRS determination letter (proof of 501(c)(3) status)
- [ ] Articles of Incorporation with nonprofit designation
- [ ] Statement of charitable purpose
- [ ] Board member list
- [ ] Business license application (still required)

### Fee Exemptions

**Verification**:
- Each city/county decides independently
- Some honor 501(c)(3) exemptions, others do NOT
- Must verify with your specific city
- Submit IRS documentation with application

**Example - Santa Ana**:
- Business license REQUIRED
- Submit copy of 501(c)(3) form for fee exemption consideration
- License itself is mandatory, fees may or may not be waived

### Tax Compliance

**Annual Requirements**:
- Statement of Information with Secretary of State (annually)
- Form 990 filing with IRS (annually)
- Compliance with state charitable solicitation laws
- Quarterly payroll taxes (if employees)
