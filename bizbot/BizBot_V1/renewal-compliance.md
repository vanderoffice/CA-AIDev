# Phase 4: Renewal & Ongoing Compliance

Manage your business license continuously after acquisition. Annual renewals and ongoing requirements keep your license valid.

## Annual Renewals

### Standard Timeline (Most Cities)

**Expiration Date**: December 31st (most cities - exceptions exist)
**Renewal Notices**: Mailed in December
**Payment Deadline**: February 1st or March 1st (varies by city)
**Late Penalty Structure** (typical):
- 10% penalty on amount owed (first month late)
- 1% monthly interest on unpaid balance
- Additional 40% penalty if 2+ months late
- Total possible penalties: 50%+ of license cost

**EXCEPTION - Berkeley**: Renewal deadline is March 1st (expiration Sept 30th)

### Renewal Methods

**Online** (fastest):
- Available in most major cities
- Processing: 1-2 weeks
- Immediate confirmation
- Most convenient option

**Mail**:
- Send payment + renewal form
- Processing: 2-4 weeks
- Allow extra time for postal delays

**In-Person**:
- Some cities accept walk-in renewals
- Hours vary by jurisdiction
- Fastest if available (same-day possible)

### Required Information for Renewal

- Business Account Number (BAN) or License Number
- Actual gross receipts/revenue for prior year (or estimate if first renewal)
- Current number of employees (full-time and part-time)
- Updated business activity information
- Any changes to business (address, ownership, activities)
- Payment for renewal fees
- SB 205 compliance form (if applicable)

**Important**: Some cities adjust fees based on actual revenue vs. estimated

## Statement of Information Updates

### LLCs: File Every 2 Years

**Timeline**: 6-month filing window based on formation month
**Portal**: https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov/
**Form**: LLC-12
**Penalty**: $250 if missed + potential FTB suspension
**Cost**: $0-$50

### Corporations: File Annually

**Timeline**: 6-month window based on formation month
**Portal**: https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov/
**Form**: SI-550
**Penalty**: $250 if missed + potential FTB suspension
**Cost**: $0-$50

### File Updated Statement When

In addition to regular schedule, update immediately if:
- Change in officers/directors/managers/members
- Change in business address
- Change in registered agent
- Change in principal executive office address

### Nonprofit Entities: File Annually

- Required annually with Secretary of State
- Same portal as corporate forms
- Form depends on entity type

## Ongoing Business Changes Requiring License Updates

### Notify city/county of changes within 30 days

**Changes Requiring Notification**:
- [ ] Change of ownership (new owner needs new license or transfer)
- [ ] Change of business location (requires new zoning clearance)
- [ ] Change of business name
- [ ] Change of business activity/type
- [ ] Addition of business locations
- [ ] Significant expansion or restructuring

**Note**: Business license is typically NOT transferable to new owners; new license required

**Process**:
1. Contact city/county business license department
2. File amendment or change of ownership form
3. Pay any applicable fees
4. Receive updated license documentation

## Tax Compliance

### CDTFA (Sales Tax) - If You Have Seller's Permit

**Filing Frequency**:
- Quarterly: If average monthly sales tax < $1,200
- Monthly: If average monthly sales tax â‰¥ $1,200
- Annual: If average monthly sales tax < $100

**Requirements**:
- File Form 401-A (sales tax return)
- Report gross sales and tax collected
- Remit tax owed by deadline
- Maintain records of all sales

**Deadlines**:
- Quarterly: Due month after quarter ends (Apr 15, Jul 15, Oct 15, Jan 15)
- Monthly: Due 25th day of following month
- Electronic filing available: https://onlineservices.cdtfa.ca.gov/

**Penalties for Late Payment**:
- Underpayment interest: 1% per month
- Failure to file: $10-$100 per month + interest
- Audit adjustments can be substantial

### Franchise Tax Board - LLC Annual Requirement

**LLCs MUST pay**:
- Minimum $800 annual franchise tax (even if $0 revenue)
- Due: 15th day of 4th month of tax year (typically April 15th)
- Form: Form 568 (LLC Return of Income)

**File Online**: https://www.ftb.ca.gov/
**First Year**: Exemption available if $0 revenue and didn't conduct business

**Failure to Pay**: Results in business suspension, penalties, and interest

### Franchise Tax Board - Corporation Annual Requirement

**Corporations MUST pay**:
- Minimum $800 annual franchise tax
- Due: 15th day of 4th month of tax year
- Form: Form 100 (Corporation Franchise or Income Tax Return)

**File Online**: https://www.ftb.ca.gov/

## Record Keeping Requirements

### Maintain for at least 4 years

**Essential Records**:
- [ ] Business license certificates (current and past)
- [ ] License renewal receipts and documentation
- [ ] Tax returns and payment records
- [ ] Sales records and tax collected (if applicable)
- [ ] Employee payroll records
- [ ] Permits and inspection reports
- [ ] Agency correspondence
- [ ] Financial records

**Why**: Audits, inspections, and dispute resolution require documented proof

### License Display Requirements

- Business license must be **prominently displayed** at business location
- Visible to public and regulatory inspectors
- Physical copy or framed certificate (check local requirements)
- Failure to display: $25-$100 fine per day in many cities

## Business License Compliance Checks

### Cities May Conduct Field Audits

**What They Check**:
- Valid business license on display
- Business activity matches license description
- No zoning violations
- No unpaid fees/taxes

**Operating Without License**:
- Fines: $25-$500+ per day
- Back taxes owed
- Potential business closure
- Penalties can exceed business value

**If Visited by Inspector**:
- Politely provide license
- Note inspector name and agency
- Ask for inspection report
- Address any violations promptly

## Industry-Specific Compliance

### Medical/Healthcare Businesses

**Ongoing Requirements**:
- Maintain current Medical Board license (if physician-owned)
- Keep CDPH facility license current
- Annual health inspections required
- Medi-Cal billing compliance (if applicable)
- Professional liability insurance required
- Staff training certifications current

### Food Service/Restaurants

**Ongoing Requirements**:
- Annual health inspections
- Food handler certifications for all staff (current)
- Kitchen sanitation compliance
- Menu updates submitted to health dept (some jurisdictions)
- Grease trap maintenance (if applicable)

### Cannabis Businesses

**Ongoing Requirements**:
- Track-and-trace system (METRC) compliance
- Regular state and local inspections
- Security camera maintenance and recordings
- Compliance reporting
- Track permit expiration (often annual renewal)

### Personal Care Services (Salons, Barbershops)

**Ongoing Requirements**:
- Individual practitioner licenses current for all staff
- Sanitation standards compliance
- Regular health inspections
- Bloodborne pathogen training current

## Penalties & Consequences

### Operating Without Valid License

**Daily Penalties**: $25-$500+ depending on city
**Back Taxes**: All unpaid sales tax, income tax
**Cumulative Costs**: Can exceed thousands of dollars in weeks
**Business Closure**: Immediate closure by city/county
**Criminal Charges**: Possible for willful violations

### Late Renewal Penalties

**Typical Penalty Structure**:
- 10% penalty (first 30 days late)
- Plus 1% monthly interest on amount owed
- Plus 40% penalty (if 60+ days late)
- Example: $200 license, 3 months late = $200 + $20 (penalty) + $6 (interest) + $80 (40% penalty) = **$306 total**

### Tax Filing Penalties

**CDTFA (Sales Tax)**:
- Failure to file: $10-$100 per month + interest
- Underreporting: 15-25% penalty on underreported amount
- Fraud: 75-100% penalty + interest + possible prosecution

**FTB (Franchise Tax)**:
- Late payment: 5% penalty + 1% monthly interest
- Failure to file: $100+ penalty + suspension
- Audit adjustments: Substantial additional taxes + penalties

### SB 205 Violations

- License non-renewal
- Civil citations
- Fines up to $1,000+ per violation
- Potential permit revocation

## Closing a Business Properly

### If You're Ceasing Operations

**Don't Just Disappear**. Follow proper closure process:

1. **Cancel Business License** (30-60 days before closing)
   - File closure application with city/county
   - Pay final fees/taxes
   - Surrender license certificate

2. **File Final Tax Returns**
   - Final sales tax return (CDTFA)
   - Final franchise tax return (FTB)
   - IRS final return (if sole prop/partnership)

3. **File Business Entity Closure**
   - LLC: Certificate of Cancellation
   - Corporation: Certificate of Dissolution
   - Final Statement of Information with SOS

4. **Cancel Permits & Licenses**
   - Seller's permit (if applicable)
   - Professional licenses
   - Industry-specific permits

5. **File FBN Abandonment** (if applicable)
   - With county clerk where filed
   - Prevents name from staying active

**Failure to Properly Close**:
- Continued tax liability
- FBN may stay active (others can't use name)
- Potential FTB liens
- Penalties and interest accumulate
