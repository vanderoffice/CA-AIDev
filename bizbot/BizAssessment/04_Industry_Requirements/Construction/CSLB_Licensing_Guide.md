## Contractors State License Board (CSLB) Overview

**Agency:** California Contractors State License Board
**URL:** https://www.cslb.ca.gov
**Phone:** (800) 321-CSLB (2752)

### What CSLB Regulates

- All contractors performing work valued over $500 (labor + materials)
- 44 different license classifications
- Over 280,000 active licensees
- Approximately 20,000 new applications annually

### License NOT Required

- Work under $500 total (labor + materials)
- Owner-builder (own residence, specific exemptions)
- Handyperson work under $500 per project
- **2025 Update (AB 2622):** Handyperson exemption limit increased for minor work

## License Classifications

### Class A - General Engineering

**Scope:** Fixed works requiring specialized engineering knowledge
- Irrigation systems
- Electrical generation/transmission
- Highways and streets
- Tunnels, bridges
- Dams, waterways
- Sewerage systems
- Pipelines

**Requirements:**
- 4 years journey-level experience in general engineering
- Pass trade and law exams

### Class B - General Building

**Scope:** Building construction requiring at least two unrelated trades
- New construction
- Remodels and alterations
- Commercial buildings
- Residential buildings

**Requirements:**
- 4 years journey-level experience in general building
- Pass trade and law exams

**Key Limitation:**
- Cannot perform specialty work over $500 unless holding that C classification
- Can self-perform up to 3 trades as part of a larger project

## License Requirements

### 1. Experience Requirement

**Minimum:** 4 years journey-level experience within past 10 years

**What Counts:**
- Direct hands-on work in the trade
- Supervisory experience (50% hands-on minimum)
- Foreman or superintendent experience
- Trade school (maximum 3 years credit)
- Military experience (with documentation)

**What Doesn't Count:**
- Helper or laborer time
- Administrative work only
- Time as owner without direct involvement
- Undocumented experience

**Documentation Required:**
- Employer verification on CSLB forms
- Dates of employment
- Hours per week
- Specific duties performed
- Supervisor contact information

### 2. Examinations

**Two Exams Required:**

| Exam | Questions | Time | Passing Score |
|------|-----------|------|---------------|
| Law & Business | 115 | 2.5 hours | 72% |
| Trade Exam | Varies by classification | 3-5 hours | 72% |

**Law & Business Exam Topics:**
- California contractor license law
- Business management
- Contract law
- Estimating and bidding
- Project management
- Safety regulations
- Mechanics lien law

**Trade Exam:**
- Classification-specific technical knowledge
- Code requirements
- Safety practices
- Installation methods
- Troubleshooting

**Exam Scheduling:**
- Schedule online at www.cslb.ca.gov or call (800) 321-CSLB
- Testing centers throughout California
- Computer-based testing
- Results typically same day

**Retakes:**
- No limit on retake attempts
- 18-month period to pass both exams
- $60 fee per retake

### 3. Contractor Bond

**Required:** $25,000 Contractor Bond

**Purpose:**
- Protects consumers against contractor violations
- Covers judgments for willful/fraudulent acts
- Does NOT replace general liability insurance

**Cost:**
- Based on credit score
- Typical range: $100-500/year
- Poor credit: May require cash deposit or higher premium

**Surety Bond Providers:**
- Must be admitted in California
- Many insurance agents can arrange
- CSLB maintains list of bond providers

### 4. Workers' Compensation

**Exemption Available:**
- Sole owner with NO employees can certify exempt
- Must file Exemption Certificate with CSLB
- Becomes "Active-Exempt" license status

**If You Have Employees:**
- Workers' comp insurance MANDATORY
- Must maintain continuously
- Lapses = automatic license suspension
- CSLB receives electronic notification of cancellations

**Cost Factors:**
- Classification (roofing highest, clerical lowest)
- Payroll amount
- Claims history (experience modification rate)
- Typical range: $3,000-50,000+/year

### 5. Qualifying Individual (RME/RMO)

Every license must have a Qualifying Individual who meets experience/exam requirements:

| Title | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| RME (Responsible Managing Employee) | W-2 employee, works for one company |
| RMO (Responsible Managing Officer) | Owner, partner, or corporate officer |
| RMM (Responsible Managing Manager) | LLC member-manager |

**Requirements:**
- Must be involved in construction operations
- Cannot qualify more than one company
- Must be compensated for services
- Must direct and control construction activities

## Application Process

### Step 1: Gather Documentation

**Required Forms:**
- Application for Original Contractor License
- Experience Verification forms (from past employers)
- Criminal history disclosure (if applicable)
- Partnership, corporate, or LLC documents (if not sole proprietor)

### Step 2: Submit Application

**Online:** https://www.cslb.ca.gov (preferred)
**Mail:** CSLB P.O. Box 26000, Sacramento, CA 95826

**Application Fee:** $450

### Step 3: Application Review

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for initial review

**Common Delays:**
- Missing experience documentation
- Insufficient detail on work performed
- Incomplete business entity documents
- Criminal background issues

### Step 4: Schedule & Pass Exams

**Timeline:** Schedule within 18 months of application approval

**Requirements:**
- Pass Law & Business exam
- Pass Trade exam(s) for each classification

### Step 5: Post-Exam Requirements

**After passing exams, submit within 90 days:**
- Original license fee: $200
- Contractor bond ($25,000)
- Workers' compensation or exemption certificate

### Step 6: License Issued

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks after all requirements met

**Receive:**
- Pocket license card
- Wall certificate
- License number (7 digits)

## 2025 Regulatory Updates

### AB 2622 - Handyperson Exemption

**Effective:** January 1, 2025

**Changes:**
- Clarifies handyperson exemption for minor work
- Work under $500 per project still exempt
- Does NOT change $500 threshold
- Provides clearer guidance on what constitutes "minor" work

### SB 1455 - CSLB Sunset Extension

**Status:** CSLB sunset date extended

**Impact:**
- Board continues operations
- Enforcement continues unchanged
- No disruption to licensing

### License Bond Increase (Pending)

**Background:**
- Current $25,000 bond hasn't increased since 2016
- Industry and consumer groups have proposed increases
- Watch for potential legislation in 2025-2026

### Workers' Comp Monitoring

**Enhanced Electronic Verification:**
- Real-time insurance status updates to CSLB
- Faster suspension for lapses
- Stricter enforcement

## Special Requirements by Classification

### C-10 Electrical

**Additional Requirements:**
- Certification Card from Division of Apprenticeship Standards
- Journey-level electrician certification
- 8,000 hours (approximately 4 years) electrical work

### C-36 Plumbing

**Additional Requirements:**
- Journey-level plumber card (from local jurisdiction or union)
- Understanding of California Plumbing Code

### C-20 HVAC

**EPA Section 608 Certification:**
- Required for handling refrigerants
- Not a CSLB requirement, but federal law
- Obtain through EPA-approved testing organization

### C-22 Asbestos Abatement

**Additional Requirements:**
- Cal/OSHA registration
- Specialized training certification
- Notification requirements for each project

### C-39 Roofing

**Higher Insurance Costs:**
- Roofing classification has highest workers' comp rates
- Typical X-mod adjustments significant
- Safety program essential

## Common Violations & Penalties

### Operating Without License

| Offense | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| First offense | Misdemeanor, up to 6 months jail, $5,000 fine |
| Subsequent offense | Up to 1 year jail, $10,000 fine |
| Contract over $500 | Criminal prosecution possible |

### License Violations

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Abandonment | License suspension/revocation |
| Failure to pay workers | Administrative citation |
| Fraudulent use of license | Revocation |
| Substandard work | Citation, correction required |
| Workers' comp violation | $1,000-5,000 per day |

### Administrative Citations

**Civil Penalties:**
- Up to $15,000 per violation
- Immediate payment required
- Must correct violation
- Posted to public license record

## Renewal Requirements

### Timeline

| Deadline | Action |
|----------|--------|
| 60 days before expiration | Renewal notice mailed |
| By expiration date | Submit renewal + fee |
| Within 5 years of expiration | Can reinstate (higher fee) |
| After 5 years | Must re-apply as new applicant |

### Renewal Fee: $450 (every 2 years)

### Continuing Requirements

- Maintain bond continuously
- Maintain workers' comp (if applicable)
- Update address within 90 days of change
- Report criminal convictions
- Report civil judgments related to contracting

### Delinquent Renewal

| Status | Fee |
|--------|-----|
| On-time | $450 |
| Late (within 5 years) | $450 + 50% penalty |
| After 5 years | Must reapply |

## Business Licenses & Local Requirements

### City Business License

**Required in:** Every city/county where you perform work

**Cost:** $50-500+ per jurisdiction

**Process:**
1. Apply with city business license office
2. Provide CSLB license number
3. Pay fee
4. Renew annually

### Building Permits (Per Project)

**Contractor Responsibilities:**
- Pull permits for permitted work
- Cannot work without required permits
- Subject to inspection
- Permit on display at job site

### Home Improvement Salesperson (HIS)

**Required if:**
- Sales representatives solicit home improvement contracts
- Does NOT apply to contractors selling their own work

**Cost:** $75 initial, $75 renewal
**Exam:** Required

## FAQ

### Can I work while my application is pending?

**No.** You must have an active license before performing work over $500.

### Can I use my employee's license?

**No.** The license belongs to the business entity, not the individual. The RME/RMO cannot transfer their qualification.

### What if I'm from another state?

California does NOT have reciprocity with other states. You must:
- Meet California experience requirements
- Pass California exams
- Obtain California bond and insurance

### Can I be RMO/RME for two companies?

**No.** You can only qualify one active license at a time.

### How do I add a classification?

1. Submit Add Classification application
2. Document experience in new classification
3. Pass trade exam for new classification
4. Pay fee ($225)
