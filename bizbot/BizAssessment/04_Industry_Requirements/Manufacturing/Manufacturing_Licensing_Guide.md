# California Manufacturing Business Licensing Guide

Complete licensing and permitting pathway for manufacturing businesses in California (2025).

---

## Quick Reference

| Permit/License | Agency | Timeline | Cost |
|----------------|--------|----------|------|
| Entity Formation | SOS | 1-2 weeks | $70-100 |
| EIN | IRS | Immediate | Free |
| Seller's Permit | CDTFA | Same day | Free |
| Local Business License | City/County | 1-3 weeks | $50-500+ |
| Air Quality Permit | Local AQMD | 7 days - 6+ months | Varies |
| Stormwater Permit (IGP) | SWRCB | 2-4 weeks | Free |
| CUPA Permit (Hazmat) | Local CUPA | 1-2 months | $200-3,000+ |
| Building Permit | City | 1-3 months | Varies |

**CRITICAL:** Air quality permits are typically the longest lead-time item.

## Business Formation & Registration

### Secretary of State

**Agency:** California Secretary of State
**URL:** https://bizfileonline.sos.ca.gov

| Entity Type | Filing Fee | Timeline |
|-------------|------------|----------|
| LLC | $70 | 5-10 days |
| Corporation | $100 | 5-10 days |
| DBA (Fictitious Name) | $10-80 | County recorder |

**Required for All Entities:**
- Registered agent in California
- Annual Statement of Information

### Federal EIN

**Agency:** IRS
**URL:** https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online

- Required regardless of employees
- Apply online - immediate issuance
- Free (no fee)
- Must form entity with SOS first

### CDTFA Seller's Permit

**Agency:** California Department of Tax and Fee Administration
**URL:** https://cdtfa.ca.gov

**Who Needs It:**
- All manufacturers selling tangible goods
- Both wholesale and retail manufacturers
- Even if not selling directly to consumers

**Cost:** Free (security deposit may be required)

**Online Registration:** https://cdtfa.ca.gov/services/registration.htm

### Local Business License

**Agency:** City or County
**Cost:** $50-500+ (varies by jurisdiction)
**Timeline:** 1-3 weeks

- Required in addition to Seller's Permit
- Fees may be based on employees, gross receipts, or flat rate
- Some jurisdictions have manufacturing-specific rates

## Environmental Permits (CRITICAL)

### Air Quality Management Districts

**Overview:** California has 35 local air districts regulating stationary pollution sources.

**Major Districts:**

| District | Region | URL |
|----------|--------|-----|
| South Coast AQMD | LA, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino | https://www.aqmd.gov |
| Bay Area AQMD | SF Bay Area | https://www.baaqmd.gov |
| San Joaquin Valley APCD | Central Valley | https://www.valleyair.org |
| Sacramento Metro AQMD | Sacramento Region | https://www.airquality.org |
| San Diego APCD | San Diego County | https://www.sdapcd.org |

### Permit to Construct vs Permit to Operate

**Permit to Construct (PTC):**
- Required BEFORE purchasing/installing equipment
- Includes BACT (Best Available Control Technology) analysis
- Must obtain before breaking ground

**Permit to Operate (PTO):**
- Issued after equipment installed and tested
- Required for ongoing operation
- Annual renewal with fees

### Equipment Requiring Air Permits

- Spray booths / paint application
- Welding operations
- Boilers and combustion equipment
- Solvent cleaning operations
- Coating and finishing lines
- Plastics/composite manufacturing
- Metal fabrication equipment
- Printing presses

### Source Classification

| Classification | Complexity | Processing Time |
|----------------|------------|-----------------|
| Minor Source | 1-2 simple units | ~7 days |
| Moderate Source | Non-standard equipment | ~30 days |
| Major Source | Complex operations, toxics | 3-6+ months |

### Title V Permits

**Required if ANY of these apply:**
- ≥10 tons/year of any single hazardous air pollutant
- ≥25 tons/year combined hazardous air pollutants
- Major source thresholds for criteria pollutants

**Additional Requirements:**
- Comprehensive permit application
- Public comment period
- Higher permit fees
- Extensive reporting

### Air Permit Fees (South Coast AQMD Example)

| Fee Type | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| Permit Processing | Varies by equipment |
| Annual Renewal | Varies by source |
| Emissions Fee | Per-ton for >4 tons/year |
| Small Business Discount | Available (Rule 301) |

## Water Quality Permits

### Industrial General Permit (IGP)

**Agency:** State Water Resources Control Board
**URL:** https://www.waterboards.ca.gov/water_issues/programs/stormwater/industrial.html

**Who Needs It:**
- Manufacturing facilities with stormwater discharge
- Nine federally defined categories of industrial activities

**Requirements:**
- Register through SMARTS system
- Develop Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)
- Monitor discharge quality
- Submit annual reports

**Cost:** No permit fee (registration is free)

**TMDL Requirements:**
- Check if in impaired watershed
- Additional pollutant restrictions may apply
- Use SWRCB Map Tool to verify

### Wastewater Discharge Permits

**For discharges to sanitary sewer:**

- Contact local sanitary district
- Pretreatment permit required
- Cannot discharge: oils, flammables, corrosives, toxics
- Fees based on volume and pollutant loading

## CUPA (Certified Unified Program Agency)

### What is CUPA?

Local agencies that consolidate six environmental programs:

| Program | What it Covers |
|---------|----------------|
| Hazardous Materials Business Plans (HMBP) | Chemical storage/handling |
| Underground Storage Tanks (UST) | Fuel/chemical tanks |
| Aboveground Petroleum Storage (APSA) | Above-ground fuel tanks |
| Hazardous Waste Generator | Waste generation/disposal |
| CalARP | Accidental release prevention |
| Tiered Permitting | Hazardous waste treatment |

**Find Your CUPA:** Contact local Environmental Health Department

### Hazardous Materials Business Plan (HMBP)

**Required if you store:**
- ≥500 lbs solid hazardous material
- ≥55 gallons liquid hazardous material
- ≥200 cubic feet compressed gas

**Common Manufacturing Hazmat:**
- Oils (new and used)
- Fuels (gasoline, diesel, propane)
- Solvents
- Antifreeze
- Compressed gases

**Deadline:** March 1 annually via CERS (California Environmental Reporting System)

**URL:** https://cers.calepa.ca.gov

### Underground Storage Tank (UST) Permits

**2025 CRITICAL DEADLINE:**
- Single-walled USTs must be closed by December 31, 2025
- Closure proposals due December 2, 2025
- Non-compliance = cannot operate

**Requirements:**
- Operating permit from CUPA
- Financial responsibility (insurance/guarantee)
- Monitoring and leak detection
- Periodic testing

**Fees:** ~$1,000+ per tank annually

### Aboveground Petroleum Storage (APSA)

**Required if storing >1,320 gallons petroleum aboveground**

**Requirements:**
- SPCC Plan (Spill Prevention Control and Countermeasure)
- Secondary containment
- Report to CUPA via CERS

**Fees:** $800-$2,000+ based on volume

### CalARP (California Accidental Release Prevention)

**Required if handling regulated substances above thresholds**

**Program Levels:**

| Level | Complexity | Example Facilities |
|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Level 1 | Low risk processes | Small operations |
| Level 2 | Moderate complexity | Medium manufacturers |
| Level 3 | Complex processes | Chemical plants |
| Level 4 | Refineries only | Petroleum refineries |

**Requirements:**
- Risk Management Plan (RMP)
- Process hazard analysis
- Emergency response coordination

**Fees:** ~$2,600+ annually

### Hazardous Waste Generator Requirements

| Generator Type | Threshold | Requirements |
|----------------|-----------|--------------|
| CESQG | <55 gal/month | Minimal reporting |
| SQG | 55-1,000 kg/month | HMBP, basic compliance |
| LQG | >1,000 kg/month | Full compliance, tiered permitting |

**All Generators Must:**
- Maintain waste records
- Use registered hazardous waste transporters
- Complete hazardous waste manifests
- Report to CUPA

## Cal/OSHA Requirements

### Injury and Illness Prevention Program (IIPP)

**Required for ALL California employers**

**Must Include:**
1. Responsibility assignments
2. Compliance procedures
3. Hazard communication
4. Hazard assessment procedures
5. Accident investigation protocols
6. Hazard correction procedures
7. Training requirements
8. Recordkeeping

**URL:** https://www.dir.ca.gov/dosh/dosh_publications/IIPP-Model-high-hazard.html

### Hazard Communication

**Requirements:**
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for all hazardous chemicals
- Container labels with hazard info
- Chemical inventory
- Employee training

### Machine Guarding

**Requirements:**
- Guard rotating/moving parts
- Power shut-off within reach
- Lockout capability for maintenance
- Splashguards for coolant systems
- Regular safety inspections

### Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

**Required for service/maintenance:**
- Written LOTO procedures
- Personal locks and tags
- Identify all energy sources
- Employee training
- Testing before work begins

### 2025 Poster Update

**Effective July 2025:**
- Updated Cal/OSHA Safety and Health Protection poster required
- Must display in visible location
- Available at Cal/OSHA website

## Fire Department Permits

### Hazardous Materials Storage

**Permit Required for:**
- Flammable liquids
- Oxidizers
- Compressed gases
- Other regulated materials

**Requirements:**
- Storage limits
- Container specifications
- Ventilation
- Separation distances

### High-Piled Storage (2025 Update)

**NEW 2025 Requirements:**
- Stricter sprinkler requirements
- Fire barrier specifications
- Emergency access aisles
- Regular inspections

**Penalties for non-compliance are significant**

| Operation | Permit Needed |
|-----------|---------------|

| Powder coating | Air + Fire |

## Specialized Manufacturing Categories

### Food Manufacturing

**California:**
- Processed Food Registration (PFR) from CDPH
- URL: https://www.cdph.ca.gov/Programs/CEH/DFDCS/Pages/FDBPrograms/FoodSafetyProgram/ProcessedFoodRegistration.aspx

**Federal:**
- FDA Food Facility Registration
- Current Good Manufacturing Practices compliance
- Registration renewal: every even year

### Pharmaceutical/Medical Devices

**Medical Device Manufacturing:**
- License from CDPH Food and Drug Branch
- FDA registration required
- ISO 13485:2003 certification or FDA inspection within 2 years
- Fee: ~$200/year

**Drug Manufacturing:**
- CDPH license
- Fingerprint/background check
- Fee: $200-300/year

### Chemical Manufacturing

**Additional Requirements:**
- Title V air permit (likely)
- CalARP Risk Management Plan
- Process Safety Management compliance
- Comprehensive environmental permits

### Metal Fabrication/Machining

**Common Permits:**
- Air permits for welding, coating
- Hazmat for coolants, solvents
- Fire permits for hot work

### Plastics/Composites

**Air Quality:**
- VOC emissions permits
- BACT requirements if >10 lbs/day ROC
- Health risk assessment may be required

## Zoning Requirements

### Industrial Zone Types

| Zone | Description | Uses |
|------|-------------|------|
| M-1 | Light Manufacturing | Warehousing, assembly, R&D |
| M-2 | Heavy Manufacturing | Heavy industry, processing |
| CM | Commercial Manufacturing | Mixed commercial/light industrial |

### Before Leasing Space

1. Verify zoning permits manufacturing use
2. Check for CUP (Conditional Use Permit) requirements
3. Review building permits for proposed use
4. Confirm utility capacity (power, water, sewer)

### Building Permits

**Required for:**
- New construction
- Tenant improvements
- Equipment installation
- Change of use

**Timeline:** 1-3 months typical

**AB 2234:** Standardized 30-60 day processing timelines for housing projects (may apply to some manufacturing)

## 2024-2025 Regulatory Changes

### Climate Reporting (SB 253/261)

**Effective 2026:**
- Companies >$1B revenue must report greenhouse gas emissions
- Scope 1 and 2 reporting required
- Climate risk disclosure

### Extended Producer Responsibility (SB 54)

**Plastic Packaging:**
- Must be recyclable/compostable by 2032
- EPS food service ware prohibited January 1, 2025
- Producer responsibility organization participation required

### Underground Storage Tank Deadline

**December 31, 2025:**
- All single-walled USTs must be closed
- Non-compliance = cannot operate

### Fire Code Updates

**2025 California Fire Code:**
- New high-piled storage requirements
- Enhanced sprinkler standards
- Stricter inspection requirements

## Timeline Overview

### Typical Manufacturing Startup Timeline

```
Month 1-2: Business Formation
├── Form LLC/Corp with SOS (1-2 weeks)
├── Obtain EIN (immediate)
├── Register with CDTFA (same day)
└── Apply for local business license (1-3 weeks)

Month 2-3: Environmental Planning
├── Identify required air permits
├── Contact local AQMD
├── Register with CUPA
├── Apply for IGP coverage

Month 3-6: Permit Processing
├── Air permit processing (7 days - 6+ months)
├── CUPA permit (1-2 months)
├── Building permits (1-3 months)
├── Fire permits (2-4 weeks)

Month 4-6+: Final Approvals
├── Complete construction
├── Final inspections
├── Permit to Operate issued
└── Begin operations
```

### Longest Lead-Time Items

1. **Air Quality Permits** (Major Source) - 3-6+ months
2. **Building Permits** - 1-3 months
3. **CalARP Review** - 2-3 months
4. **Local CUP** (if required) - 2-4 months

**Recommendation:** Start permitting 6-9 months before planned opening.
