## License Types

### On-Sale Licenses (Consumption On Premises)

#### Beer & Wine Licenses

| Type | Name | Description | Minors | Food Required |
|------|------|-------------|--------|---------------|
| 41 | On-Sale Beer & Wine (Eating Place) | Beer/wine for restaurants | Allowed | Yes |
| 42 | On-Sale Beer & Wine (Public Premises) | Beer/wine for bars | NOT Allowed | No |
| 61 | On-Sale Beer (Public Premises) | Beer only | NOT Allowed | No |

**Type 41 Details:**
- Must operate as bona fide eating place
- Requires suitable kitchen facilities
- Must make actual and substantial meal sales
- Beer and wine for on-premises OR off-premises (sealed containers)
- Distilled spirits prohibited (except brandy/rum/liqueurs for cooking)

**Type 42 Details:**
- For bars, taverns, nightclubs
- No food service required
- Minors prohibited from entering
- Must post "No Person Under 21 Allowed" signs

#### General Licenses (Beer, Wine & Spirits)

| Type | Name | Description | Minors | Food Required |
|------|------|-------------|--------|---------------|
| 47 | On-Sale General (Eating Place) | Full bar for restaurants | Allowed | Yes |
| 48 | On-Sale General (Public Premises) | Full bar for bars/clubs | NOT Allowed | No |
| 57 | Special On-Sale General | Members and guests only | Allowed | No |

**Type 47 Details:**
- Most common restaurant liquor license
- Beer, wine, and distilled spirits
- Off-sale beer and wine permitted (sealed containers)
- Must operate as bona fide eating place
- **Highly restricted** - issued through priority drawing

**Type 48 Details:**
- For bars, nightclubs, taverns
- No food service required
- Minors prohibited
- Subject to strict local scrutiny

#### Club Licenses

| Type | Name | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| 51 | Club | Private club (members/guests only) |
| 52 | Veterans' Club | Veterans' organizations only |

#### Specialty On-Sale Licenses

| Type | Name | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| 75 | Brewpub-Restaurant | Restaurant + on-site beer brewing |
| 78 | Wine, Food and Art Culture Museum | Museum with wine service |
| 90 | Music Venue | Concert halls, performance venues |

**Type 90 (Music Venue) Details:**
- NEW license type for entertainment venues
- Sales only 2 hours before to 1 hour after live performances
- Must have professional audio/lighting equipment
- Must employ sound engineers, bookers, security

### Off-Sale Licenses (Package Sales)

| Type | Name | Description | Minors |
|------|------|-------------|--------|
| 20 | Off-Sale Beer & Wine | Beer/wine retail (convenience stores, grocery) | Allowed |
| 21 | Off-Sale General | Full liquor store | Allowed |
| 85 | Limited Off-Sale Wine | Wine by mail/internet only (no storefront) | N/A |

**Type 20 vs Type 21:**
- Type 20: Beer and wine only, lower application fee ($410)
- Type 21: All alcoholic beverages, higher application fee ($19,315)

### Manufacturing & Wholesale Licenses

| Type | Name | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| 01 | Beer Manufacturer | Brewery (>60,000 barrels/year) |
| 23 | Small Beer Manufacturer | Microbrewery (<60,000 barrels/year) |
| 02 | Winegrower | Winery |
| 04 | Distilled Spirits Manufacturer | Large distillery |
| 74 | Craft Distiller | Small distillery (<150,000 gallons/year) |

**Type 74 (Craft Distiller) Details:**
- Up to 150,000 gallons/year production
- May sell up to 4.5 liters/customer/day at premises (increased from 2.25L in 2026)
- May operate on-premises tasting room
- NEW: Direct shipper permit available (Type 94, effective 2026)

### Temporary & Special Event Licenses

| Type | Name | Description | Fee |
|------|------|-------------|-----|
| 31 | Special Daily (Auction) | Nonprofit fundraising auctions | $100 |
| 34 | Daily Beer & Wine | Nonprofit beer/wine events | $50 |
| 37 | Daily General | Nonprofit full bar events | $75 |
| 58 | Caterer's Permit | Off-site catering authorization | $100-$1,000/day |

**Caterer's Permit (Type 58):**
- Not standalone - requires base on-sale license
- Up to 36 events per year per location
- Fees based on attendance:
  - 0-999 attendees: $100/day
  - 1,000-4,999: $325/day
  - 5,000+: $1,000/day

## Application Process

### Step 1: Pre-Application Preparation

**Verify Zoning FIRST:**
1. Contact city/county planning department
2. Confirm alcohol sales permitted at location
3. Determine if Conditional Use Permit (CUP) required
4. Some jurisdictions require CUP BEFORE ABC application

**Federal Requirements:**
- Contact TTB (Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau)
- Determine if federal permit required
- Must match same persons/address as ABC license

**State Tax Registration:**
- Obtain Seller's Permit from CDTFA
- Required before any alcohol sales

### Step 2: Initial Consultation

**Required:** Personal visit to ABC district office
- Cannot be done by mail
- Bring all owners/partners
- Receive forms and fee information
- Get advice on applicable laws

### Step 3: Application Filing

**Required Documents:**

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Application forms | Varies by license type |
| Personal history affidavits | Background check |
| Citizenship verification | Legal status |
| Proof of premises interest | Lease or deed |
| Floor plan/diagrams | Premises layout |
| Criminal history disclosure | If applicable |

**Business Information Required:**
- Planned operating hours
- Nature of operation
- Financial information
- All bank accounts for business
- Source of funds
- Landlord information

**Escrow Requirement (Transfers):**
- Required when purchasing existing license
- Full purchase price must be in escrow
- Escrow holder must be neutral third party

### Step 4: Public Notice Period (30 Days)

**Posted Notice:**
- White/yellow Form ABC-207
- Conspicuous location visible from exterior
- Must remain posted 30 continuous days

**Mailed Notices:**
- ABC mails to: Sheriff, Police Chief, DA, City Council/Board of Supervisors
- Applicant may need to mail to residents within 500 feet

**Newspaper Publication:**
- Required for some license types
- Check with ABC district office

### Step 5: Investigation (45-50 Days)

**ABC Investigates:**
- Personal history and employment
- Financial stability
- Criminal history
- Premises suitability
- Distance to schools, churches, hospitals
- Local law enforcement consultation
- Zoning compliance

### Step 6: Protest Period

**Who Can Protest:**
- Any person within 30 days of notice posting

**Valid Protest Grounds:**
- Would create public nuisance
- Would cause/add to crime
- Conflicts with zoning
- Undue concentration of licenses
- Location in high-crime area
- Would interfere with quiet enjoyment of nearby residents

**If Protested:**
- ABC investigates protest claims
- May require administrative hearing
- Can add 30-90+ days to timeline

### Timeline Summary

| Application Type | Typical Timeline |
|-----------------|------------------|
| Person-to-Person Transfer (same premises) | 75 days |
| Original License (new premises) | 90 days |
| With Protests/Hearings | 90-180+ days |
| With Local CUP Required | Add 3-6 months |

## Operating Restrictions

### Hours of Sale

**Statewide Rule:**
- **Prohibited:** 2:00 AM to 6:00 AM (no sales, delivery, or consumption)
- Applies to ALL on-sale and off-sale licenses
- Local jurisdictions may impose earlier closing times

**Delivery Restrictions:**
- Manufacturer/wholesaler deliveries: 3:00 AM to 8:00 PM weekdays
- No deliveries on Sundays (with exceptions)

### Minor Access Rules

**Premises Where Minors PROHIBITED:**
- Type 42 (On-Sale Beer & Wine, Public Premises)
- Type 48 (On-Sale General, Public Premises)
- Type 61 (On-Sale Beer, Public Premises)

**Required Signage:**
- "No Person Under 21 Allowed"
- Minimum 7" x 11" size
- 1" lettering minimum
- Posted at entrances and interior

**Premises Where Minors ALLOWED:**
- Type 41 (Eating Place, Beer & Wine)
- Type 47 (Eating Place, General)
- Type 75 (Brewpub-Restaurant)
- Type 51/52 (Clubs)
- All Off-Sale licenses

### Minor Employment

| Restriction | Rule |
|-------------|------|
| Under 21 | Cannot prepare/serve alcohol in areas designed for alcohol service |
| Ages 18-21 | MAY serve in food service areas (incidental to duties) |
| Under 18 (off-sale) | Must be supervised by person 21+ |
| Ages 18-21 | MAY work as musicians (limited conditions) |

### Food Requirements

**Bona Fide Eating Place (Types 41, 47, 75):**
- Must have suitable kitchen facilities
- Must make actual and substantial meal sales
- ABC defines what constitutes a "meal"
- Cannot be just bar snacks

### Entertainment

**Type 90 (Music Venue):**
- Alcohol only during performances
- 2 hours before to 1 hour after show
- Must have professional equipment
- May host limited private events

## Premises Requirements

### Distance Restrictions

| Sensitive Use | Restriction |
|---------------|-------------|
| Residences | No license within 100 feet unless won't interfere with quiet enjoyment |
| Schools, churches, hospitals | No license in "immediate vicinity" unless won't interfere |
| Children's playgrounds | Case-by-case evaluation |

### Undue Concentration

If area has:
- More licenses than county average, OR
- Crime rate 20%+ higher than surrounding area

Then: Must prove "public convenience or necessity"

### Conditional Use Permits (CUP)

Many jurisdictions require CUP before ABC will process:
- Check with city planning
- CUP process may take 3-6 months
- Some CUPs have expiration dates
- Premises closure >15 days may trigger new CUP requirement

## License Transfers

### Person-to-Person (Ownership Change)

**Same Premises:**
- Faster process (~75 days)
- No public notice required
- No protest period
- Both parties must appear at ABC
- Escrow required

**Different Premises:**
- Full application process
- Public notice required
- Protest period applies
- Same timeline as new license

### Premises-to-Premises (Relocation)

- Moving existing license to new location
- Full investigation of new premises
- Public notice required
- Same as new application timeline

### Temporary Permits

**During Transfer:**
- Available for person-to-person at same premises
- Valid up to 4 months (extendable to 8)
- Allows operations while transfer processes
- Fee: $100

## Responsible Beverage Service (RBS)

### Requirement (Effective July 1, 2022)

**Who Must Be Certified:**
- All servers at on-premises locations
- All managers at on-premises locations

**Timeline:**
- Must certify within 60 days of first employment
- Must register in RBS Portal
- Must complete ABC-approved training
- Must pass ABC certification exam within 30 days of training

**Certification Valid:** 3 years (then recertify)

### Training Topics

- Social impact of alcohol
- Effects on body and behavior
- Applicable laws and regulations
- Prevention and intervention techniques
- Management policies

**Languages Available:** English, Chinese, Korean, Spanish, Vietnamese

## Enforcement & Compliance

### ABC Inspections

**IMPACT Team Inspections:**
- Random selection of licensed outlets
- Check for violations
- 20-day follow-up if issues found
- Uncorrected violations reported to district office

**What They Check:**
- Posted signs
- Food service (eating places)
- Illegal activities
- Loitering, litter, graffiti
- Weapons
- Overall compliance

### Penalties

**Administrative Discipline Range:**

| Violation | Typical Penalty |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Operating condition violations | 15-day suspension (5 stayed) |
| Sale to minor | 30-day suspension |
| Recurring violations | License revocation |
| Narcotics on premises | License revocation |
| Hidden/undisclosed ownership | License revocation |

**Offer in Compromise (OIC):**
- Alternative to suspension (≤15 days)
- Financial settlement option
- Avoids operational disruption

### Minor Decoy Operations

- Law enforcement uses decoys under age 20
- Tests compliance with age verification
- NOT entrapment (per CA Supreme Court)
- Violations result in administrative action

## Special Situations

### Cannabis Proximity

**STRICT SEPARATION:**
- Cannot sell alcohol AND cannabis at same premises
- Cannot be located so customers pass through one to reach other
- ABC and DCC enforce separation

### Adding Bar to Restaurant

**Options:**
- Apply for Type 47 (On-Sale General, Eating Place)
- Highly competitive - often requires priority drawing
- Alternative: Type 41 (beer and wine only) - easier to obtain

### Brewery/Winery/Distillery Tasting Rooms

| License | On-Premises Sales | Tasting Room |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|
| Type 01 (Beer Manufacturer) | Yes | Yes (with food/RBS) |
| Type 23 (Small Beer Manufacturer) | Yes | Yes |
| Type 02 (Winegrower) | Yes | Yes |
| Type 74 (Craft Distiller) | Up to 4.5L/customer/day | Yes (with food) |
